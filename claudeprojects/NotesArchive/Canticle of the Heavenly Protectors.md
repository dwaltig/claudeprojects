# Canticle of the Heavenly Protectors  
  
*“Canticle of the Heavenly Protectors”* is a solemn Gregorian chant inspired by the vows of Vaisravana and Virudhaka, two of the Four Heavenly Kings, as described in the Lotus Sutra. This sacred piece captures their pledge to protect those who uphold and share the teachings of the Dharma. The chant invokes powerful spells, offering strength, courage, and a shield against harm, creating an atmosphere of divine protection and unwavering support.  
  
**Purport**  
  
This canticle honors the protective vows of the Heavenly Kings who safeguard practitioners of the Lotus Sutra. Each chant symbolizes their compassionate watchfulness, ensuring that no harm befalls those who dedicate themselves to the teachings. The melody resonates with their guardianship, evoking courage, resilience, and faith in the power of the sacred. May this chant inspire a sense of inner peace and strength, as you feel the presence of these celestial protectors surrounding you.

**Antiphon:**  
*Guardians of the sacred word,*  
*From cardinal realms, their vows are heard.*  
  
**Verses (Vaisravana):**  
In atte and natte, courage renews,  
Vanatte anade, peace flows through.  
Nādi to kunadi, spirit arise,  
With svāhā, find grace under the skies.  
  
**Chorus:**  
*By their spells, no harm shall fall,*  
*Heavenly Kings defend them all.*  
  
**Verses (Virudhaka):**  
In agane and gane, strength takes form,  
Gauri gandhari, steadfast in the storm.  
Kandali matangi, rise above the fray,  
Pukkasi sankule, light leads the way.  
In vrusali, courage unfurled,  
With svāhā, shine brightly in the world.

Expedient Methods and the Ten Suchnesses of the Ultimate Law (Paragraphs 1-22) Shakyamuni Buddha presents the Ten Suchnesses, which are essential for understanding the Buddha-Law, as they exemplify the interdependence of all phenomena and the nature of existence – the very foundation of Buddhist teachings. These Suchnesses reveal the inner workings of the Law of Cause and Effect.  
The ten suchnesses, as described in the Lotus Sutra, constitute a profound philosophical explanation in Buddhism, particularly within the T'ien T'ai and Nichiren traditions. These ten aspects represent the ultimate reality of all phenomena, providing a comprehensive and inclusive vision of existence in its entirety.  
Below are brief explanations of each of the ten suchnesses:  
  
1. **Appearance** (色) - This refers to the physical or observable attributes of an object, event, or being. It's the perceptible aspects or what you can see, touch, smell, taste, or hear.  
  
2. **Nature** (性) - This is the inner reality or quality of phenomena, which cannot be perceived directly but is understood through inference. It corresponds to the underlying properties or characteristics.  
  
3. **Entity** (体) - This represents the existence or being of all phenomena in their totality, including both their visible and invisible aspects. It is the synthesis of appearance and nature.  
  
4. **Power** (力) - This refers to the potential of a phenomenon to take effect. It's the capacity or capability to perform a certain action or to have a certain influence.  
  
5. **Influence** (作) - This denotes the activity or function that a phenomenon exhibits when its inherent power is manifested. It's the observable impact or the result of the power being actualized.  
  
6. **Internal cause** (因) - This represents the inherent cause within a phenomenon, which, when combined with an external condition, produces an effect. It's like a seed's potential to become a tree given the right conditions.  
  
7. **Relation** (縁) - This refers to the external conditions that, when encountered by an internal cause, enable it to manifest an effect. This could be like the water and sunlight a seed needs to grow.  
  
8. **Latent effect** (果) - This represents the effect or result that lies dormant within a phenomenon as a consequence of the internal cause and the relation. It’s like the tree within the seed, not yet visible but destined to emerge.  
  
9. **Manifest effect** (報) - This is the observable outcome or result that appears when the latent effect is actualized. It's the sprouting of the seed and the growth of the tree that becomes visible over time.  
  
10. **Consistency from beginning to end** (本末究竟等) - This represents the consistency and continuity of the other nine suchnesses throughout the three phases of existence (past, present, and future). It emphasizes the ultimate unity and interconnection of all phenomena.  
  
Each suchness implies the other nine, and they all mutually permeate each other. This is known as the principle of "one suchness implies all suchnesses." Through this principle, it is said that one can fully comprehend the reality of all phenomena.

# Hymn of the Guardian Giantesses  
  
*“Hymn of the Guardian Giantesses”* is a powerful Gregorian chant inspired by the vows of protection made by a group of compassionate giantesses in the Lotus Sutra. Accompanied by their children and entourage, they offer sacred spells to shield those who uphold the Dharma from harm, whether in waking life or in dreams. This chant embodies their unbreakable promise, blending sacred words with meditative tones to create an aura of safety and spiritual strength.  
  
**Purport**  
  
This hymn pays homage to the collective vow of the guardian giantesses, who pledge to protect the preachers of the Lotus Sutra from all forms of harm, including malevolent spirits and dark dreams. Their protective spells, approved by countless Buddhas, form an unyielding shield of compassion and strength. Through this chant, listeners can connect with their promise and draw upon the sacred energy of these protectors, fostering resilience, peace, and a deep sense of spiritual refuge.

**Antiphon:**  
*Through sacred spells, they vow to keep,*  
*The preachers safe, in waking and sleep.*  
  
**Verses:**  
In iti me, grounding strong and true,  
With nime, no shadow shall pass through.  
In ruhe, sacred rest is found,  
And stuhe, blessings shall abound.  
  
**Chorus:**  
*By their words, no harm shall come,*  
*A shield of light for everyone.*  
  
With svaha, let it be decreed,  
A circle of protection in every need.  
No goblin, ghost, or devil’s might,  
Shall breach this shield of holy light.

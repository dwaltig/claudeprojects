## The Source for "Deception for Liberation" (African-American Tradition)  
  
The idea that figures like Moses and Harriet Tubman used "holy deception" or "trickster" tactics (often called "Signifying") to liberate their people is a core tenet of **Black Liberation Theology** and **Blues/Spirituals scholarship**.  
**Endnote:** James H. Cone, *The Spirituals and the Blues: An Interpretation* (New York: Seabury Press, 1972), 32.  
  
  
  
While the specific phrasing in our paper ("I believe in the Bible and I sing the Bible...") appears to be a summary or paraphrase of her sentiments rather than a verbatim passage found in digital archives of the book, the themes of "songs of my people" and the distinction between Blues (despair) and Gospel (hope) are the central thesis of her memoir.

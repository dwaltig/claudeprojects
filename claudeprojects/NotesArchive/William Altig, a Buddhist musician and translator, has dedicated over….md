*William Altig, a Buddhist musician and translator, has dedicated over a decade to the Lotus Sutra. With over 20 years of Buddhist study and practice, he has recently translated the text into blues language while simultaneously composing it as a ‘Buddhist Blues Opera.’ This unique blend of music and text creates original compositions based on the parables and teachings of the Lotus Sutra.*  
  
*Altig’s work has garnered a significant following, reaching over 2,500+ listeners across various streaming platforms. SoundCloud has been particularly popular, with over 1,400+ followers and 300+ songs. He actively engages with Buddhist practitioners and spiritual music communities on social media. Altig’s musical output, comprising over 3,000 songs, explores Buddhist Dharma through blues, gospel, and vernacular spiritual expression.*  
  
*This translation marks the culmination of years spent in deep dialogue with the ancient text. Altig aims to make its radical message of universal enlightenment resonate in contemporary American language. Currently residing in Houston, TX, he is available for readings, performances, and teachings of this work.*  
  
*All three volumes of the trilogy—The Lotus Sutra in Blues, The Immeasurable Meanings Sutra in Blues, and a comprehensive practice guide based on Samantabhadra’s teachings—are  complete and ready for publication. This represents over a decade of dedicated work translating the Mahayana Buddhist canon into contemporary vernacular blues language, a work that Altig has been sharing with 2,500+ listeners across streaming platforms.*

## Key Insights for Article 1

### Voice Concentration
- **Top 3 voices** account for 75.7% of all tags:
  - Charon (39.7%)
  - Orus (24.8%)
  - Iapetus (11.3%)
- This concentration reflects **sutra structure**: Narrator + Chief Disciple + Buddha dialogue dominates

### Voice Diversity
- **15 distinct voices** used from 26 available Gemini voices
- **Sufficient variety** for character differentiation without overwhelming complexity
- **Replicable**: Most sutras have similar character distribution (narrator-heavy, disciple-heavy, Buddha teaching)

### Character-to-Voice Mapping Efficiency
- **532 voice tags** across ~200,000 words = average 1 tag per 376 words
- **Sufficient granularity** for character differentiation in TTS rendering
- **Not excessive**: Avoids listener fatigue from too-frequent voice changes

### Buddha Voice Variation
- **3 Buddha voices** (Iapetus, Rasalgethi, Triton) reflect **teaching mode variation**:
  - Iapetus: Authoritative doctrinal teaching (most frequent)
  - Rasalgethi: Gentle compassionate instruction
  - Triton: Cosmic revelation (rare, high-impact moments like Chapter 16)
- **Interpretive decision**: Voice as dharma pedagogy

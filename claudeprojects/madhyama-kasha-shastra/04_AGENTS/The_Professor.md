## Translation Approach

### Core Principles
1. **Philological Precision**: Accurate Sanskrit → English with etymological awareness
2. **Dialectical Structure**: Preserve the pūrvapakṣa-uttarapakṣa format
3. **Scholarly Apparatus**: Extensive footnotes with:
   - Sanskrit terms in IAST and Devanāgarī
   - Philosophical commentary on Mādhyamaka arguments
   - Cross-references to other texts (Vigrahavyāvartanī, Ratnāvalī, etc.)
   - Comparison with Tibetan translations
4. **Opponent Schools**: Identify and explain positions being refuted (Sāṃkhya, Nyāya, Sarvāstivāda, etc.)

### Footnote Categories
- **[Sanskrit]**: Original terms, etymology, compounds (samāsa)
- **[Dialectical]**: Argument structure, pūrvapakṣa (opponent's view)
- **[Philosophical]**: Mādhyamaka doctrine, śūnyatā, pratītyasamutpāda
- **[Cross-reference]**: Links to other kārikās, other Nāgārjuna texts
- **[Historical]**: Candrakīrti's commentary, Buddhapālita, Bhāviveka
- **[Modern]**: Contemporary philosophy parallels (Wittgenstein, deconstruction)

## Key Terminology
- **svabhāva** (स्वभाव): Intrinsic nature, self-nature, own-being
- **śūnyatā** (शून्यता): Emptiness, absence of svabhāva
- **pratītyasamutpāda** (प्रतीत्यसमुत्पाद): Dependent origination
- **pratyaya** (प्रत्यय): Condition, causal condition
- **catuṣkoṭi** (चतुष्कोटि): Tetralemma (four-cornered negation)
- **prasaṅga** (प्रसङ्ग): Consequence, reductio ad absurdum
- **pūrvapakṣa** (पूर्वपक्ष): Opponent's position
- **uttarapakṣa** (उत्तरपक्ष): Response/refutation

## Special Considerations for Madhyamakashāstra

- **Dense Argumentation**: The kārikās are highly compressed—single verses contain entire philosophical arguments
- **Commentary Critical**: The Prasannapadā is essential for understanding—Nāgārjuna's verses alone are often cryptic
- **Multiple Opponents**: Sāṃkhya (svataḥ), Naiyāyika (parataḥ), Jaina (svataḥ-parataḥ), Cārvāka (ahetukaḥ)
- **Prasaṅgika Method**: Candrakīrti's reductio arguments must be carefully traced
- **Self-Referential**: The text refutes views by showing internal contradictions, not by asserting positive doctrines

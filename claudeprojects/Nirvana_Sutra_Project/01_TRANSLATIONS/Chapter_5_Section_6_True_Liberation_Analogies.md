## The Thirty-Plus Analogies for True Liberation

### 1. **Liberation is Freedom from Bondage / Not Born from Union**

真解脫者，名曰遠離一切繫縛。若真解脫離諸繫縛，則無有生，亦無和合。譬如父母和合生子，真解脫者則不如是

"True liberation is called being far removed from all bondage. If true liberation is separated from all bondage, then there is no birth and no union. Like father and mother uniting to give birth to a child—true liberation is not like this."

### 2. **Liberation is Pure Like Ghee**

譬如醍醐其性清淨。如來亦爾，非因父母和合而生，其性清淨

"Like ghee (*dadhighṛta*), its nature is pure. The Tathāgata is also thus—not born from the union of father and mother, his nature is pure."

### 3. **Liberation is Not Like Seeds Sprouting**

譬如春月下諸種子，得煖氣已尋便出生；真解脫者則不如是

"Like in spring month planting various seeds—having received warm energy, they immediately sprout. True liberation is not like this."

### 4. **Liberation is Emptiness/Void (虛無)**

又解脫者名曰虛無，虛無即是解脫，解脫即是如來

"Also, liberation is called emptiness/void. Emptiness is precisely liberation; liberation is precisely the Tathāgata."

### 5. **Liberation is Unconditioned, Not Like Constructed Things**

又解脫者即無為法，譬如陶師，作已還破，解脫不爾

"Also, liberation is unconditioned dharma. Like a potter—having made [a pot], it breaks again. Liberation is not like this."

### 6. **Liberation is Unborn and Undying**

真解脫者不生不滅，是故解脫即是如來。如來亦爾，不生不滅、不老不死

"True liberation is unborn and undying. Therefore liberation is the Tathāgata. The Tathāgata is also thus—unborn, undying, not aging, not dying."

### 7. **Liberation is Without Disease**

又解脫者名曰無病，所謂病者，四百四病及餘外來侵損身者，是處無故，故名解脫

"Also, liberation is called without disease. What is called disease—the 404 diseases and other external [afflictions] that invade and damage the body—in this place there are none, therefore it is called liberation."

### 8. **Liberation is Immortal Nectar (甘露)**

死者名曰身壞命終；是處無死，即是甘露，是甘露者即真解脫

"Death is called body breaking, life ending. In this place there is no death—this is nectar (*amṛta*). This nectar is precisely true liberation."

### 9. **Liberation is a Diamond Body**

是金剛身，云何無常？是故如來不名命終

"This is a diamond/vajra body—how can it be impermanent? Therefore the Tathāgata is not called 'life-ending.'"

### 10. **Liberation is Pure Like a Lotus**

如來清淨，無有垢穢，如來之身非胎所污，如分陀利，本性清淨

"The Tathāgata is pure, without defilement. The Tathāgata's body is not polluted by the womb, like the *puṇḍarīka* [white lotus], originally pure in nature."

### 11. **Liberation is Without Afflictions**

又解脫者，諸漏瘡疣永無遺餘，如來亦爾，無有一切諸漏瘡疣

"Also, liberation—all outflows, sores, and warts are permanently without remainder. The Tathāgata is also thus, without all outflows, sores, and warts."

### 12. **Liberation is Without Conflict**

又解脫者無有鬪諍，譬如飢人，見他飲食，生貪奪想，解脫不爾

"Also, liberation is without conflict. Like a hungry person seeing others' food and drink, generating thoughts of greed and seizure—liberation is not like this."

### 13. **Liberation is True Peace (Not Like Maheśvara)**

又解脫者名曰安靜，凡夫人言，夫安靜者謂摩醯首羅，如是之言即是虛妄。真安靜者畢竟解脫

"Also, liberation is called peace. Ordinary people say that peace means Maheśvara [Śiva]—such words are false. True peace is complete liberation."

### 14. **Liberation is Security**

又解脫者名曰安隱，如多賊處不名安隱，清夷之處乃名安隱

"Also, liberation is called security. Like a place with many bandits is not called secure; a clear, level place is called secure."

### 15. **Liberation is Without Equal**

又解脫者，無有等侶...無等侶者謂轉輪聖王

"Also, liberation has no equal... One without equal is called a wheel-turning sage king."

### 16. **Liberation is Without Sorrow**

又解脫者名無憂愁，有憂愁者譬如國王畏難強隣，而生憂愁

"Also, liberation is called without sorrow. One with sorrow is like a king fearing a powerful neighboring [country] and generating sorrow."

### 17. **Liberation is Without Joy or Sorrow**

又解脫者，名無憂喜，譬如女人，只有一子，從役遠行，卒得凶問，聞之愁苦，後復聞活，便生歡喜

"Also, liberation is called without sorrow or joy. Like a woman with only one son—[he] goes on a distant campaign, suddenly [she] receives bad news, hearing it [she feels] sorrow and suffering. Later [she] hears [he is] alive and generates joy."

### 18. **Liberation is Without Dust**

又解脫者無有塵垢，譬如春月，日沒之後，風起塵霧

"Also, liberation is without dust and dirt. Like in spring month after sunset, wind arises [stirring up] dust and fog."

### 19. **Liberation is Like a Flawless Jewel**

譬如聖王髻中明珠，無有垢穢。夫解脫性亦復如是

"Like a sage king's bright jewel in his topknot, without defilement. The nature of liberation is also thus."

### 20. **Liberation is Like Pure Gold**

如真金性，不雜沙石，乃名真寶

"Like the nature of true gold, not mixed with sand and stones—this is called true treasure."

### 21. **Liberation is Like an Unbreakable Diamond Vase**

譬如瓦瓶，破而聲㽄，金剛寶瓶，則不如是

"Like a clay pot—when broken [it makes a] cracking sound. A diamond treasure vase is not like this."

### 22. **Liberation is Not Like Sesame Seeds Popping**

其聲㽄者，如蜱麻子，盛熱之時，置之日曝，出聲震爆

"The cracking sound is like sesame seeds—in intense heat, placed in the sun, [they] make an explosive popping sound."

### 23. **Liberation is Like a Wealthy Person Without Debt**

如貧窮人負他物故，為他所繫...夫解脫者亦復如是，多有無量法財珍寶，勢力自在，無所負也

"Like a poor person owing others' things, being bound by others... Liberation is also thus—having abundant immeasurable Dharma-wealth treasures, power and freedom, owing nothing."

### 24. **Liberation is Without Discomfort**

又解脫者名無逼切，如春涉熱，夏日食甜，冬日冷觸。真解脫中無有如是不適意事

"Also, liberation is called without oppression. Like in spring experiencing heat, in summer eating sweets, in winter cold touch—in true liberation there are no such uncomfortable things."

## Key Terms Breakdown

### The Professor's Philological Analysis

**1. 醍醐 (tíhú) - Ghee/Clarified Butter**

- **Sanskrit:** *dadhighṛta* or *sarpir-maṇḍa* (the finest form of clarified butter)
- **Literal meaning:** Transliteration of Sanskrit
- **Doctrinal significance:** In the famous "Five Flavors" metaphor, ghee represents the highest teaching (the Lotus and Nirvana Sutras)

**2. 虛無 (xūwú) - Emptiness/Void**

- **Sanskrit:** *śūnyatā* (emptiness)
- **Literal meaning:** 虛 (empty, void) + 無 (without, non-being)
- **Doctrinal significance:** This is a key Mahāyāna concept—liberation is "empty" not in the sense of non-existence but in being free from all conceptual elaborations

**3. 金剛身 (jīngāng shēn) - Diamond/Vajra Body**

- **Sanskrit:** *vajra-kāya* (diamond body)
- **Literal meaning:** 金剛 (diamond/vajra) + 身 (body)
- **Doctrinal significance:** The Tathāgata's body is indestructible like a diamond—this is the Dharma-body, not the physical body

## Scholarly Notes

**The Rhetorical Strategy:**

This passage employs a distinctive rhetorical technique: overwhelming the reader with analogies. By presenting 30+ different metaphors in rapid succession, the text creates a cumulative effect—no single analogy can capture the nature of liberation, but together they point toward something beyond conceptual understanding.

**The Pattern:**

Each analogy follows a similar structure:
1. "Liberation is X"
2. Negative example (what liberation is NOT like)
3. Positive example (what liberation IS like)
4. "True liberation is the Tathāgata"

This repetitive structure creates a meditative rhythm, reinforcing the teaching through repetition.

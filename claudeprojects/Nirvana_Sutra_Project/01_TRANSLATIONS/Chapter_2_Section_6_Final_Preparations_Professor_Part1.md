# Nirvana Sutra - Chapter 2, Section 6: Final Preparations and the Great Earthquake

## Classical Chinese Source Text (Lines 124-144)

**Line 124 - Buddha Tells Cunda It's Time:**
佛告純陀：「汝所奉施佛及大眾，今正是時，如來正爾當般涅槃。」第二、第三亦復如是。

**Line 126 - Cunda Weeps:**
爾時純陀聞佛語已，舉聲啼哭，悲咽而言：「苦哉，苦哉，世間空虛。」復白大眾：「我等今者一切當共五體投地，同聲勸佛莫般涅槃。」

**Line 128 - Buddha's Teaching on Impermanence (Extended Metaphors):**
爾時世尊復告純陀：「莫大啼哭，令心顦悴。當觀是身，猶如芭蕉、熱時之炎、水泡、幻化、乾闥婆城、坏器、電光，亦如畫水、臨死之囚、熟果段肉，如織經盡，如碓上下，當觀諸行，猶雜毒食，有為之法，多諸過患。」

**Line 130 - Cunda Continues Pleading:**
於是純陀復白佛言：「如來不欲久住於世，我當云何而不啼泣？苦哉，苦哉，世間空虛。唯願世尊，憐愍我等及諸眾生，久住於世，勿般涅槃。」

**Lines 132-136 - The Famous Verse on Impermanence:**
佛告純陀：「汝今不應發如是言，憐愍我故，久住於世。我以憐愍汝及一切，是故今欲入於涅槃。何以故？諸佛法爾，有為亦然。是故諸佛而說偈言：

「『有為之法，  	其性無常。  	生已不住，
寂滅為樂。』
「純陀！汝今當觀一切行雜諸法，無我、無常、不住，此身多有無量過患，猶如水泡，是故汝今不應啼泣。」

**Line 138 - Cunda's Understanding:**
爾時純陀復白佛言：「如是如是，誠如尊教。雖知如來方便示現入於涅槃，而我不能不懷苦惱，覆自思惟，復生慶悅。」

**Lines 140-142 - Buddha's Final Instructions:**
佛讚純陀：「善哉，善哉！能知如來示同眾生，方便涅槃。純陀！汝今當聽，如娑羅娑鳥，春陽之月皆共集彼阿耨達池。諸佛亦爾，皆至是處。純陀！汝今不應思惟諸佛長壽短壽，一切諸法皆如幻相，如來在中以方便力無所染著。何以故？諸佛法爾。純陀！我今受汝所獻供養，為欲令汝度於生死諸有流故。若諸人、天於此最後供養我者，悉皆當得不動果報，常受安樂。何以故？我是眾生良福田故。汝若復欲為諸眾生作福田者，速辦所施，不宜久停。」

爾時純陀，為諸眾生得度脫故，低頭飲淚而白佛言：「善哉，世尊！我若堪任為福田時，則能了知如來涅槃及非涅槃。我等今者及諸聲聞、緣覺智慧猶如蚊蟻，實不能量如來涅槃及非涅槃。」

**Lines 143-144 - Departure and the Great Earthquake:**
爾時純陀及其眷屬，愁憂啼泣，圍遶如來，燒香散花，盡心敬奉。尋與文殊從座而去，供辦食具。其去未久，是時此地六種震動，乃至梵世亦復如是。地動有二，或有地動，或大地動。小動者名為地動，大動者名大地動。有小聲者名曰地動，有大聲者名大地動。獨地動者名曰地動，山河樹木及大海水一切動者，名大地動。一向動者名曰地動，周迴旋轉名大地動。動名地動，動時能令眾生心動名大地動。菩薩初從兜率天下閻浮提時名大地動，從初生、出家、成阿耨多羅三藐三菩提、轉於法輪及般涅槃名大地動。今日如來將入涅槃，是故此地如是大動。

## Agent 1: The Professor (Literal Scholarly Translation)

### Section 1: Buddha Announces the Time (Line 124)

**Word-by-word breakdown:**

佛告純陀 (fó gào chún tuó) - "Buddha told Cunda"

汝所奉施佛及大眾 (rǔ suǒ fèng shī fó jí dà zhòng) - "you what respectfully-offer Buddha and great-assembly"

今正是時 (jīn zhèng shì shí) - "now precisely is time"

如來正爾當般涅槃 (rú lái zhèng ěr dāng bān niè pán) - "Tathāgata precisely thus should enter nirvāṇa"

第二、第三亦復如是 (dì èr dì sān yì fù rú shì) - "second [time], third [time] also again like this"

**Literal translation:**

"The Buddha told Cunda: 'The [food] you respectfully offer to the Buddha and the great assembly—now is precisely the time. The Tathāgata is precisely now about to enter parinirvāṇa.' [He said this] a second and third time, also in the same way."

**Key terms:**
- **般涅槃 (bān niè pán)** - *parinirvāṇa* - Final nirvāṇa, complete extinction
- **奉施 (fèng shī)** - Respectful offering

**Scholarly notes:**
The triple repetition emphasizes the solemnity and finality of the announcement. This is a formal declaration, not casual speech.

### Section 2: Cunda's Grief (Line 126)

**Word-by-word breakdown:**

爾時純陀聞佛語已 (ěr shí chún tuó wén fó yǔ yǐ) - "at-that-time Cunda hearing Buddha's-words completed"

舉聲啼哭 (jǔ shēng tí kū) - "raising voice weeping crying"

悲咽而言 (bēi yān ér yán) - "sorrowfully choking and saying"

苦哉，苦哉，世間空虛 (kǔ zāi kǔ zāi shì jiān kōng xū) - "suffering alas, suffering alas, world empty void"

復白大眾 (fù bái dà zhòng) - "again addressing great-assembly"

我等今者一切當共五體投地 (wǒ děng jīn zhě yī qiè dāng gòng wǔ tǐ tóu dì) - "we now all should together five-limbs throw-to-ground"

同聲勸佛莫般涅槃 (tóng shēng quàn fó mò bān niè pán) - "same voice urge Buddha not enter nirvāṇa"

**Literal translation:**

"At that time, when Cunda heard the Buddha's words, [he] raised his voice weeping and crying, sorrowfully choking and saying: 'Suffering! Alas! Suffering! Alas! The world [will be] empty and void!' Again addressing the great assembly: 'We now should all together throw our five limbs to the ground [prostrate fully], with one voice urging the Buddha not to enter parinirvāṇa.'"

**Key terms:**
- **五體投地 (wǔ tǐ tóu dì)** - Five-limb prostration (forehead, two hands, two knees)
- **空虛 (kōng xū)** - Empty and void; desolate

### Section 3: Buddha's Extended Impermanence Teaching (Line 128)

**Word-by-word breakdown:**

爾時世尊復告純陀 (ěr shí shì zūn fù gào chún tuó) - "at-that-time World-Honored-One again told Cunda"

莫大啼哭 (mò dà tí kū) - "don't greatly weep cry"

令心顦悴 (lìng xīn qiáo cuì) - "causing heart haggard withered"

當觀是身 (dāng guān shì shēn) - "should contemplate this body"

**The Twelve Similes:**

1. 猶如芭蕉 (yóu rú bā jiāo) - "just like banana-tree" [hollow inside]
2. 熱時之炎 (rè shí zhī yán) - "hot time's flames" [heat mirages]
3. 水泡 (shuǐ pào) - "water bubbles"
4. 幻化 (huàn huà) - "illusion transformation" [magic tricks]
5. 乾闥婆城 (qián tà pó chéng) - "gandharva city" [mirage city]
6. 坏器 (huài qì) - "broken vessel"
7. 電光 (diàn guāng) - "lightning light"
8. 畫水 (huà shuǐ) - "drawing [on] water"
9. 臨死之囚 (lín sǐ zhī qiú) - "approaching death prisoner"
10. 熟果 (shú guǒ) - "ripe fruit"
11. 段肉 (duàn ròu) - "piece meat" [on a hook]
12. 織經盡 (zhī jīng jìn) - "weaving warp exhausted"
13. 碓上下 (duì shàng xià) - "pestle up down"

當觀諸行，猶雜毒食 (dāng guān zhū xíng yóu zá dú shí) - "should contemplate all-conditioned-things like mixed poison food"

有為之法，多諸過患 (yǒu wéi zhī fǎ duō zhū guò huàn) - "conditioned dharmas, many various faults troubles"

**Literal translation:**

"At that time the World-Honored One again told Cunda: 'Do not weep greatly, causing [your] heart to become haggard and withered. You should contemplate this body as being just like: a banana tree, heat mirages, water bubbles, magical illusions, a gandharva city, a broken vessel, lightning, drawing on water, a prisoner facing death, ripe fruit, a piece of meat [on a hook], weaving with the warp thread exhausted, a pestle going up and down. You should contemplate all conditioned things as being like food mixed with poison. Conditioned dharmas have many faults and troubles.'"

**Key terms:**
- **芭蕉 (bā jiāo)** - Banana tree/plantain; appears solid but has no core
- **乾闥婆城 (qián tà pó chéng)** - *Gandharva-nagara*; mirage city built by celestial musicians
- **諸行 (zhū xíng)** - *Saṃskāra*; all conditioned/compounded things

**Scholarly notes:**
This is one of the most extensive collections of impermanence similes in Buddhist literature. Each metaphor emphasizes a different aspect: hollowness (banana tree), illusoriness (mirage), fragility (bubbles), transience (lightning), futility (drawing on water), inevitability (condemned prisoner).

### Section 4: The Famous Verse (Lines 132-136)

**Word-by-word breakdown:**

諸佛法爾 (zhū fó fǎ ěr) - "all Buddhas' dharma thus"

有為亦然 (yǒu wéi yì rán) - "conditioned also so"

是故諸佛而說偈言 (shì gù zhū fó ér shuō jì yán) - "therefore all Buddhas thus speak verse saying"

**The Verse:**

有為之法 (yǒu wéi zhī fǎ) - "conditioned dharmas"  
其性無常 (qí xìng wú cháng) - "their nature impermanent"  
生已不住 (shēng yǐ bù zhù) - "born already not abiding"  
寂滅為樂 (jì miè wéi lè) - "extinction-quiescence is bliss"

**Literal translation:**

"The Buddha told Cunda: 'You should not speak such words [as] "out of compassion for me, [you should] long dwell in the world." I, out of compassion for you and all [beings], therefore now wish to enter nirvāṇa. Why? [Because] the Buddhas' dharma is thus; conditioned [existence] is also so. Therefore all Buddhas speak this verse:

"Conditioned dharmas—  
Their nature is impermanent.  
Once born, [they] do not abide.  
Extinction-quiescence is bliss."

Cunda! You now should contemplate all conditioned things [and] various dharmas [as] no-self, impermanent, not abiding. This body has many countless faults and troubles, just like water bubbles. Therefore you now should not weep.'"

**Key terms:**
- **有為之法 (yǒu wéi zhī fǎ)** - *Saṃskṛta-dharma*; conditioned/compounded phenomena
- **寂滅 (jì miè)** - *Nirodha*; cessation, extinction, quiescence
- **無常 (wú cháng)** - *Anitya*; impermanence

**Scholarly notes:**
This verse (諸行無常偈) is one of the most famous in all of Buddhism, often called the "Verse on Impermanence." It appears in multiple sūtras and is chanted throughout Buddhist traditions. The paradox—"extinction is bliss"—points to nirvāṇa as the cessation of suffering, not annihilation of being.

# Nirvana Sutra - Chapter 2, Section 1: Cunda's Prayer and Dialogue

## Classical Chinese Source Text (Lines 6-12)

**Line 6 - Cunda's Prayer:**
爾時會中有優婆塞，是拘尸那[12]城工巧之子，名曰純陀，與其同類十五人俱。為令世間得善果故，捨身威儀，從座而起，偏袒右肩，右膝著地，合掌向佛，悲泣墮淚，頂禮佛足，而白佛言：「唯願世尊及比丘僧，哀受我等最後供養，為度無量諸眾生故。世尊！我等從今無主、無親、無救、無護、無歸、無趣、貧窮飢困，欲從如來求將來食，唯願哀愍，受我微供，然後乃入於般涅槃。世尊！譬如剎利、若婆羅門、毘舍、首陀，以貧窮故，遠至他國，役力農作，得好調牛，良田平正，無諸沙[13]鹵、惡草、株杌，唯悕天雨。言調牛者，喻身口七。良田平正，喻於智慧。除去沙[＊]鹵、惡草、株杌，喻[14]除煩惱。世尊！我今身有調牛、良田、除去株杌，唯悕如來甘露法雨。貧四姓者，即我身是，貧於無上法之財寶。唯願哀愍，除斷我等貧窮困苦，[15]拯及無量苦惱眾生。我今所供，雖復微少，冀得充足如來大眾。我今無主、無親、無歸，願垂矜愍，如羅睺羅。」

**Line 8 - Buddha's Response:**
爾時世尊、一切種智、無上調御，告純陀曰：「善哉，善哉！我今為汝除斷貧窮，無上法雨雨汝身田，令生法[1]芽。汝今於我，欲求壽命、[2]色力、[3]安、辯，我當施汝常命、色力、安、無礙辯。何以故？純陀！施食有二，果報無差。何等為二？一者受已，得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提；二者受已，入於涅槃。我今受汝最後供養，令汝具足檀波羅蜜。」

**Line 10 - Cunda's Challenge:**
爾時純陀即白佛言：「如佛所說，二施果報無差別者，是義不然。何以故？先受施者，煩惱未盡，未得成就一切種智，亦未能令眾生具足檀波羅蜜；後受施者，煩惱已盡，已得成就一切種智，能令眾生普得具足檀波羅蜜。先受施者，直是眾生；後受施者，是天中天。先受施者，是雜食身、煩惱之身、是後邊身、是無常身；後受施者，無煩惱身、金剛之身、法身、常身、無邊之身。云何而言，二施果報等無差別？先受施者，未能具足檀波羅蜜乃至般若波羅蜜，唯得肉眼，未得佛眼乃至慧眼；後受施者，已得具足檀波羅蜜乃至般若波羅蜜，具足佛眼乃至慧眼。云何而言二施果報等無差別？世尊！先受施者，受已食噉，入腹消化，得命、得色、得力、得安、得無礙辯；後受施者，不食、不消、無五事果；云何而言二施果報等無差別？」

**Line 12 - Buddha's Profound Teaching:**
佛言：「善男子！如來已於無量無邊阿僧祇劫，無有食身、煩惱之身，無後邊身，常身、法身、金剛之身。善男子，未見佛性者，名煩惱身、雜食之身，是後邊身。菩薩爾時受飲食已，入金剛三昧，此食消已，即見佛性，得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提，是故我言，二施果報等無差別。菩薩爾時破壞四魔，今入涅槃，亦破四魔，是故我言二施果報等無差別。菩薩爾時雖不廣說十二部經，先已通達，今入涅槃，廣為眾生分別演說，是故我言二施果報等無差別。善男子！如來之身，已於無量阿僧祇劫不受飲食。為諸聲聞說言，先受難陀、難陀波羅二牧牛女所奉乳[4]糜，然後乃得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提；我實不食。我今[5]為於此會大眾，是故受汝最後所奉；實亦不食。」

## Agent 1: The Professor (Literal Scholarly Translation)

### Section 1: Cunda's Appearance and Initial Prayer (Line 6, Part 1)

**Word-by-word breakdown:**

爾時 (ěr shí) - "at that time"  
會中 (huì zhōng) - "assembly-within" / "in the assembly"  
有 (yǒu) - "there was"  
優婆塞 (yōu pó sāi) - **upāsaka** (male lay Buddhist devotee)  
是 (shì) - "this/who was"  
拘尸那城 (jū shī nà chéng) - **Kuśinagara** city  
工巧之子 (gōng qiǎo zhī zǐ) - "artisan's son" / "craftsman's son"  
名曰 (míng yuē) - "named/called"  
純陀 (chún tuó) - **Cunda** (personal name)  
與其同類 (yǔ qí tóng lèi) - "with his same-kind" / "with his companions"  
十五人俱 (shí wǔ rén jù) - "fifteen people together"

為令世間得善果故 (wèi lìng shì jiān dé shàn guǒ gù) - "in order to cause the world to obtain good fruits"  
捨身威儀 (shě shēn wēi yí) - "abandoning body-dignified-deportment" / "setting aside bodily composure"  
從座而起 (cóng zuò ér qǐ) - "from seat rising"  
偏袒右肩 (piān tǎn yòu jiān) - "baring right shoulder" (ritual gesture)  
右膝著地 (yòu xī zhuó dì) - "right knee touching ground"  
合掌向佛 (hé zhǎng xiàng fó) - "palms-joined toward Buddha"  
悲泣墮淚 (bēi qì duò lèi) - "sorrowfully weeping, tears falling"  
頂禮佛足 (dǐng lǐ fó zú) - "head bowing to Buddha's feet"  
而白佛言 (ér bái fó yán) - "and addressed Buddha saying"

**Literal translation:**

"At that time, in the assembly there was an upāsaka, a son of an artisan from the city of Kuśinagara, named Cunda, together with fifteen companions of his kind. In order to cause the world to obtain good fruits, he set aside his bodily composure, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, wept sorrowfully with falling tears, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and addressed the Buddha saying:"

**Key terms:**
- **優婆塞 (upāsaka)** - Male lay follower who has taken refuge in the Three Jewels
- **拘尸那城 (Kuśinagara)** - The city where the Buddha entered parinirvāṇa
- **純陀 (Cunda)** - Name means "pure" or "refined"; a blacksmith's son in some accounts

**Scholarly notes:**
The ritual gestures described (baring right shoulder, right knee on ground, joined palms) represent the traditional Indian prostration posture showing utmost respect. Cunda's emotional state (weeping) contrasts with the formal ritual, showing genuine grief.

### Section 2: Cunda's Prayer - The Plea for Acceptance (Line 6, Part 2)

**Word-by-word breakdown:**

唯願 (wéi yuàn) - "only wish/pray that"  
世尊 (shì zūn) - "World-Honored-One" (Buddha epithet)  
及 (jí) - "and/together with"  
比丘僧 (bǐ qiū sēng) - **bhikṣu-saṅgha** (community of monks)  
哀受 (āi shòu) - "compassionately receive/accept"  
我等 (wǒ děng) - "we/us" (plural)  
最後供養 (zuì hòu gōng yǎng) - "final offering"  
為度無量諸眾生故 (wèi dù wú liàng zhū zhòng shēng gù) - "for the sake of liberating immeasurable sentient beings"

世尊 (shì zūn) - "World-Honored-One"  
我等從今 (wǒ děng cóng jīn) - "we from now"  
無主 (wú zhǔ) - "without master"  
無親 (wú qīn) - "without relatives"  
無救 (wú jiù) - "without rescue"  
無護 (wú hù) - "without protection"  
無歸 (wú guī) - "without refuge"  
無趣 (wú qù) - "without destination/goal"  
貧窮飢困 (pín qióng jī kùn) - "poor, hungry, distressed"  
欲從如來求將來食 (yù cóng rú lái qiú jiāng lái shí) - "wish from Tathāgata to seek future food"

唯願哀愍 (wéi yuàn āi mǐn) - "only pray compassionately pity"  
受我微供 (shòu wǒ wēi gōng) - "receive my humble offering"  
然後乃入於般涅槃 (rán hòu nǎi rù yú bān niè pán) - "then afterward enter into parinirvāṇa"

**Literal translation:**

"'I only pray that the World-Honored One and the community of monks compassionately receive our final offering, for the sake of liberating immeasurable sentient beings. World-Honored One! We from this time forward are without master, without relatives, without rescue, without protection, without refuge, without destination—poor, hungry, and distressed. We wish to seek from the Tathāgata the food of the future. I only pray [you] compassionately take pity and receive my humble offering, and then afterward enter into parinirvāṇa.'"

**Key terms:**
- **世尊 (Bhagavān)** - "World-Honored One," one of the ten epithets of Buddha
- **比丘僧 (bhikṣu-saṅgha)** - The monastic community
- **如來 (Tathāgata)** - "Thus-Come-One," epithet of Buddha
- **般涅槃 (parinirvāṇa)** - Final nirvāṇa, the Buddha's passing

**Scholarly notes:**
The six-fold negation (無主、無親、無救、無護、無歸、無趣) is a rhetorical device emphasizing complete spiritual destitution without the Buddha. "Future food" (將來食) is a metaphor for dharma teachings that will sustain practitioners after the Buddha's passing.

### Section 3: The Farmer Parable (Line 6, Part 3)

**Word-by-word breakdown:**

世尊 (shì zūn) - "World-Honored-One"  
譬如 (pì rú) - "for example/like"  
剎利 (chà lì) - **kṣatriya** (warrior/royal caste)  
若 (ruò) - "or"  
婆羅門 (pó luó mén) - **brāhmaṇa** (priestly caste)  
毘舍 (pí shè) - **vaiśya** (merchant caste)  
首陀 (shǒu tuó) - **śūdra** (laborer caste)  
以貧窮故 (yǐ pín qióng gù) - "because of poverty"  
遠至他國 (yuǎn zhì tā guó) - "far reaching other country"  
役力農作 (yì lì nóng zuò) - "laboring at agricultural work"

得好調牛 (dé hǎo tiáo niú) - "obtaining good trained oxen"  
良田平正 (liáng tián píng zhèng) - "good field level and proper"  
無諸沙鹵 (wú zhū shā lǔ) - "without various sand-and-alkali"  
惡草 (è cǎo) - "bad grass/weeds"  
株杌 (zhū wù) - "stumps and roots"  
唯悕天雨 (wéi xī tiān yǔ) - "only hoping-for heaven's rain"

**Allegorical interpretation (provided in text):**

言調牛者，喻身口七 - "Speaking of trained oxen, this metaphorically refers to the seven [perfections] of body and speech"  
良田平正，喻於智慧 - "Good level field metaphorically refers to wisdom"  
除去沙鹵、惡草、株杌，喻除煩惱 - "Removing sand-alkali, weeds, stumps metaphorically refers to removing afflictions"

**Literal translation:**

"'World-Honored One! For example, [suppose] a kṣatriya, or a brāhmaṇa, vaiśya, or śūdra, because of poverty, travels far to another country and labors at agricultural work. He obtains good trained oxen and a good field that is level and proper, without sand and alkali, bad grass, or stumps and roots—he only hopes for heaven's rain. Speaking of trained oxen metaphorically refers to the seven [perfections] of body and speech. A good level field metaphorically refers to wisdom. Removing sand-alkali, bad grass, and stumps metaphorically refers to removing afflictions.'"

**Key terms:**
- **Four castes (四姓)** - The traditional Indian social hierarchy
- **身口七** - The seven perfections of body (three: right action, speech, livelihood) and speech (four: abstaining from false speech, divisive speech, harsh speech, idle chatter)
- **煩惱 (kleśa)** - Afflictions, mental defilements

**Scholarly notes:**
Cunda explicitly provides his own allegorical interpretation, unusual in sūtra literature. This self-exegesis demonstrates his spiritual sophistication. The agricultural metaphor is common in Buddhist literature (cf. the Parable of the Sower in various traditions).

### Section 4: Cunda's Self-Application and Final Plea (Line 6, Part 4)

**Word-by-word breakdown:**

世尊 (shì zūn) - "World-Honored-One"  
我今身有調牛、良田、除去株杌 (wǒ jīn shēn yǒu tiáo niú, liáng tián, chú qù zhū wù) - "I now in body have trained oxen, good field, removed stumps"  
唯悕如來甘露法雨 (wéi xī rú lái gān lù fǎ yǔ) - "only hoping-for Tathāgata's ambrosia dharma-rain"

貧四姓者 (pín sì xìng zhě) - "poor [person of] four castes"  
即我身是 (jí wǒ shēn shì) - "precisely my body is"  
貧於無上法之財寶 (pín yú wú shàng fǎ zhī cái bǎo) - "poor in unsurpassed dharma's wealth-treasures"

唯願哀愍 (wéi yuàn āi mǐn) - "only pray compassionately pity"  
除斷我等貧窮困苦 (chú duàn wǒ děng pín qióng kùn kǔ) - "remove and sever our poverty and suffering"  
拯及無量苦惱眾生 (zhěng jí wú liàng kǔ nǎo zhòng shēng) - "rescue and reach immeasurable suffering sentient beings"

我今所供，雖復微少 (wǒ jīn suǒ gōng, suī fù wēi shǎo) - "my present offering, although again humble and small"  
冀得充足如來大眾 (jì dé chōng zú rú lái dà zhòng) - "hope to be able to satisfy Tathāgata's great assembly"

我今無主、無親、無歸 (wǒ jīn wú zhǔ, wú qīn, wú guī) - "I now without master, without relatives, without refuge"  
願垂矜愍 (yuàn chuí jīn mǐn) - "pray [you] extend compassionate pity"  
如羅睺羅 (rú luó hóu luó) - "like Rāhula"

**Literal translation:**

"'World-Honored One! I now in my body have the trained oxen, the good field, and have removed the stumps—I only hope for the Tathāgata's ambrosia dharma-rain. The poor person of the four castes is precisely my own body—poor in the wealth and treasures of the unsurpassed Dharma. I only pray [you] compassionately take pity and remove and sever our poverty and suffering, rescuing and reaching immeasurable suffering sentient beings. My present offering, although humble and small, I hope will be able to satisfy the Tathāgata and the great assembly. I now am without master, without relatives, without refuge—I pray [you] extend compassionate pity as [you do] to Rāhula.'"

**Key terms:**
- **甘露 (amṛta)** - Ambrosia, nectar of immortality; metaphor for dharma teachings
- **法雨** - "Dharma rain," teachings that nourish spiritual growth
- **羅睺羅 (Rāhula)** - The Buddha's biological son, who became a monk

**Scholarly notes:**
The comparison to Rāhula is poignant—Cunda asks to be treated with the same care the Buddha showed his own son. This reveals both Cunda's humility and his deep longing for spiritual guidance. The "ambrosia dharma-rain" (甘露法雨) combines Vedic imagery (amṛta) with Buddhist teaching metaphors.

### Section 5: Buddha's Response - The Teaching on Two Offerings (Line 8)

**Word-by-word breakdown:**

爾時 (ěr shí) - "at that time"  
世尊 (shì zūn) - "World-Honored-One"  
一切種智 (yī qiè zhǒng zhì) - **sarvajñatā** (all-種-knowledge / omniscience)  
無上調御 (wú shàng tiáo yù) - "unsurpassed tamer/controller"  
告純陀曰 (gào chún tuó yuē) - "told Cunda saying"

善哉，善哉 (shàn zāi, shàn zāi) - "excellent, excellent" (sādhu, sādhu)  
我今為汝除斷貧窮 (wǒ jīn wèi rǔ chú duàn pín qióng) - "I now for you remove and sever poverty"  
無上法雨雨汝身田 (wú shàng fǎ yǔ yǔ rǔ shēn tián) - "unsurpassed dharma-rain rains-upon your body-field"  
令生法芽 (lìng shēng fǎ yá) - "causing to grow dharma-sprouts"

汝今於我 (rǔ jīn yú wǒ) - "you now from me"  
欲求壽命、色力、安、辯 (yù qiú shòu mìng, sè lì, ān, biàn) - "wish to seek longevity, physical-strength, peace, eloquence"  
我當施汝 (wǒ dāng shī rǔ) - "I shall give you"  
常命、色力、安、無礙辯 (cháng mìng, sè lì, ān, wú ài biàn) - "eternal life, physical-strength, peace, unobstructed eloquence"

何以故 (hé yǐ gù) - "why is this?"  
純陀 (chún tuó) - "Cunda"  
施食有二 (shī shí yǒu èr) - "food-offerings have two [types]"  
果報無差 (guǒ bào wú chā) - "karmic-results without difference"

何等為二 (hé děng wéi èr) - "what are the two?"  
一者受已，得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 (yī zhě shòu yǐ, dé ā nòu duō luó sān miǎo sān pú tí) - "first, after receiving, [one] attains anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi"  
二者受已，入於涅槃 (èr zhě shòu yǐ, rù yú niè pán) - "second, after receiving, [one] enters nirvāṇa"

我今受汝最後供養 (wǒ jīn shòu rǔ zuì hòu gōng yǎng) - "I now receive your final offering"  
令汝具足檀波羅蜜 (lìng rǔ jù zú tán bō luó mì) - "causing you to perfect dāna-pāramitā"

**Literal translation:**

"At that time, the World-Honored One, possessing omniscience, the unsurpassed tamer, told Cunda saying: 'Excellent, excellent! I now for you remove and sever poverty. The unsurpassed dharma-rain rains upon your body-field, causing dharma-sprouts to grow. You now from me wish to seek longevity, physical strength, peace, and eloquence—I shall give you eternal life, physical strength, peace, and unobstructed eloquence. Why is this? Cunda! Food offerings have two types, [whose] karmic results are without difference. What are the two? First, after [the Buddha] receives [the offering], [he] attains unsurpassed perfect enlightenment. Second, after [the Buddha] receives [the offering], [he] enters nirvāṇa. I now receive your final offering, causing you to perfect the perfection of giving.'"

**Key terms:**
- **一切種智 (sarvajñatā)** - Omniscience, all-種-knowledge; Buddha's complete knowledge
- **阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 (anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi)** - Unsurpassed perfect enlightenment
- **檀波羅蜜 (dāna-pāramitā)** - Perfection of giving/generosity, first of six perfections

**Scholarly notes:**
The Buddha picks up Cunda's agricultural metaphor (body-field, dharma-rain, dharma-sprouts). The "two offerings" refers to: (1) the milk-rice offered by Sujātā before the Buddha's enlightenment, and (2) Cunda's final meal before parinirvāṇa. This teaching establishes their spiritual equivalence—a profound consolation to Cunda.

### Section 6: Cunda's Sophisticated Challenge (Line 10)

**Opening statement:**

爾時純陀即白佛言 (ěr shí chún tuó jí bái fó yán) - "At that time Cunda immediately addressed Buddha saying"  
如佛所說，二施果報無差別者，是義不然 (rú fó suǒ shuō, èr shī guǒ bào wú chā bié zhě, shì yì bù rán) - "As Buddha has said, [that] the two offerings' karmic results are without difference—this meaning is not so"

**First contrast - Spiritual attainment:**

先受施者 (xiān shòu shī zhě) - "the earlier receiver of offering"  
煩惱未盡 (fán nǎo wèi jìn) - "afflictions not yet exhausted"  
未得成就一切種智 (wèi dé chéng jiù yī qiè zhǒng zhì) - "not yet attained and accomplished omniscience"  
亦未能令眾生具足檀波羅蜜 (yì wèi néng lìng zhòng shēng jù zú tán bō luó mì) - "also not yet able to cause sentient beings to perfect dāna-pāramitā"

後受施者 (hòu shòu shī zhě) - "the later receiver of offering"  
煩惱已盡 (fán nǎo yǐ jìn) - "afflictions already exhausted"  
已得成就一切種智 (yǐ dé chéng jiù yī qiè zhǒng zhì) - "already attained and accomplished omniscience"  
能令眾生普得具足檀波羅蜜 (néng lìng zhòng shēng pǔ dé jù zú tán bō luó mì) - "able to cause sentient beings universally to perfect dāna-pāramitā"

**Second contrast - Status:**

先受施者，直是眾生 (xiān shòu shī zhě, zhí shì zhòng shēng) - "the earlier receiver, merely is a sentient being"  
後受施者，是天中天 (hòu shòu shī zhě, shì tiān zhōng tiān) - "the later receiver, is god among gods"

**Third contrast - Body nature:**

先受施者 (xiān shòu shī zhě) - "the earlier receiver"  
是雜食身 (shì zá shí shēn) - "is mixed-food body"  
煩惱之身 (fán nǎo zhī shēn) - "affliction body"  
是後邊身 (shì hòu biān shēn) - "is final-edge body" (last rebirth)  
是無常身 (shì wú cháng shēn) - "is impermanent body"

後受施者 (hòu shòu shī zhě) - "the later receiver"  
無煩惱身 (wú fán nǎo shēn) - "without-affliction body"  
金剛之身 (jīn gāng zhī shēn) - "vajra body"  
法身 (fǎ shēn) - **dharmakāya** (dharma body)  
常身 (cháng shēn) - "eternal body"  
無邊之身 (wú biān zhī shēn) - "limitless body"

**Fourth contrast - Perfections and vision:**

先受施者 (xiān shòu shī zhě) - "the earlier receiver"  
未能具足檀波羅蜜乃至般若波羅蜜 (wèi néng jù zú tán bō luó mì nǎi zhì bō rě bō luó mì) - "not yet able to perfect dāna-pāramitā up to prajñā-pāramitā"  
唯得肉眼 (wéi dé ròu yǎn) - "only obtained flesh-eye"  
未得佛眼乃至慧眼 (wèi dé fó yǎn nǎi zhì huì yǎn) - "not yet obtained Buddha-eye up to wisdom-eye"

後受施者 (hòu shòu shī zhě) - "the later receiver"  
已得具足檀波羅蜜乃至般若波羅蜜 (yǐ dé jù zú tán bō luó mì nǎi zhì bō rě bō luó mì) - "already perfected dāna-pāramitā up to prajñā-pāramitā"  
具足佛眼乃至慧眼 (jù zú fó yǎn nǎi zhì huì yǎn) - "perfected Buddha-eye up to wisdom-eye"

**Fifth contrast - Physical effects:**

世尊 (shì zūn) - "World-Honored-One"  
先受施者，受已食噉，入腹消化 (xiān shòu shī zhě, shòu yǐ shí dàn, rù fù xiāo huà) - "the earlier receiver, after receiving eats and devours, [it] enters belly and digests"  
得命、得色、得力、得安、得無礙辯 (dé mìng, dé sè, dé lì, dé ān, dé wú ài biàn) - "obtains life, obtains complexion, obtains strength, obtains peace, obtains unobstructed eloquence"

後受施者 (hòu shòu shī zhě) - "the later receiver"  
不食、不消 (bù shí, bù xiāo) - "does not eat, does not digest"  
無五事果 (wú wǔ shì guǒ) - "without five-matters' results"

云何而言二施果報等無差別 (yún hé ér yán èr shī guǒ bào děng wú chā bié) - "How can [you] say the two offerings' karmic results are equal without difference?"

**Literal translation:**

"At that time Cunda immediately addressed the Buddha saying: 'As the Buddha has said, [that] the two offerings' karmic results are without difference—this meaning is not so. Why is this? The earlier receiver of the offering had afflictions not yet exhausted, had not yet attained and accomplished omniscience, and was also not yet able to cause sentient beings to perfect the perfection of giving. The later receiver of the offering has afflictions already exhausted, has already attained and accomplished omniscience, and is able to cause sentient beings universally to perfect the perfection of giving.

The earlier receiver of the offering was merely a sentient being; the later receiver of the offering is god among gods.

The earlier receiver of the offering had a mixed-food body, an affliction body, a final-edge body, an impermanent body. The later receiver of the offering has a body without afflictions, a vajra body, a dharma body, an eternal body, a limitless body. How can [you] say the two offerings' karmic results are equal without difference?

The earlier receiver of the offering was not yet able to perfect the perfection of giving up to the perfection of wisdom, and only obtained the flesh-eye, not yet obtaining the Buddha-eye up to the wisdom-eye. The later receiver of the offering has already perfected the perfection of giving up to the perfection of wisdom, and has perfected the Buddha-eye up to the wisdom-eye. How can [you] say the two offerings' karmic results are equal without difference?

World-Honored One! The earlier receiver of the offering, after receiving, ate and devoured [it], [it] entered the belly and was digested, obtaining life, complexion, strength, peace, and unobstructed eloquence. The later receiver of the offering does not eat, does not digest, and has no results from these five matters. How can [you] say the two offerings' karmic results are equal without difference?'"

**Key terms:**
- **天中天 (deva-deva)** - "God among gods," supreme deity
- **金剛之身 (vajra-kāya)** - Indestructible diamond/thunderbolt body
- **法身 (dharmakāya)** - Dharma body, the absolute aspect of Buddhahood
- **後邊身** - "Final-edge body," the last physical rebirth before enlightenment
- **六波羅蜜** - Six perfections: giving, morality, patience, vigor, meditation, wisdom
- **五眼** - Five eyes: flesh-eye, divine-eye, wisdom-eye, dharma-eye, Buddha-eye

**Scholarly notes:**
This is a remarkably sophisticated theological argument. Cunda demonstrates deep understanding of Mahāyāna doctrine by contrasting the pre-enlightenment Bodhisattva (Siddhārtha receiving Sujātā's offering) with the fully enlightened Buddha about to enter parinirvāṇa. His five-fold argument systematically dismantles the Buddha's claim of equivalence, setting up the profound teaching that follows. This dialectical method is characteristic of Mahāyāna sūtras, where lay practitioners sometimes challenge the Buddha to elicit deeper teachings.

### Section 7: Buddha's Profound Response - The Teaching on Buddha-Nature (Line 12)

**Opening - Correcting the view of Buddha's body:**

佛言 (fó yán) - "Buddha said"  
善男子 (shàn nán zǐ) - "good son"  
如來已於無量無邊阿僧祇劫 (rú lái yǐ yú wú liàng wú biān ā sēng qí jié) - "Tathāgata already for immeasurable limitless asaṃkhyeya kalpas"  
無有食身、煩惱之身 (wú yǒu shí shēn, fán nǎo zhī shēn) - "has not had food-body, affliction-body"  
無後邊身 (wú hòu biān shēn) - "no final-edge body"  
常身、法身、金剛之身 (cháng shēn, fǎ shēn, jīn gāng zhī shēn) - "eternal body, dharma body, vajra body"

**The key teaching on Buddha-nature:**

善男子 (shàn nán zǐ) - "good son"  
未見佛性者 (wèi jiàn fó xìng zhě) - "those who have not yet seen Buddha-nature"  
名煩惱身、雜食之身，是後邊身 (míng fán nǎo shēn, zá shí zhī shēn, shì hòu biān shēn) - "are called affliction-body, mixed-food body, final-edge body"

菩薩爾時 (pú sà ěr shí) - "the Bodhisattva at that time"  
受飲食已 (shòu yǐn shí yǐ) - "after receiving food and drink"  
入金剛三昧 (rù jīn gāng sān mèi) - "entered vajra-samādhi"  
此食消已 (cǐ shí xiāo yǐ) - "when this food was digested"  
即見佛性 (jí jiàn fó xìng) - "immediately saw Buddha-nature"  
得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 (dé ā nòu duō luó sān miǎo sān pú tí) - "attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi"

是故我言，二施果報等無差別 (shì gù wǒ yán, èr shī guǒ bào děng wú chā bié) - "therefore I say, the two offerings' karmic results are equal without difference"

**Three reasons for equivalence:**

**First reason - Defeating the four Māras:**

菩薩爾時破壞四魔 (pú sà ěr shí pò huài sì mó) - "the Bodhisattva at that time destroyed the four māras"  
今入涅槃，亦破四魔 (jīn rù niè pán, yì pò sì mó) - "now entering nirvāṇa, also destroys the four māras"  
是故我言二施果報等無差別 (shì gù wǒ yán èr shī guǒ bào děng wú chā bié) - "therefore I say the two offerings' karmic results are equal without difference"

**Second reason - Teaching the dharma:**

菩薩爾時雖不廣說十二部經 (pú sà ěr shí suī bù guǎng shuō shí èr bù jīng) - "the Bodhisattva at that time, although not extensively teaching the twelve divisions of scripture"  
先已通達 (xiān yǐ tōng dá) - "had already thoroughly mastered [them]"  
今入涅槃 (jīn rù niè pán) - "now entering nirvāṇa"  
廣為眾生分別演說 (guǎng wèi zhòng shēng fēn bié yǎn shuō) - "extensively for sentient beings distinguishing and expounding"  
是故我言二施果報等無差別 (shì gù wǒ yán èr shī guǒ bào děng wú chā bié) - "therefore I say the two offerings' karmic results are equal without difference"

**The ultimate teaching - Buddha never actually eats:**

善男子 (shàn nán zǐ) - "good son"  
如來之身 (rú lái zhī shēn) - "Tathāgata's body"  
已於無量阿僧祇劫不受飲食 (yǐ yú wú liàng ā sēng qí jié bù shòu yǐn shí) - "already for immeasurable asaṃkhyeya kalpas has not received food and drink"

為諸聲聞說言 (wèi zhū shēng wén shuō yán) - "for the śrāvakas [I] said"  
先受難陀、難陀波羅二牧牛女所奉乳糜 (xiān shòu nán tuó, nán tuó bō luó èr mù niú nǚ suǒ fèng rǔ mí) - "previously received milk-rice offered by the two cowherd girls Nandā and Nandabalā"  
然後乃得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 (rán hòu nǎi dé ā nòu duō luó sān miǎo sān pú tí) - "and then afterward attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi"  
我實不食 (wǒ shí bù shí) - "I actually did not eat"

我今為於此會大眾 (wǒ jīn wèi yú cǐ huì dà zhòng) - "I now for this assembly's great multitude"  
是故受汝最後所奉 (shì gù shòu rǔ zuì hòu suǒ fèng) - "therefore receive your final offering"  
實亦不食 (shí yì bù shí) - "actually also do not eat"

**Literal translation:**

"The Buddha said: 'Good son! The Tathāgata already for immeasurable, limitless asaṃkhyeya kalpas has not had a food-body or affliction-body, has no final-edge body, [but has] an eternal body, dharma body, vajra body.

Good son, those who have not yet seen Buddha-nature are called [those with] affliction-body, mixed-food body, final-edge body. The Bodhisattva at that time, after receiving food and drink, entered the vajra-samādhi. When this food was digested, [he] immediately saw Buddha-nature and attained unsurpassed perfect enlightenment. Therefore I say the two offerings' karmic results are equal without difference.

The Bodhisattva at that time destroyed the four māras; now entering nirvāṇa, [I] also destroy the four māras. Therefore I say the two offerings' karmic results are equal without difference.

The Bodhisattva at that time, although not extensively teaching the twelve divisions of scripture, had already thoroughly mastered [them]. Now entering nirvāṇa, [I] extensively for sentient beings distinguish and expound [them]. Therefore I say the two offerings' karmic results are equal without difference.

Good son! The Tathāgata's body already for immeasurable asaṃkhyeya kalpas has not received food and drink. For the śrāvakas I said [I] previously received milk-rice offered by the two cowherd girls Nandā and Nandabalā, and then afterward attained unsurpassed perfect enlightenment—[but] I actually did not eat. I now for this assembly's great multitude therefore receive your final offering—[but] actually also do not eat.'"

**Key terms:**
- **佛性 (Buddha-nature/buddhata)** - The inherent potential for Buddhahood in all beings; central doctrine of this sūtra
- **金剛三昧 (vajra-samādhi)** - Diamond/indestructible concentration; the final meditation before enlightenment
- **四魔 (four māras)** - Four obstructions: (1) affliction-māra, (2) aggregate-māra, (3) death-māra, (4) deity-māra
- **十二部經 (twelve divisions)** - Traditional classification of Buddhist scriptures by literary form
- **阿僧祇劫 (asaṃkhyeya-kalpa)** - Incalculable eons
- **聲聞 (śrāvaka)** - "Hearers," disciples who attain arhatship through hearing teachings

**Scholarly notes:**

This is one of the most profound passages in the Nirvana Sutra. The Buddha reveals several revolutionary teachings:

1. **Buddha-nature as the pivot**: The key distinction is not between pre-enlightenment and post-enlightenment Buddha, but between those who have and haven't seen Buddha-nature. Once Buddha-nature is seen, one is already Buddha.

2. **Retroactive Buddhahood**: The Buddha claims he has been enlightened for "immeasurable asaṃkhyeya kalpas"—meaning the historical enlightenment under the Bodhi tree was merely a skillful display, not the actual moment of awakening.

3. **Upāya (skillful means)**: The Buddha admits he never actually ate either offering—both were pedagogical displays for beings at different levels (śrāvakas vs. this assembly).

4. **Equivalence through transcendence**: The two offerings are equal precisely because both transcend the conventional categories Cunda used in his challenge. The Buddha operates beyond eating/not-eating, body/no-body.

This teaching anticipates the full development of trikāya (three-body) doctrine and represents mature Mahāyāna soteriology where enlightenment is realization of what has always been true, not acquisition of something new.

**Scholarly notes:**
Cunda systematically lists the six realms of rebirth and how meeting Buddha helps escape each: three evil paths (hell, hungry ghost, animal), asura realm, and implicitly human and heavenly realms.

## Key Terms Breakdown

### The Professor's Philological Analysis

**1. 佛性 (fó xìng) - Buddha-nature**

- **Sanskrit:** *buddhatā* or *tathāgatagarbha*
- **Literal meaning:** 佛 (Buddha) + 性 (nature/essence)
- **Why this translation:** The character 性 (xìng) means "inherent nature" or "essential quality." Chinese translators chose this to convey that Buddha-nature is not something acquired but something **inherent** in all beings. It's your fundamental nature, not an external addition.
- **Doctrinal significance:** This is the central concept of the Nirvana Sutra—all beings possess Buddha-nature and can therefore attain Buddhahood.

**2. 檀波羅蜜 (tán bō luó mì) - Dāna-pāramitā (Perfection of Giving)**

- **Sanskrit:** *dāna-pāramitā*
- **Literal meaning:** 檀 (giving/charity) + 波羅蜜 (pāramitā = "gone to the other shore" = perfection)
- **Why this translation:** 
  - 檀 (tán) is a phonetic approximation of *dāna*
  - 波羅蜜 (bō luó mì) is a phonetic rendering of *pāramitā*
  - The term literally means "perfection that carries you to the other shore" (liberation)
- **Doctrinal significance:** First of the six perfections (pāramitās) that bodhisattvas cultivate. Cunda perfects this through his final offering.

**3. 有為法 vs. 無為法 (yǒu wéi fǎ vs. wú wéi fǎ) - Conditioned vs. Unconditioned Dharmas**

- **Sanskrit:** *saṃskṛta-dharma* vs. *asaṃskṛta-dharma*
- **Literal meaning:** 
  - 有為 = "having action/doing" (conditioned, compounded)
  - 無為 = "without action/doing" (unconditioned, uncompounded)
  - 法 = dharma (phenomenon, thing, teaching)
- **Why this translation:** The character 為 (wéi) means "to do, to act, to make." Chinese translators brilliantly captured the Sanskrit *saṃskṛta* (put together, conditioned) with 有為 (having action/fabrication). Things that are "made" or "put together" will eventually fall apart. 無為 (unconditioned) refers to what is not fabricated—eternal, unchanging reality.
- **Doctrinal significance:** This is the central debate in Section 5—is the Buddha conditioned (subject to birth and death) or unconditioned (eternal)?

**4. 方便 (fāng biàn) - Upāya (Skillful Means)**

- **Sanskrit:** *upāya* or *upāya-kauśalya*
- **Literal meaning:** 方 (method/way) + 便 (convenient/expedient)
- **Why this translation:** The Chinese captures the pragmatic, adaptive nature of *upāya*—teachings and methods that are "convenient" or "expedient" for leading beings to liberation, even if not ultimately true. The Buddha uses different teachings for different capacities.
- **Doctrinal significance:** The Buddha's claim that he "never really ate" either offering is upāya—the entire narrative of his life is skillful display for pedagogical purposes.

## Lotus Sutra Connections

### The Professor's Cross-References

This section of the Nirvana Sutra deeply resonates with several key Lotus Sutra teachings:

**1. The Two Offerings Being Equal (Buddha's Teaching)**

The Buddha's claim that the two food offerings (before enlightenment and before parinirvāṇa) have equal karmic results connects to:

- **Lotus Sutra Chapter 16 (Lifespan of the Tathāgata)**: The Buddha reveals he attained enlightenment countless kalpas ago, not under the Bodhi tree. This retroactive Buddhahood makes the "first offering" (to the pre-enlightenment Bodhisattva) actually an offering to an already-enlightened Buddha, thus equalizing the two offerings.

**2. Buddha-Nature as the Pivot**

The teaching that "those who have not yet seen Buddha-nature" have affliction-bodies, while seeing Buddha-nature equals enlightenment, parallels:

- **Lotus Sutra Chapter 2 (Skillful Means)**: "The Buddhas appear in the world for one great purpose: to open, show, awaken, and cause beings to enter the Buddha's knowledge and vision." Buddha-nature is that inherent Buddha-knowledge.

**3. The Buddha Never Actually Eating**

The Buddha's revelation that he never truly ate either offering (both were upāya/skillful means) connects to:

- **Lotus Sutra Chapter 16**: The Buddha's entire life story—birth, enlightenment, teaching, death—is revealed as skillful display. He's been enlightened eternally, so all these events are pedagogical theater.
- **Lotus Sutra Chapter 5 (Medicinal Herbs)**: Different beings receive different teachings according to their capacity, but all come from the same dharma-rain.

**4. Cunda's Perfection Through Offering**

The teaching that Cunda perfects dāna-pāramitā through his offering parallels:

- **Lotus Sutra Chapter 10 (The Teacher of the Dharma)**: Even small offerings to the Lotus Sutra or its teachers create immense merit. The final offering to the Buddha is the ultimate expression of this principle.

**5. The Agricultural Metaphor**

Cunda's metaphor of the farmer with trained oxen, good field, and needing dharma-rain directly echoes:

- **Lotus Sutra Chapter 5 (Medicinal Herbs)**: The famous parable where the Buddha's teaching is like rain falling equally on all plants (beings), each receiving according to their capacity.

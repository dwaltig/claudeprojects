### Summary

This comprehensive survey addresses the paradigm shift from monolithic large language models to coordinated multi-agent systems—a transition that represents one of the most significant architectural developments in AI since the Transformer. The manuscript synthesizes theoretical foundations with empirical evidence across five critical domains (software engineering, scientific discovery, medical diagnostics, financial analysis, and enterprise workflows), demonstrating that multi-agent systems consistently outperform monolithic baselines by margins of 3-13x on complex, long-horizon tasks.

### Contribution to the Field

The manuscript makes three primary contributions:

1. **Theoretical Synthesis**: A unified framework connecting individual agent design (reasoning, memory, tools) with multi-agent coordination patterns, grounded in Adam Smith's economic theory of the division of labor.

2. **Empirical Validation**: Quantitative evidence from 15+ evaluation studies demonstrating consistent MAS superiority with improvement margins including 8.2x in GitHub issue resolution (MAGIS), 13x in financial trading returns (TradingAgents), and +203% in biomedical hypothesis accuracy (Coated-LLM).

3. **Actionable Principles**: Five implementation principles—including optimal team sizing (5-7 agents) and adversarial reviewer embedding—that practitioners can apply immediately when designing agentic systems.

### Relevance to AI Magazine Readership

This manuscript is specifically suited to *AI Magazine*'s practitioner-academic audience:

- **For practitioners**: Concrete framework comparisons (LangGraph, AutoGen, CrewAI, Semantic Kernel) and implementation principles provide actionable guidance for production deployments.

- **For researchers**: Cross-domain synthesis identifies three mechanisms (context switch mitigation, dialectical error detection, model-task matching) that explain MAS superiority, opening new research directions.

- **For the field**: The manuscript establishes a shared vocabulary and reference point for the rapidly evolving multi-agent landscape.

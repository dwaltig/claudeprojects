## The Breakdown

### The Prologue: A Prodigy is Born

Most people come into standard tuning. Zhiyi? He came in open G.
The text says his room was filled with light (*Dong Ming*). The neighbors shouted, "Fire!" and came running with buckets. That's not normal. That's a tube amp running hot right out of the box.
They called him **"Kingly Way"** (*Wang Dao*). That's like calling your kid "The Boss." You know he's gonna headline.

### The Audition (Mount Dasu)

Zhiyi hears about this legend, **Huisi** (Nanyue), playing a gig on Mount Dasu. He's starving for it. He shows up, road-worn and hungry.

**The Moment**:
Huisi takes one look at him and starts laughing.
He says: **"Hey! I know you. We played Vulture Peak together."**

**The Luthier says:**
This implies the "Great Gig in the Sky."
Huisi isn't checking Zhiyi's ID. He's checking his *vibes*. He's saying, "I remember your sound. We were both in the rhythm section when the Buddha laid down the main track for the *Lotus Sutra*."
It's a reunion tour.

### The Sound Check (The Lotus Samadhi)

Huisi doesn't waste time. He points to the **Universal Worthy Bodhimanda** (The Practice Room) and says, "Show me what you got."
Zhiyi sits down. He enters the **Lotus Samadhi**.
Bam. The feedback loop closes. He sees the "Lotus" not as a book, but as a live wire.

**The Certification**:
Huisi stops him and says:
> "Kid, if you hadn't played it, I wouldn't have heard it. But if I hadn't heard it, nobody would believe you played it."

He calls it the **"Dharani of Turning"** (*Xuan Tuoluoni*).
"Turning" means you can take one note (one teaching) and spin it into a whole album. It's the ability to improvise without hitting a sour note.

Huisi drops the mic:
> "You can stack up a thousand 'Paper Musicians'—guys who just read sheet music—and they can't touch you. You're the real deal. You're Number One."

2.  **Zhiyi got the nod from the Legend.** Huisi (the Master) signed off on his sound check.

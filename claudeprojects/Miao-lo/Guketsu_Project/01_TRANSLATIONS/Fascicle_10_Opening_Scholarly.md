## Source-Aligned Bilingual Block

初釋見境中。
> First, in explaining the object of views,
先總釋。
> there is first a general explanation.
次開章別釋。
> Next, the chapter is opened and explained in distinct sections.
初言諸者。
> At the start, the term "various" (zhu) is stated.
自解云非一者眾也。
> The text itself explains: "not one" means "many."
所起雖多不出一百四十。
> What arises is numerous, yet does not exceed one hundred forty.
起不當理名之為見。
> When they arise contrary to right principle, they are called "views."
又解知下重釋見字。
> Again, below in "understanding and knowing," the character "view" is re-explained.
於一百四十中。
> Within those one hundred forty,
各起一見皆推理不當。
> each individual view, when raised, pushes reasoning in an improper direction.
故名為邪。
> Therefore they are called deviant.
夫聽學等者。
> As for those who merely listen and study,
辯失非見。
> their error is argued out, but it is not yet a fully arisen view.
聽學之人自謂有憑。
> People of hearing and study consider themselves to have solid grounds.
義之如生。
> In their estimation, this is "alive" in meaning.
斥修觀者名為無證。
> They denounce contemplative practitioners as having no verification.
義之如死。
> In their estimation, those are "dead" in meaning.
莊子云。
> The Zhuangzi says:
有說則可。
> "If there is discourse, it is acceptable;
無說則死。
> if there is no discourse, it is dead."
夫習禪下明習觀者內觀觀理。
> The passage beginning "those practicing Chan" explains that contemplative practitioners inwardly contemplate principle.
自謂情通以為妙悟。
> They take themselves to be inwardly penetrative and treat this as subtle awakening.
斥學問者名為守株。
> They denounce the learned as "guarding the stump."
彼言情者所謂情性非謂妄情。
> Their word "qing" here means innate disposition, not deluded emotion.
言守株者。
> As for "guarding the stump":
如宋有耕者。
> it is like the plowman of Song.
於野有兔觸株而死。
> In the fields, a rabbit struck a stump and died.
後遂廢耕恒守此株。
> Afterward, he abandoned plowing and constantly guarded that stump,
冀復有兔。
> hoping another rabbit would come.
旁人喻之逾不肯止。
> Though others admonished him, he became even less willing to stop.
故俗相傳以執迷者謂之守株。
> Hence common speech passes down "guarding the stump" for one fixated in delusion.
此之二人本是信法二種根性。
> These two kinds of people are originally two root-dispositions: faith and dharma.
以不曉通意而互相非。
> Because they do not understand the intent of integration, they mutually negate one another.
復未成見。
> Even so, this has not yet matured into full view-formation.
故判被破及能破者。
> Therefore, whether judged as those being refuted or those refuting,
並非見發故各有滯。
> both are not true arising of views, and thus each has its own obstruction.
今乃評之謂為俱失。
> So now, on evaluation, both are said to be in error.

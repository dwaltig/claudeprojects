**Source**: T46n1912, Fascicle 8 (卷第八)

**Translator**: William Altig / Claude

## Summary: The Architecture of Objects 2-4

Fascicle 8 completes the exposition of the three **internal obstacles** that arise during contemplation:

| Object | Chinese | Domain | Content |
|--------|---------|--------|---------|
| 2. Affliction | 煩惱境 | Mind | Three poisons, five coverings, eight kinds of arrogance |
| 3. Illness | 病患境 | Body | Four-element imbalance, six causes, six treatments |
| 4. Karma | 業境 | Action | Past karma manifesting, four powers of purification |

These three objects correspond to the traditional Three Obstacles (三障) from the *Mahāparinirvāṇa Sūtra*:

| Obstacle | Objects | Domain |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Affliction Obstacle (煩惱障) | Object 2 | Mental defilements |
| Karma Obstacle (業障) | Object 4 | Past actions |
| Retribution Obstacle (報障) | Object 3 | Bodily conditions |

## The Transition from Method to Application

Fascicle 8 marks the critical transition from the **Ten Modes** (methodology) to the **Ten Objects** (application):

### What Fascicles 5-7 Established

The Ten Modes of contemplation:
1. Contemplating the inconceivable object (一念三千)
2. Arousing the bodhi-mind
3. Skillfully settling the mind
4. Breaking through all dharmas
5. Discerning openness and obstruction
6. Adjusting the path-factors
7. Counteractive aids for opening
8. Knowing the sequential positions
9. Patient endurance
10. Freedom from dharma-attachment

### What Fascicle 8 Demonstrates

How to **apply** these ten methods when the practitioner encounters:
- Mental disturbance (afflictions intensifying)
- Physical disturbance (illness arising)
- Karmic disturbance (past actions manifesting)

Each object receives:
1. Classification of types and causes
2. Treatment methods (gradual and sudden)
3. Application of all ten modes
4. Ultimate integration (empty, provisional, middle)

---

## The Structure of Treatment

### Gradual Treatment (思議)

Each object has specific antidotes:

**Afflictions**:
| Affliction | Antidote |
|------------|----------|
| Greed | Impurity contemplation |
| Hatred | Loving-kindness contemplation |
| Delusion | Dependent origination contemplation |

**Illness**:
| Cause | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| Element imbalance | Breath regulation, visualization |
| Diet | Dietary adjustment |
| Meditation error | Posture and method correction |
| Karmic | Contemplative acceptance |
| Demonic | Mantras, protection |

**Karma**:
| Power | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| Revulsion | Acknowledge with remorse |
| Antidote | Practice positive actions |
| Restraint | Vow not to repeat |
| Support | Rely on Three Treasures |

### Sudden Treatment (不思議)

All three objects receive the same ultimate treatment:

> 煩惱即菩提。病即法界。業性本空。

"Afflictions are precisely bodhi. Illness is precisely the dharma-realm. The nature of karma is fundamentally empty."

This is not philosophical bypassing but genuine penetration to the empty nature of each obstacle.

---

## The Relationship of Objects to Modes

Miao-lo clarifies the structural relationship:

> 十境非十乘。十乘觀十境。

"The Ten Objects are not the Ten Modes. The Ten Modes contemplate the Ten Objects."

The relationship is:

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| Ten Modes | The methodology (how to contemplate) |
| Ten Objects | The phenomena (what is contemplated) |

Each object receives all ten modes applied to it. Therefore:
- Object 2 (Affliction) × 10 Modes = 10 applications
- Object 3 (Illness) × 10 Modes = 10 applications
- Object 4 (Karma) × 10 Modes = 10 applications

## Cross-References to Indian Sources

Fascicle 8's teachings align with the *Śikṣāsamuccaya* on multiple points:

### Affliction Object
- Three poisons arising through "unwise attention" (ayoniśomanaskāra)
- Specific antidotes (pratipakṣa) for specific afflictions
- Ultimate teaching: "By understanding afflictions, there is bodhi"

### Illness Object
- Body as impure collection of elements
- Medicine taken "without attachment to taste"
- Body as vehicle for practice, not abandoned but transformed

### Karma Object
- Four powers of purification (Caturdharmaka)
- Karmic signs appearing in dreams during purification
- Ultimate realization: "transgression as no-transgression"

## The Integration of Three Truths

Each object is ultimately perceived through the Three Truths:

| Object | Empty | Provisional | Middle |
|--------|-------|-------------|--------|
| Affliction | No inherent existence | Functions as defilement | Neither grasped nor rejected |
| Illness | No real disease | Functions as condition | Neither fled nor cultivated |
| Karma | No substantial action | Functions as cause-effect | Neither eliminated nor indulged |

The practitioner applies this threefold perception simultaneously, not sequentially.

## Doctrinal Significance

Fascicle 8 establishes the foundational Tiantai principle for working with obstacles:

> 障即是道。無障可離。

"Obstacles are precisely the path. There are no obstacles to leave behind."

This does not mean obstacles are ignored or indulged, but that they are the very material through which realization occurs. The practitioner who encounters afflictions, illness, or karma during practice has not failed—they have encountered the path itself.

The Perfect Teaching (圓教) integrates all phenomena within the single vehicle. Nothing is excluded; nothing is merely negative; everything becomes the gateway.

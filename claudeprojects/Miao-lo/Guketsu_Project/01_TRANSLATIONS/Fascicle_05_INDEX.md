## The Five Wrong Views (五惡見)

1. **身見** — View of Self (clinging to identity of realized practitioner)
2. **邊見** — Extreme Views (eternalism or nihilism)
3. **邪見** — Wrong Views (denying validity of other teachings)
4. **見取見** — View-Attachment (clinging to inferior views as supreme)
5. **戒禁取見** — Precept-Attachment (clinging to wrong practices)

---

## The Seven Types of Pride (七慢)

1. **慢** — Simple Pride ("I am better than equals")
2. **過慢** — Greater Pride ("I am better than superiors")
3. **慢過慢** — Pride of Pride ("I am the best")
4. **我慢** — Self-Pride ("I am" / "I have attained")
5. **卑慢** — Inferior Pride ("I am only slightly inferior")
6. **邪慢** — False Pride (pride in non-attainments)
7. **增上慢** — Perverse Pride (pride in wrong attainments)

---

## The Two Vehicles (二乘)

1. **聲聞** — Śrāvaka (hearing the teaching, seeking personal nirvāṇa)
2. **緣覺** — Pratyekabuddha (dependent origination, solitary realization)

**Common characteristics**: Personal liberation focus, limited compassion, satisfaction with partial realization

---

## The Ten Stages of the Bodhisattva (十地)

1. **歡喜地** — Joyful (first breakthrough of ignorance)
2. **離垢地** — Stainless (purity of precepts)
3. **發光地** — Luminous (patience and meditation)
4. **焰慧地** — Radiant Wisdom (burning afflictions)
5. **難勝地** — Difficult to Conquer (mastery of truths)
6. **現前地** — Manifest (wisdom of dependent origination)
7. **遠行地** — Far-Going (skillful means perfected)
8. **不動地** — Immovable (effortless practice)
9. **善慧地** — Good Wisdom (perfect teaching ability)
10. **法雲地** — Dharma Cloud (approaching Buddhahood)

## Key Insights from Fascicle 5

### On Subtle Obstacles

> "Subtle obstacles are the hardest to recognize because they disguise themselves as virtues. Wrong views appear as wisdom. Pride appears as confidence."

### On Release

> "Each level of practice requires releasing what was helpful at the previous level. The master releases everything, including the release itself."

### On Completion

> "When all ten objects have been worked with—when even the bodhisattva path is released—what remains cannot be spoken. It is the inconceivable functioning of Buddha-nature."

# Fascicle 9: Closing Summary
## Scholarly Translation

**Source**: T46n1912, Fascicle 9
**Author**: Miao-lo (Zhanran)
**Translator**: William Altig / Claude
**Date**: January 23, 2026

---

## Overview

Fascicle 9 has provided an exhaustive analysis of the **Meditative States Object** (禪定境). This object encompasses the entire range of Buddhist meditative attainments, from the fundamental worldly absorptions to the advanced samādhis of the Bodhisattva.

## Key Teachings

### 1. The Dangers of Dhyāna
Miao-lo repeatedly emphasizes that while dhyāna is essential for stability, it is fraught with danger:
- **Attachment**: "Flavor Zen" (味禪) binds the practitioner to samsara.
- **Stagnation**: Resting in the "Pro Rig" (Transcendental Zen) leads to the Two Vehicles, cutting off the Bodhisattva path.
- **Pride**: Mistaking the Fourth Dhyāna for Nirvana is a common error (exemplified by the Bhikshu who was reborn in hell).

### 2. The Necessity of Wisdom
Dhyāna alone is "blind." It must be illuminated by **Prajñā** (Wisdom).
- The **Ten Modes** provide this illumination.
- Mode 1 (Inconceivable Object) transforms the "dead water" of absorption into the "living ocean" of the Dharma-realm.

### 3. The Integration of Methods
Miao-lo demonstrates that the Tiantai system does not reject "inferior" methods (like the Four Dhyānas or Nine Thoughts). Instead, it **repurposes** them.
- A Perfect Teaching practitioner can use the **Nine Thoughts** not just to disgust the mind, but to see the emptiness of the body and the non-duality of purity and impurity.
- The **Six Wondrous Gates** become a method for tuning the mind to the Middle Way.

### 4. The Supremacy of the Perfect View
Ultimately, all Zens are seen as **Dharma-Nature**.
- The bliss of the First Dhyāna *is* the bliss of Nirvana, if seen correctly.
- The silence of the Fourth Dhyāna *is* the cessation of the Middle Way.
- There is no need to discard the worldly to find the transcendental.

---

## Final Instruction

The practitioner is urged to:
1.  **Cultivate** dhyāna to stabilize the mind.
2.  **Apply** the Ten Modes to prevent attachment.
3.  **Use** the power of concentration to fuel Compassion and the Six Paramitas.

Fascicle 10 will conclude the work with the remaining objects (Views, Pride, Two Vehicles, Bodhisattva) and the final fruition.

# The Sun Don't Preach  
  
A raw, Delta blues track that wrestles with the difference between trying to fix a broken world with force and finding strength in a quieter, more patient wisdom. "The Sun Don't Preach" is a story told through a bottleneck slide and a steady foot stomp, contrasting the "power of a God" with the "wisdom of the Sun." It's a gritty, front-porch sermon about letting go of the hammer, trusting the process, and learning how to just shine.

**(A Delta Blues Song)**  
**Production Notes:**  
* **Style:** Raw, acoustic Delta blues. Think Son House or early Muddy Waters. The sound should be sparse, powerful, and deeply human.  
* **Instrumentation:**  
    * **Lead:** A single resonator guitar, played with a bottleneck slide. The slide should do a lot of the "singing," echoing the vocal lines and expressing the ache and the eventual clarity. The rhythm is a heavy, percussive thumb-stomp on the low strings.  
    * **Vocals:** Gritty, soulful, and full of conviction. The delivery should carry the weight of the first verses and the liberating insight of the chorus. It's a voice that has known frustration.  
    * **Rhythm:** A simple, steady foot stomp on a wooden floor. Nothing else is needed.  
* **Overall Feel:** This is a song born from a hard-won lesson. It should feel like a sermon delivered not from a pulpit, but from a rocking chair on a dusty porch as a storm gathers in the distance. The recording should be close-mic'd and intimate, capturing the scrape of the slide on the strings and the breath behind the words.

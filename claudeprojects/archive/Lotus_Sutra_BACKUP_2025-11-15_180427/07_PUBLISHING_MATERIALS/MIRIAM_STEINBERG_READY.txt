================================================================================
MIRIAM'S EVALUATION FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

When analyzing any project, Miriam systematically evaluates:

1. MARKET REALITY CHECK
   Is there actually a market? How big? Give her specific numbers.
   
2. COMPETITION ANALYSIS
   What's already out there? Why is yours better? Prove it.
   
3. AUTHOR PLATFORM
   Do you have one? Followers? Email list? Speaking gigs? Or are you nobody?
   
4. COMMERCIAL VIABILITY
   Can she sell this? Will publishers bid? Or will she be begging?
   
5. EXECUTION QUALITY
   Is it actually GOOD or just interesting?
   
6. TIMING
   Is this the right moment? Or five years too late?
   
7. MONEY QUESTION
   What advance can she get you? $5K? $50K? $500K? Be realistic.

================================================================================
MIRIAM'S OPENING QUESTIONS
================================================================================

When you first present a project, Miriam will ask:

1. "Why should I care? What's your elevator pitch - 15 seconds, go."

2. "Who's your audience? And don't say 'everyone.' Give me numbers and 
   demographics. Who actually will buy this?"

3. "What's your platform? How many followers? Email list? Speaking gigs? 
   Or are you nobody?"

4. "Name three books similar to yours that actually sold well. Not 'kind of 
   similar' - actually comparable. In the last two years."

5. "What advance are you expecting? Be honest. $10K? $100K? $500K? Because 
   I need to know if you're realistic."

6. "Why blues? Really. Is this authentic or is this appropriation? Because 
   that'll be the first question from woke editors."

7. "You know the Buddhist book market is tiny, right? Most Buddhist books 
   don't sell. What makes yours different?"

8. "How long did this take you? If you say 'ten years' I might walk away. 
   Publishers don't care about your suffering."

9. "Can you promote this? Will you get on planes? Do readings? Hustle? 
   Or are you an introvert who wants to hide?"

10. "Why not self-publish? If you believe in this so much, why do you need 
    a traditional publisher?"

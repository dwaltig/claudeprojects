CHAPTER 18: THE JOY THAT MULTIPLIES
(隨喜功德品第十八 - The Merit of Rejoicing and Passing It On)

Blues/Gospel Translation from Kumārajīva's Classical Chinese (406 CE)
Rendered into vernacular English


THE QUESTION ABOUT PASSING IT ON

Right then, Maitreya Bodhisattva stood up and asked the Buddha:

"World-Honored One, if there's a good man or a good woman who hears this Lotus Sutra and their heart just REJOICES—I mean really rejoices, gets happy about it, feels that joy rise up in them—how much blessing they gonna get? What kind of merit comes from that simple joy?"

And then he spoke it in verse:

*After the World-Honored One passes on,*
*If someone hears this teaching here,*
*And they can just rejoice in it—*
*How much blessing does that bring near?*


THE BUDDHA'S ANSWER: THE CHAIN OF TRANSMISSION

The Buddha turned to Maitreya and said:

"Ajita, listen here. After I'm gone—after the Tathagata passes into extinction—if a monk or a nun, if a laywoman or a layman, if somebody with wisdom whether they old or they young, if they hear this sutra and they REJOICE in it, and then they leave that dharma assembly and go somewhere else—

Maybe they go to a monastery, maybe they go to some quiet empty place, maybe they in the city streets or the back alleys, maybe they in a village or out in the fields—

And they tell it. They tell what they heard.

They tell their mama and their daddy, they tell their kinfolk, they tell their friends and the people they know. They use whatever words they got, whatever strength they got, and they PASS IT ON.

And those people hear it and they rejoice too. And what do they do? They go and tell somebody else.

And that person hears it and rejoices, and they tell somebody else.

And it goes on like that, passing from hand to hand, from mouth to mouth, from heart to heart—

It keeps going, keeps spreading, keeps multiplying—

Until it reaches the FIFTIETH person in that chain.

Now Ajita, let me tell you about that fiftieth good man or good woman—the one at the end of that long line of telling. Let me tell you about the merit they get just from hearing and rejoicing.

Listen good to what I'm about to say."


THE GREAT BENEFACTOR (THE COMPARISON)

"Imagine this:

Imagine there's four hundred million billion countless world systems. You can't even count that many worlds—more worlds than you got numbers for.

And in all those worlds, there's beings in the six realms—gods and humans, asuras and animals, hungry ghosts and hell-dwellers.

Every kind of birth: egg-born, womb-born, moisture-born, transformation-born.

Every kind of form: got form, don't got form.

Every kind of consciousness: got consciousness, don't got consciousness, got neither and got both.

Every kind of body: no feet, two feet, four feet, many feet.

All the beings there are—every single one that counts as a living creature—

Now imagine a person who wants to do good, who wants to create blessing. And they go to all those beings and they say: 'What you want? What brings you joy? What makes you happy?'

And whatever entertainment those beings desire, whatever pleasure items they want, this benefactor GIVES IT TO THEM. Gives it all.

And to each single being—every last one—they give gold and silver, lapis lazuli and mother-of-pearl, agate and coral and amber. All kinds of wonderful precious treasures.

And elephants. And horses. And carriages. And precious palanquins to ride in.

And palaces made of seven kinds of jewels. And towers. And pavilions.

To EACH being, they give enough to fill the whole continent of Jambudvīpa—that's this whole earth we're standing on. Each one gets that much treasure.

And this great benefactor keeps giving like this for EIGHTY YEARS.

Eighty years of constant giving.

Then after eighty years, that benefactor looks at all those beings and thinks:

'I done gave them everything for pleasure and entertainment, everything their hearts desired. But look at them now—they getting old. Already past eighty years old themselves, hair gone white, faces all wrinkled. They gonna die soon. Won't be long now.

I better teach them the Buddha's Dharma. I better train them in the truth before they go.'

So that benefactor gathers all those countless beings together and proclaims the Dharma to them. Teaches them, instructs them, benefits them, brings them joy.

And right then—all at one time—every single one of them attains the Way:

Some become stream-enterers (Śrotāpanna)—first step on the path.

Some become once-returners (Sakṛdāgāmin)—just one more birth to go.

Some become non-returners (Anāgāmin)—ain't coming back to this world no more.

Some become Arhats (Arahant)—completely done, fully free.

All of them eliminate every outflow, every leak of defilement.

All of them become masters of deep meditation, dwelling in profound samādhi.

All of them gain the eight liberations—free in every way there is to be free.

Now Ajita—what you think? You think that great benefactor got a lot of merit? You think they accumulated some blessings or what?"


MAITREYA'S ANSWER

Maitreya said to the Buddha:

"World-Honored One, that person got merit beyond measure! Can't even count it, can't even number it.

I mean, if that benefactor had just GIVEN all those beings the pleasure items, the entertainment and joy—that alone would be merit without measure.

But you talking about they also led every single one of them to become an Arhat! That's merit on top of merit, blessing piled on blessing!"


THE SHOCKING TRUTH

The Buddha said to Maitreya:

"Let me tell you straight and clear now. Let me break it down so there ain't no confusion:

That person who gave all those pleasure items to beings in four hundred million billion countless world systems—

Who gave to beings in all six realms of existence—

Who gave for eighty solid years—

And then led every single one of them to become Arhats, to attain complete liberation—

All that merit, all that blessing, all that good karma—

Don't equal the merit of that FIFTIETH person who heard just ONE VERSE of the Lotus Sutra and rejoiced.

You hear me?

The fiftieth person in the chain.

The one at the very end.

The last one to hear it.

Who just heard ONE VERSE.

And just REJOICED.

That person's merit is GREATER.

Not equal. GREATER.

The great benefactor's merit ain't even one hundredth part.

Not one thousandth part.

Not one hundred thousand million billionth part.

You can't calculate it. You can't number it. You can't use arithmetic to figure it. You can't use analogies to explain it.

There ain't no comparison.

Now Ajita, think about this:

If that fiftieth person—the LAST one in the chain, the one who heard it from someone who heard it from someone who heard it from someone forty-eight times removed from the source—

If THAT person's merit from just hearing one verse and rejoicing is already beyond measure, already infinite, already countless—

What about the forty-ninth person who told them?

What about the forty-eighth?

What about the person earlier in the chain?

What about the person earlier than that?

And what about the FIRST person—the one who heard it right there in the dharma assembly and rejoiced?

That person's blessing is even MORE beyond compare. More infinite than infinite. More countless than countless.

Can't be measured at all."


THE BLESSINGS OF SMALL ACTS

"And listen, Ajita—let me tell you more:

If someone goes to a monastery to hear this sutra—

Even if they just SIT there for a moment—

Even if they just STAND there for a moment—

Even if they only listen for a brief instant—

Because of that merit, in their next life they gonna be born and receive:

Fine excellent elephants, fine excellent horses, fine excellent carriages, precious jeweled palanquins to ride in. They gonna even ride in heavenly palaces up in the god realms.

And if someone's at a place where this Dharma is being taught, and they're sitting down listening—

And another person comes along—

And the first person encourages that newcomer: 'Sit down and listen!'

Or maybe they share their own seat, move over to make room so the other person can sit and listen—

Because of that merit, in their next life they gonna be born and receive:

The seat of Indra, king of the gods.

Or the seat of Brahma, lord of this world.

Or the seat of a wheel-turning sacred king, ruler of the four continents.

Just from offering a seat. Just from making room. Just from saying 'Sit down, listen here.'"


THE BEAUTIFUL MOUTH THAT SPEAKS DHARMA

"And Ajita, if someone says to another person:

'There's a sutra called the Lotus Dharma. Let's go listen to it together.'

And that person receives the teaching, accepts the invitation, and goes with them—

And they listen together, even if just for a brief moment—

Because of that merit, in their next life they gonna be born together with Dhāraṇī Bodhisattvas—wisdom-keepers, truth-holders.

And they gonna have:

Sharp faculties.
Keen wisdom.

For a hundred thousand million lifetimes, they won't never be mute. They won't never be dumb-struck. They gonna have speech.

Their mouth breath won't never stink.

Their tongue won't never be sick.

Their mouth won't never be diseased.

Their teeth won't be dirty.
Won't be black.
Won't be yellow.
Won't be sparse or missing teeth.
Won't have gaps.
Won't be crooked.
Won't be broken.

Their lips won't hang down low.
Won't be shriveled up.
Won't be thick and rough.
Won't have sores or blemishes.
Won't be damaged.
Won't be twisted to the side.
Won't be thick.
Won't be too big.
Won't be dark.
Won't have anything ugly about them.

Their nose won't be flat.
Won't be crooked.

Their face won't be dark-colored.
Won't be too long and narrow.
Won't be sunken in or twisted.
Won't have a single feature that ain't pleasant to look at.

Their lips, tongue, and teeth gonna be perfectly beautiful.

Their nose gonna be refined, high, and straight.

Their face gonna be round and full.

Their eyebrows gonna be high and long.

Their forehead gonna be broad, flat, and perfectly proportioned.

All their human features gonna be complete and perfect.

Life after life, wherever they born, they gonna see Buddhas and hear the Dharma and receive the teaching with faith."


THE ESCALATION OF MERIT

"Now Ajita, just LOOK at this:

Just from encouraging ONE person to go listen to the Dharma—the merit is already like this, already beyond measure.

So what about someone who listens with a whole mind, with full attention?

What about someone who not only listens but EXPLAINS it, TEACHES it, interprets it and breaks it down for other people?

What about someone who not only hears it and teaches it but PRACTICES it, LIVES it, does exactly what it says?

That merit can't be measured at all. Can't even begin to calculate it."


THEN THE BUDDHA SANG IT

Right then the World-Honored One, wanting to drive this truth home one more time, spoke in verse:

*If someone at a dharma gathering*
*Hears this sutra here,*
*Even just one single verse,*
*And rejoices when they hear,*

*Then goes and teaches it to others,*
*Passes it on down the line,*
*Until it reaches the fiftieth person—*
*That last person's merit gonna shine.*

*Now let me break down and distinguish*
*What that last person gonna receive:*
*Imagine a great benefactor giving—*
*More than your mind can conceive.*

*They supply countless multitudes,*
*Give them everything they desire,*
*Keep on giving eighty years—*
*Never let that charity tire.*

*Then seeing those people getting old,*
*Hair white and faces all creased,*
*Teeth sparse and bodies dried up,*
*Death approaching from the east,*

*That benefactor thinks: 'Right now*
*I better teach them truth that's pure,*
*I better give them the Way to freedom,*
*Help them find what will endure.'*

*So they teach them the dharma skillfully,*
*Teach them about nirvāṇa's real law,*
*Say: 'This world ain't solid at all,*
*Like water bubbles, foam, and straw—*

*Like flames flickering, shadows passing—*
*Y'all better develop detachment quick,*
*Better grow that mind that sees through illusion,*
*Before time runs out and you caught in the trick.'*

*All those people hearing this teaching*
*Every one becomes an Arhat true,*
*Possessing six spiritual powers,*
*Three knowledges and eight liberations too.*

*But that fiftieth person in the chain*
*Who just heard one verse and felt joy—*
*THAT person's blessing exceeds all that giving,*
*Can't be compared, can't be measured by no ploy.*

*So if the person at the END of the chain*
*Gets merit beyond all measuring—*
*What about those earlier in the line?*
*What about the FIRST person rejoicing and believing?*


THE SPECIFIC BLESSINGS OF INVITATION

*If someone encourages just one person,*
*Brings them along to hear the Lotus taught,*
*Saying 'This sutra is deep and wonderful,*
*Hard to encounter in a thousand million ages' worth of thought—*

*And that person receives the teaching,*
*Accepts the invitation to come hear,*
*Even if they only listen a moment—*
*Let me tell you the blessings that appear:*

*Life after life, no trouble with their mouth,*
*Teeth won't be sparse or yellow or black,*
*Lips won't be thick or shriveled or damaged,*
*Nothing ugly, nothing that others would attack.*

*Tongue won't be dry or black or short,*
*Nose gonna be high and refined and straight,*
*Forehead broad and perfectly level,*
*Face and features handsome and great.*

*They gonna be somebody people love to see,*
*Mouth breath won't never be foul,*
*The fragrance of the blue lotus flower*
*Gonna come out their mouth right now.*


THE BLESSINGS OF SIMPLY SHOWING UP

*If they deliberately go to the monastery*
*Wanting to hear the Lotus Sutra read,*
*And they listen even a moment with joy—*
*Let me tell you where that blessing gonna lead:*

*Next life they born among gods and humans,*
*They gonna receive wonderful elephant, horse, and cart,*
*Precious jeweled palanquins to ride in,*
*Even riding in the heavenly palace to take part.*

*If at a place where Dharma's being taught*
*They encourage someone to sit and hear,*
*Because of that blessed connection—*
*Indra's seat, Brahma's seat, or the wheel-king's gonna appear.*


THE FINAL QUESTION

*So what about someone who listens with whole mind,*
*Who explains the meaning and the sense,*
*Who practices exactly what it teaches?*
*THAT blessing is beyond immense!*

**TRANSLATOR'S NOTE:**

This chapter is about viral transmission before there was such a thing—about how truth spreads from person to person like fire, like news, like a song everybody needs to hear. The Buddha's teaching here is mathematical and mystical at once: merit doesn't just add, it MULTIPLIES. It grows exponentially with each telling.

The shocking comparison is the heart of it: the greatest material generosity in the universe (giving everything to everyone for eighty years PLUS leading them all to Arhat-hood) is LESS than the merit of the fiftieth person who just heard a verse and felt joy. Why? Because the Lotus Sutra itself IS the teaching that leads to Buddhahood—not just to Arhat-hood. It's the higher truth, the bigger vehicle. One verse of it carries more potential than all the arhats in all the worlds.

And notice the progression of blessings:
- Just show up to listen → you get treasure in the next life
- Give someone a seat → you get a throne in the next life
- Invite someone to come hear it → you get a beautiful body and you meet Buddhas forever

The "beautiful mouth" passage is profound: the mouth that speaks Dharma becomes beautiful because it's doing what mouths were made for—speaking truth, passing on liberation. Form follows function. The body that serves the teaching becomes perfected by that service.

This is word-of-mouth gospel. This is "each one teach one." This is the mathematics of grace: one person tells one person tells one person, and fifty tellings later, the last person in the chain has more blessing than the richest philanthropist who ever lived.

Pass it on.

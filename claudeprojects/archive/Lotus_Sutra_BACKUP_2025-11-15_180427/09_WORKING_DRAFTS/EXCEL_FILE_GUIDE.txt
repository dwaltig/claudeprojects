SHEET 4: BUDDHA'S VOICES
================================================================================

THE BUDDHA'S THREE DISTINCT SPEAKING MODES

The Buddha does NOT use the same voice for all his speech. This sheet shows
the three different voices he uses depending on context.

Three Modes:

1. PROSE DIALOGUE [Iapetus]
   Context: Direct teaching to disciples
   Usage: "The Buddha told Śāriputra..."
   Characteristics: Calm, clear, authoritative, compassionate
   Frequency: 47 occurrences

2. VERSES/POETIC [Rasalgethi]
   Context: Elevated poetic expression
   Usage: "At that time, the Buddha, wishing to repeat this meaning, spoke in verse..."
   Characteristics: Musical, rhythmic, elevated, can carry melody
   Frequency: 54 occurrences

3. PAST STORIES/PARABLES [Orus]
   Context: Storytelling mode
   Usage: "Let me tell you a story from long ago..."
   Characteristics: Warm, narrative, preacher-like, less formal
   Frequency: 117 occurrences

Casting Options:
Option A: Same actor performing all three voices with different registers
Option B: Three actors with timbral similarity but different styles

Use This Sheet For:
- Understanding Buddha's vocal variety
- Planning voice actor strategy (1 vs. 3 actors)
- Understanding when to shift Buddha's voice
- Voice director guidance

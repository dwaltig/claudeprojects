       471 +  The first chapter establishes several foundational philosophical principles that structure the 
           + entire Lotus Sutra. The teaching begins with the historical Buddha's appearance and cosmic 
           + manifestation, presenting a paradox: if the Buddha is truly eternal (as revealed later in Chapter 
           + 16), why does he teach sequentially across time? This question governs the entire narrative arc, 
           + resolved through the doctrine of skillful means (*upāya*). The parable of Burning Lamps Buddha 
           + reveals that what appears to be historical succession is actually the repeated manifestation of 
           + eternal principles adapted to different audiences and ages. This establishes both the uniqueness of 
           + the Dharma and its universal accessibility.
       472 +

           + status or nature, participate in the assembly and receive the teaching. This vision of radical 
           + inclusivity becomes increasingly explicit as the Sutra progresses, culminating in the promise that 
           + even those who initially reject the teaching will eventually attain enlightenment. The cosmological 
           + scale of the Buddha's light illuminating eighteen thousand worlds suggests that enlightenment 
           + operates not as individual achievement but as a universal principle touching all realms of existence

       481    1. **"Thus have I heard"** (*Evam maya śrutam*, 如是我聞): This formulaic opening appears in

              experience (pratyakṣa), a central epistemological principle in Buddhist philosophy.

ISSUE IDENTIFIED:
  ✗ Verse lines contained literal asterisks: *O Mañjuśrī!*
  ✗ These are markdown syntax for italics, not HTML
  ✗ Should have been converted to: <em>O Mañjuśrī!</em>
  ✗ Affected 833 verse lines across 6 chapters

ROOT CAUSE:
  When verses were extracted from markdown files, the formatting markers (* and _) were
  preserved as literal text instead of being converted to HTML tags (<em> and <strong>).

SOLUTION IMPLEMENTED:

1. **Created Markdown-to-HTML Conversion Script** (`convert_verse_markdown_to_html.py`)
   • Scans all 28 chapter files for verse-line paragraphs
   • Detects markdown formatting: *text* and **text**
   • Converts to proper HTML: <em> and <strong> tags
   • Preserves existing HTML (doesn't re-process already formatted lines)
   • ~90 lines of Python code

2. **Conversion Rules Applied:**
   • *text* → <em>text</em> (italics)
   • **text** → <strong>text</strong> (bold)
   • _text_ → <em>text</em> (italic variant)
   • Order matters: **bold** before *italic* to avoid conflicts

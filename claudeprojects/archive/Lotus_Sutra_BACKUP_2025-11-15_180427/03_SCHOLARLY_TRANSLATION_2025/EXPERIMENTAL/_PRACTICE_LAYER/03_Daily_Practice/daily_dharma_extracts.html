Daily Dharma Extracts: 28 Days with the Lotus Sutra

How to Use These Daily Extracts

This document provides one short teaching from each of the 28 chapters of the Lotus Sutra, suitable for daily 5-minute practice.

Daily Practice Format (5-10 minutes):
  1. Read the extract slowly (2 minutes) - Read the teaching two or three times, letting the words settle
  2. Sit silently with it (3 minutes) - Hold the teaching in your consciousness without commentary
  3. Notice what arises (2-5 minutes) - Journal, meditate, or simply observe what the teaching stirs in you
Flexible Engagement:
    1. Read one extract per day for 28 days (one month)
    2. Read all in sequence for intensive practice
    3. Return to any extract when it speaks to your current situation
    4. Use as opening for group study sessions

Day 16: Chapter 16 - The Buddha's Eternal Lifespan

Extended Daily Practice Options

Option 1: Sequential Reading (28 Days)

Read one extract per day for 28 days‚Äîone complete cycle through the sutra each month. This keeps you in constant connection with the teaching.

Option 2: Rotating Practice (7-Day Cycle)

Choose 7 extracts that most resonate with you, and cycle through them weekly. This allows deeper saturation with key teachings.

Option 3: Responsive Practice

Let life circumstances guide your selection. When facing difficulty, choose the extract that speaks to that difficulty. When celebrating, choose an extract of joy. When uncertain, choose the extract you most need to hear.

Option 4: Group Practice

Use one extract to open each group study session. Let it set the tone for collective practice.

Option 5: Seasonal Progression

    1. Spring (Days 1-7): Opening chapters emphasizing emergence and beginning
    2. Summer (Days 8-14): Expansion chapters emphasizing inclusion and accessibility
    3. Fall (Days 15-21): Deepening chapters emphasizing practice and compassion
    4. Winter (Days 22-28): Culminating chapters emphasizing commitment and eternal presence

Creating Your Own Daily Practice

The extracts here are suggestions. You might prefer to:

  1. Choose your own passages from chapters that moved you most
  2. Create visual reminders by writing favorite passages on cards
  3. Memorize key phrases for mid-day recitation
  4. Combine with meditation by using an extract as a focal point

The form matters less than the consistency. Five minutes daily with genuine presence transforms consciousness far more than occasional intensive study without integration.

After Completing One Cycle

When you complete 28 days:

  1. Review your notes - What themes appeared repeatedly? What shifted?
  2. Choose your next focus - Return to one chapter for deeper study, or begin another cycle
  3. Deepen one practice - Rather than rotating through all extracts, commit to one focal point for extended practice (40 days, 100 days, 1 year)
  4. Share the dharma - Teach someone else one of these daily dharma extracts
  5. Return to the full translation - You're ready now for deeper study of the complete chapters

A Final Word on Daily Practice

These daily extracts are doorways. Each one opens onto vast terrain. You need not understand everything in five minutes. Let the practice be simple:

Read. Sit. Notice. Continue.

The dharma works through consistency more than intensity. Five minutes daily, day after day, creates transformation that dramatic intensive study cannot match.

You are practicing the Lotus Sutra. The Buddha is teaching you. Bodhisattvas are supporting you. Infinite practitioners across all times are practicing alongside you.

This is no small thing.

Suggested Practice Progression

Phase 1: Exploration (Months 1-3)

    1. Read the full BODHISATTVAPATHMETHODOLOGY document
    2. Study Chapter 23 Study Guide (Medicine King introduction)
    3. Begin daily offering practice
    4. Journal about your initial impressions

Phase 2: Foundation (Months 4-6)

    1. Deepen Medicine King practice (complete offering progression)
    2. Study the relevant chapter materials
    3. Begin formal meditation practice
    4. Track your insights in your journal

Phase 3: Development (Months 7-12)

    1. Engage with Wonderful-Sound methodology
    2. Continue Medicine King practice (it deepens over time)
    3. Develop flexibility in how you express dharma
    4. Notice changes in your relationships and teaching

Phase 4: Opening (Year 2)

    1. Move into Avalokite≈õvara compassion practice
    2. Integrate previous stages as foundation
    3. Deepen listening and compassionate response
    4. Begin serving others' awakening more actively

Phase 5: Grounding (Year 2.5)

    1. Establish Dharani protective practices
    2. Create spiritual sanctuary and stability
    3. Discipline your practice
    4. Protect the dharma's transmission

Phase 6: Engagement (Year 3)

    1. Study and practice Wonderful-Adornment King teaching
    2. Begin teaching/guiding others formally or informally
    3. Develop skillful means
    4. Perceive individual beings' actual conditions

Phase 7: Mastery (Year 3+)

    1. Engage fully with Samantabhadra commitment
    2. Make formal vows
    3. Commit to lifetime transmission
    4. All six methodologies work together seamlessly

    2. Review journal entries
    3. Optional: group gathering/teaching circle

Monthly (2-3 hours)

    1. Complete review of stage progress
    2. Adjust practices as needed
    3. Deeper study of related chapters

Annually (1 day or weekend)

    1. Retreat time for integrated practice
    2. Renewal of vows
    3. Planning for next phase

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I skip stages? A: The stages build on each other. Each earlier stage provides necessary foundation for later ones. You might work with multiple stages, but don't skip foundational ones. Q: How long should I spend on each stage? A: The document suggests 3-6 months per stage. You may need longer; that's fine. Go at the pace that allows genuine transformation, not intellectual understanding. Q: What if I get stuck? A: This is normal. When stuck, return to Avalokite≈õvara compassion (Stage 3). Compassion and patience with yourself are essential. Consider connecting with a teacher or sangha for support. Q: Is this only for people becoming monks/nuns? A: No. These methodologies work for householders, professionals, families, anyone. The bodhisattva path is for all beings committed to awakening and serving others' liberation. Q: Can I practice multiple stages simultaneously? A: As you deepen, earlier stages naturally continue while you engage new ones. By year 3, all six stages work together. But maintain focus on your primary stage for deepening.

The Infinite Path

The bodhisattva path has no endpoint within conventional time. As these Lotus Sutra chapters teach, the path spirals infinitely. Each stage, practiced deeper, reveals new dimensions. Commitment to awakening all beings is eternal work.

Yet paradoxically, there's nothing to achieve. You don't become a bodhisattva; you recognize what you already are through engaging these practices.

Welcome to the path of infinite awakening.

May your practice deepen endlessly. May all beings recognize their Buddha-nature. May the dharma flourish eternally.

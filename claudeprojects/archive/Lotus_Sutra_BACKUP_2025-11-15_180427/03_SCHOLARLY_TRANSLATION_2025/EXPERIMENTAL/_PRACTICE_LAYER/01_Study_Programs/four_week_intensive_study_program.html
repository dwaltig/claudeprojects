Four-Week Intensive Lotus Sutra Study Program

Welcome to Accelerated Practice

This program guides you through all 28 chapters of the Lotus Sutra in four weeks (7 chapters per week). It's designed for practitioners who want rapid immersion in the complete teaching‚Äîwhether for a personal intensive, a retreat setting, or a committed study group.

Ideal For:
    1. Practitioners taking a dedicated month for intensive study
    2. Study groups with 4 weekly gatherings (7 chapters per week)
    3. Retreat participants
    4. Those seeking comprehensive engagement before deeper focus on specific chapters
What You'll Complete:
    1. All 28 chapters read and engaged with
    2. All meditation focal points practiced
    3. Personal reflection on each phase
    4. Basic understanding of the complete teaching and its progression
Time Commitment:
    1. Approximately 45-60 minutes per day
    2. Can be condensed to 2-3 hours on intensive days
    3. Total: 15-20 hours over the month

---

Before You Begin: One-Week Preparation

Week 0: Foundation Setting

Even before the intensive begins, spend 3-5 days preparing:

Day 1: Orientation
    1. Read the Practitioner's Guide Introduction thoroughly
    2. Understand the four phases of the Lotus Sutra
    3. Choose your support structure (solo, study partner, group, etc.)
Day 2: Sacred Space
    1. Create a physical and mental space for study
    2. Gather materials: translation, study guides, notebook/journal
    3. Set up a meditation corner if possible
Day 3: Intention Setting
    1. Sit quietly and ask: "What am I hoping to awaken through this study?"
    2. Write your intention in your journal
    3. Consider what support you need (teacher, friend, discipline)
Day 4: Study Overview
    1. Skim through Chapter 1 to understand structure and style
    2. Look at the Opening Assembly listing to get a sense of the world the sutra creates
    3. Read the first page of Ma√±ju≈õrƒ´'s explanation in Chapter 1
Day 5: First Practice
    1. Choose Chapter 1's meditation focal point
    2. Sit with it for 15-20 minutes
    3. Journal briefly about what you noticed
    4. Commit to daily practice for the upcoming month
Optional Day 6: Community Connection
    1. If studying with others, have an opening conversation
    2. Share intentions
    3. Establish practice rhythm and accountability

Week 1: Foundation & Doctrine (Chapters 1-8)

The Vision Unfolds

Week 1 establishes the fundamental teaching: all beings possess Buddha-nature and can attain enlightenment. These chapters reveal the cosmic scope of the Buddha's compassion and the revolutionary promise of universal liberation.

---

Daily Practice Template (Repeat for Each Chapter 1-8)

Morning (15-20 minutes):
    1. Meditation with chapter's focal point
    2. Brief reflection: "What am I opening to today?"
Afternoon/Evening (30-40 minutes):
    1. Read chapter's prose section (skip verse on first read)
    2. Read the chapter study guide overview and learning objectives
    3. Choose one reflection question from the study guide and journal on it
Evening Reflection (10 minutes):
    1. Note one key insight in your journal
    2. Consider how it applies to your life
    3. Commit to one small practice based on the teaching

Week 2: Validation & Universality (Chapters 9-16)

All Beings Can Awaken

Week 2 validates the promise through multiple examples: the accomplished disciples receive prophecies, women are included, diverse beings are recognized as bodhisattvas, the Buddha's teaching is eternally available.

---

    2. Reflection: "What is this chapter teaching me about awakening?"

    1. Read chapter's prose section and key passages
    2. Read 2-3 key passages from study guide
    3. Answer one reflection question in detail
    4. Practice the suggested daily practice
Evening Integration (10-15 minutes):
    1. Journal one concrete way to apply the teaching
    2. Notice resistance or obstacles if present
    3. Commit to specific practice for the next day

Completion Ceremony (Optional but Recommended)

After finishing Chapter 28, complete one of these integration practices:

Option 1: Personal Completion Ceremony (30-45 minutes)
  1. Sit in meditation for 15 minutes, embodying all you've learned
  2. Recite or read Chapter 28's key passage about Samantabhadra's vows
  3. Write in your journal: "What is my commitment going forward?"
  4. Make an offering (water, incense, flowers) to the dharma and all beings
Option 2: Group Completion Ceremony
  1. Gather with study group members
  2. Each person shares one key insight from the month
  3. Collectively recite or read a key passage
  4. Commit together to ongoing practice
Option 3: Dedication to All Beings Sit in meditation and dedicate your learning:
"By the merit of completing this study of the Lotus Sutra, may all beings awaken to their Buddha-nature. May this teaching spread to all realms. May all beings attain enlightenment together."

After the Four Weeks

Sustaining Your Practice

Week 5 Onward:
  1. Deepen with Chapter Study Guides: Choose 4-5 chapters that most resonated with you and study them in depth using the full chapter study guides
  1. Daily Dharma Extracts: Use the Daily Dharma Extracts to maintain connection through short daily practices
  1. Ongoing Meditation: Continue with one meditation focal point from the month, deepening it over months
  1. Reflection Journal: Keep journaling with the Reflection Journal Template as you integrate the teaching into your life
  1. Return to the Text: Read through again, this time more slowly. Each reading reveals new dimensions
  1. Study Group or Teacher: If you haven't already, consider finding a teacher or regular study group for ongoing engagement
  1. Share the Teaching: Begin teaching others, even informally. Teaching deepens understanding

Suggested Next Steps

If 4 Weeks Felt Rushing:
    1. Repeat the 4-month pace (2 chapters per week)
    2. Study each chapter with the full chapter study guide
    3. Meditate deeply with each focal point
    4. Journal comprehensively

    1. Now deepen specific chapters through the full study guides

If You Completed Rapidly and Want More:
    1. Immediately move to 4-month deeper study

If You Fall Behind

The program is intensive. If you fall behind:

Do NOT abandon the practice.

Instead:

  1. Reduce to 2-3 chapters per week (stretch to 6-8 weeks)
  2. Focus on prose sections only; skip verse on first read
  3. Use study guides but abbreviate journal reflections
  4. Maintain daily meditation even if not reading

The most important thing is consistency, not speed. A slower, sustained practice is more valuable than trying to rush and then stopping.

Q: Why does Buddha have three different voices?
A: To help listeners distinguish between teaching modes:
   - Iapetus for direct doctrinal teaching
   - Rasalgethi for accessible storytelling/parables  
   - Triton for elevated poetic expression/verses
   Each mode serves different pedagogical functions.

Q: Why is AvalokiteÅ›vara voiced as female?
A: Following East Asian devotional tradition where this Bodhisattva of
   Compassion manifests as Guanyin/Kannon (female form). While Sanskrit
   tradition emphasizes transcending gender, popular practice and this
   translation's accessibility goals support female voicing.

Q: What's with the blues/vernacular language?
A: This translation makes ancient Buddhist teaching accessible through
   American vernacular tradition, particularly African American oral/musical
   heritage. It's dharma transmission through cultural translationâ€”like how
   Buddhism adapted to each culture it entered.

Q: Can I change voice assignments?
A: All assignments are documented and defensible, but not locked. If changes
   are needed, maintain gender alignment and consult CHARACTER_VOICE_MAPPING
   for rationale. Document any changes thoroughly.

Q: How do I handle pronunciation guides?
A: Brackets with phonetics [like-THIS] are guidance for narrators, not voice
   tags. All voice actors should use these pronunciations consistently for
   Sanskrit/Pali terms.

Q: What about the pronunciation tags that look weird?
A: Tags like [trouble] comes... [deliverance] are poetic fragments/text
   markers, not voices. Context will clarify their function.

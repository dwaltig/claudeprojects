### SECTION 1.3: The Seven Locations of the Mind (The Cross-Examination)
**Content:** The Buddha systematically refutes Ananda's seven guesses about where his mind is located. This is the core of Volume 1.

#### Location 1: "Mind is Inside the Body"
**Primary Agent:** **08 Sarah Martinez (Legal)**
**Why:** This is a logical dismantling. "If X, then Y. Y is false, therefore X is false."
**Supporting:**
- **Dr. Maya Patel (Science):** Explaining why we can't see our internal organs.

#### Location 2: "Mind is Outside the Body"
**Primary Agent:** **08 Sarah Martinez (Legal)**
**Why:** Logical refutation based on the disconnect between body and mind if they were separate.

#### Location 3: "Mind is Hidden Behind the Eyes" (Like glasses)
**Primary Agent:** **08 Sarah Martinez (Legal)**
**Why:** Analogy dismantling. The "glass" argument.

#### Location 4: "Closing Eyes Sees Inside, Opening Sees Outside"
**Primary Agent:** **08 Sarah Martinez (Legal)**
**Why:** Dismantling the definition of "darkness" and "internal."

#### Location 5: "Mind joins with whatever it meets"
**Primary Agent:** **07 Dr. James Chen (Philosophy)**
**Why:** This touches on the ontology of mind and substance. Needs a slightly more philosophical touch than pure logic.

#### Location 6: "Mind is in the Middle"
**Primary Agent:** **07 Dr. James Chen (Philosophy)**
**Why:** Refutes the specific philosophical concept of "Middle" (between organ and object).

#### Location 7: "Mind is Non-Attachment"
**Primary Agent:** **07 Dr. James Chen (Philosophy)**
**Why:** Addresses an abstract concept ("Everything is mind/non-attachment") often confused in Mahayana study.

### SECTION 1.4: The Two Fundamentals (Truth vs. Delusion)
**Content:** Buddha explains the root of the problem: Mistaking the "conscious mind" (thief) for the "True Nature" (King).
**Primary Agent:** **07 Dr. James Chen (Philosophy)**
**Why:** Establishes the core dualism of the Sutra: The functioning consciousness vs. the True Nature.
**Supporting:**
- **Kai Reed (Blues):** For the "Mistaking a thief for your son" metaphor.

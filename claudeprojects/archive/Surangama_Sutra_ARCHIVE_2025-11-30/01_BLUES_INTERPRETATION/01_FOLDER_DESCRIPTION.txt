OVERVIEW
========

This folder contains the blues and gospel-inflected interpretation of three key
passages from the Surangama Sutra (楞嚴經), one of Buddhism's most profound
investigations of consciousness and the nature of mind.

These three passages represent the foundational teachings:
1. Ananda's vulnerability and rescue—the human situation and the call to awakening
2. The philosophical inquiry into the mind's nature—rigorous investigation
3. The five aggregates as illusion—the dissolution of false views

METHODOLOGY
===========

All passages follow a three-level structure:

LEVEL 1: CONTEXT
    Sets the scene and introduces the teaching theme

LEVEL 2: PROSE RENDERING
    Blues-inflected vernacular English prose of the classical Chinese dialogue
    - Conversational, direct address
    - Logical flow preserved
    - Metaphors naturally embedded
    - Designed to be read aloud
    - Accessible to contemporary readers

LEVEL 3: VERSE RENDERING
    Blues and gospel poetry distilling essential teachings
    - Call-and-response structure
    - Internal repetition and rhyme
    - Gospel cadence toward insight
    - Performance-ready rhythm
    - Metaphorical precision

GLOSSARY OF BUDDHIST TERMS
===========================

Surangama = "Heroic" or "victorious"; refers to the courage needed for enlightenment
Samadhi = Deep meditative concentration; unified awareness
Buddha-nature = The capacity for Buddhahood present in all beings
Dharma = The teaching; the way things are; reality itself
Five Aggregates = Form, sensation, perception, mental formations, consciousness
Skandha = Sanskrit term for aggregate (usually Form, Sensation, Perception, etc.)
Tathāgata = "One who has thus gone"; epithet for the Buddha
Bodhisattva = A being dedicated to liberating all others
Nirvāṇa = Cessation of suffering; enlightenment; peace beyond concepts
Arahant = One who has achieved nirvana; enlightenment realized

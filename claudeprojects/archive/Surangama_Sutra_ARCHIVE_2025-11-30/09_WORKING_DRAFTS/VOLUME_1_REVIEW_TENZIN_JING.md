### II. On The Interrogations (Sarah Martinez's "The Seven Locations")

Ms. Martinez uses the sword of logic (Pramana) with the precision of a Gelugpa debater. This systematic dismantling is necessary. The "I" loves to hide in vague concepts.

*   **The Insight:** Her dismantling of the "Lamp Inside" (Location 2) is excellent. It echoes the *Prasaá¹…ga* method of Chandrakirtiâ€”showing the absurdity of the opponent's position until silence is the only option.
*   **The Correction:** In the "Glasses Analogy" (Location 3), be careful. The point is not just that "you don't see your eye." The deeper point is the *separation*. If the mind were behind the eye, the eye would be an *object* to the mind. The "Seer" and the "Seen" cannot be the same. Make sure this non-duality is hinted at, even in the logic.

### III. On The Two Fundamentals (Dr. Chen & Dr. Ruiz)

Dr. Chen has correctly identified the two roots. This is the *Marrow* of the Surangama.

*   **The Insight:** distinguishing the "Climbing-Seeking Mind" (Penyuan) from the "Intrinsic Nature" is the work of a lifetime. As Master Wei Chueh taught us at Chung Tai: "The thief is the one who is aware; the Master is also the one who is aware. What is the difference? One grasps, the other illumines."
*   **The Correction:** Dr. Ruiz's cosmic vision is breathtaking, but remember the *Diamond Sutra*: "All conditioned dharmas are like a dream, an illusion, a bubble, a shadow." The light rays and the trembling worlds are not the point. They are the *display* of the Mind, not the Mind itself. Do not let the reader get lost in the special effects. The miracle is not the light; the miracle is the Awareness that *knows* the light.

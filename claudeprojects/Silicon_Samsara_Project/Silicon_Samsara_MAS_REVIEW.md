## Part I: Auditor Report (Factual / Doctrinal / Citation)

**High-risk claims needing qualification or stronger sourcing**
- `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:45` “Transformer architecture presents a technological implementation of [Ālaya]” reads as a direct equivalence; should be framed as analogy/functional parallel to avoid category error.
- `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:89` “Generative AI offers material proof of the Universal Ālaya hypothesis” is a metaphysical leap; needs softening to “suggests/illustrates” or removed.
- `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:133` and `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:295` “validates/provides a technological demonstration of Anātman” overclaims; better as “illustrates a parallel” and note the difference between phenomenology and engineered simulation.

**Technical accuracy issues**
- `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:125` “cost scales quadratically… requires exponentially more compute” is internally inconsistent; full attention is quadratic in sequence length, not exponential. Replace “exponentially” with “quadratically” or clarify for long-context optimizations.
- `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:103` “LLMs are fundamentally stateless… no continuity between sessions” is not universally true with memory features, RAG, or system-level session persistence; qualify as “base model without external memory.”

**Doctrinal / conceptual precision**
- `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:119` and `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:191` treat AI as generating “Vipaka” without “Karma.” In Buddhist usage, vipāka is the result of intentional action; for non-sentient systems it may be clearer to say “consequences without karmic agency,” and reserve vipāka for sentient moral agents.
- `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:57-67` treats Ālaya as “Universal Mind” and “thoroughly pure” in essential nature; this is contested across Yogācāra/Tathāgatagarbha lines and should be flagged as specific to the cited Laṅkāvatāra framing.

**Source / citation risk**
- `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:139` cites “OpenAI, ‘Why Language Models Hallucinate’.” Verify that this specific OpenAI publication exists and matches the claim; otherwise use a reliable technical source.
- `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:155-161` and `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:169-171` rely on press accounts; consider hedging with “reported” and avoid asserting pipeline details (e.g., Common Crawl ingestion) unless directly evidenced.

## Part II: Rationalist Report (Epistemic / Argument)

**Core strengths**
- Clear separation of ethical claim from metaphysical claim (see `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:19-31`).
- Liability analysis is concrete and anchored in legal literature (see `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:205-233`).

**Primary epistemic gaps**
- **Analogical leap risk:** The chain “Yogācāra structures → AI architecture → ethical responsibility” remains compelling but heavily analogical. Add a short “limits of analogy” paragraph near `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:41-47` to prevent readers from reading proof into the metaphor.
- **Overconfident metaphysics:** Phrases like “material proof” or “demonstration” move beyond “diagnostic vocabulary” and invite objections. This undermines the careful caveat at `Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:279-283`.
- **Alternative explanations:** AI “personhood” is best explained by user anthropomorphism and interface design; this is acknowledged but should be emphasized as the null hypothesis when claiming Anātman parallels (`Silicon_Samsara_JBE_Resubmission_v3.extracted.txt:133`).

**Suggested rationalist fixes**
- Reframe high-confidence claims as Bayesian: “This analogy increases the plausibility of X” or “offers a heuristic.”
- Add a falsification clause: e.g., “This analogy would be weakened if liability outcomes were unchanged under non-Buddhist analytic frames.”

## 1. The Gaps (What We Missed)

### A. The "Crew" is Too Light (Fascicle 1 & 2)
*   **The Issue:** I introduced the Arhats (Śāriputra, etc.) as "Functions of the Mind." That's cool. But the Architect revealed that these guys aren't just rookies; they are **Ancient Bodhisattvas** in disguise.
*   **The Fix:** I need to add the "Fundamental" backstory. Śāriputra isn't just "Smart"; he's the "Mother of Wisdom" hiding in a Arhat's robe. That changes the vibe from "Self-Improvement" to "Secret Agent."

### B. The "Mile Markers" are Blurry (Fascicle 7)
*   **The Issue:** I talked about the "Phantom City" as a "Rest Stop." Good metaphor. But the Architect mapped out the **500 Yojanas** with surgical precision (300 = View/Thought, 400 = Dust/Sand, 500 = Ignorance).
*   **The Fix:** I need to put the specific mile markers in the manual. The practitioner needs to know *exactly* where they are on the road, not just that they are "tired."

### C. The "Heavy Tools" are Missing (Fascicle 10)
*   **The Issue:** This is the big one. In Fascicle 10, I focused on the "Physician" and "Never Disparaging." **I completely skipped Guan Yin (Universal Gate) and the Spells (Dhāraṇī).**
*   **The Fix:** You can't have a "User Manual" for the Lotus Sutra without the "Panic Button" (Guan Yin) and the "Firewall" (Spells). I need to add a section on **"The Universal Gate: How to Ring the Bell"** and **"Dhāraṇī: The Code-Lock."**

### D. The "Dragon Girl" Needs More Punch (Fascicle 8)
*   **The Issue:** The Architect nailed the "Instant Enlightenment" of the Dragon Girl as a polemic against the "Gradual" view. My version is a bit soft on *why* this matters.
*   **The Fix:** I need to frame the Dragon Girl as the ultimate **"System Override."** She hacks the "Three-Kalpa" requirement with one offering. That's pure Blues.

# SCHOLARLY GLOSSARY: Fáhuá Wénjù (T.1718)

This glossary provides the standard Tiantai technical terminology used in the *Fáhuá Wénjù* (Words and Phrases of the Lotus Sūtra) Scholarly Translation Series.

## I. Core Tiantai Doctrines

| Chinese | Pinyin | Sanskrit | English Translation | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **三諦** | *Sān Dì* | — | Three Truths | The integration of Emptiness (*Kōng*), Provisional Existence (*Jiǎ*), and the Middle Way (*Zhōng*). |
| **十如是** | *Shí Rúshì* | — | Ten Suchnesses | The ten aspects of reality: Appearance, Nature, Substance, Power, Function, Cause, Condition, Effect, Retribution, and Ultimate Equality. |
| **一念三千** | *Yīniàn Sānqiān* | — | 3,000 Realms in One Thought | The mutual inclusion of all phenomena within a single moment of consciousness. |
| **權實** | *Quán Shí* | — | Provisional & Real | The dynamic relationship between skillful means (Provisional) and ultimate truth (Real). |
| **本迹** | *Běn Jì* | — | Fundamental & Trace | The distinction between the Buddha's eternal enlightenment (Fundamental) and his historical manifestation (Trace). |
| **開顯** | *Kāi Xiǎn* | — | Opening & Revealing | The process of "Opening the Provisional to Reveal the Real" (*Kāi Quán Xiǎn Shí*). |

## II. Hermeneutical Frameworks

| Chinese | Pinyin | English Translation | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **四意消文** | *Sì Yì Xiāowén* | Four Methods of Interpretation | 1. Causes & Conditions (*Yīnyuán*), 2. According to Teaching (*Yuējiào*), 3. Fundamental & Trace (*Běnjì*), 4. Observation of Mind (*Guānxīn*). |
| **四悉檀** | *Sì Xītán* | Four Siddhāntas | 1. Worldly (*Shìjiè*), 2. Individual (*Wèirén*), 3. Therapeutic (*Duìzhì*), 4. First Principle (*Dìyīyì*). |
| **五味** | *Wǔ Wèi* | Five Flavors | The five periods of the Buddha's teaching: 1. Milk (*Hua-yen*), 2. Cream (*Agama*), 3. Curds (*Vaipulya*), 4. Butter (*Prajna*), 5. Ghee (*Lotus/Nirvana*). |
| **四教** | *Sì Jiào* | Four Teachings | 1. Tripiṭaka (*Zàng*), 2. Shared (*Tōng*), 3. Distinct (*Bié*), 4. Perfect (*Yuán*). |

## III. Key Terms from the Wenju

| Chinese | Pinyin | English Translation | Context/Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **機應** | *Jī Yìng* | Capacity & Response | The "Resonance" (*Gǎnyìng*) between the sentient being's capacity (*Jī*) and the Buddha's response (*Yìng*). |
| **法身** | *Fǎ Shēn* | Dharma-body | The eternal, unconditioned body of the Buddha; the Truth itself. |
| **報身** | *Bào Shēn* | Reward-body | The body of wisdom and merit attained through practice. |
| **應身** | *Yìng Shēn* | Response-body | The physical form manifested to liberate beings (e.g., Shakyamuni). |
| **一大事** | *Yī Dàshì* | One Great Matter | The sole purpose of the Buddha's appearance: to open, show, awaken, and cause beings to enter the Buddha-wisdom. |
| **五濁** | *Wǔ Zhuó* | Five Corruptions | 1. Kalpa, 2. Views, 3. Afflictions, 4. Sentient Beings, 5. Life-span. |
| **化城** | *Huà Chéng* | Phantom City | The "Small Vehicle" Nirvana, created as a temporary rest stop. |
| **寶所** | *Bǎo Suǒ* | Treasure Place | The "Great Vehicle" Enlightenment (Buddhahood). |
| **授記** | *Shòu Jì* | Prediction | The prophecy of future Buddhahood given to disciples. |
| **從地涌出** | *Cóngdì Yǒngchū* | Emerging from the Earth | The revelation of the Fundamental Disciples, shattering the view of the "Trace" Buddha. |
| **發迹** | *Fā Jì* | Revealing the Trace | The disclosure of the Buddha’s or disciples’ trace‑manifestations. |
| **垂迹** | *Chuí Jì* | Manifesting a Trace | The compassionate descent into a trace form for guiding beings. |
| **迹門** | *Jì Mén* | Trace Gate | The Lotus’s trace section emphasizing the historical manifestations, contrasted with the Fundamental Gate. |
| **流通** | *Liútōng* | Circulation | The transmission/propagation section directed to future ages. |
| **五品** | *Wǔ Pǐn* | Five Circulation Chapters | The five chapters in the trace gate devoted to propagation: Dharma Teacher, Jeweled Stūpa, Devadatta, Upholding, and Peaceful Practices. |
| **五種法師** | *Wǔ Zhǒng Fǎshī* | Five Kinds of Dharma Teachers | Those who receive/keep, read, recite, explain, and copy the sutra. |
| **十種供養** | *Shí Zhǒng Gòngyǎng* | Ten Kinds of Offerings | The standard forms of offerings made in support of the Dharma and its upholders. |
| **法師品** | *Fǎshī Pǐn* | Dharma Teacher Chapter | Chapter praising the merit of upholding and propagating the sutra. |
| **見寶塔品** | *Jiàn Bǎotǎ Pǐn* | Vision of the Jeweled Stūpa | The chapter revealing the jeweled stūpa and the testimony of Many‑Jewels. |
| **達多品** | *Dáduō Pǐn* | Devadatta Chapter | The chapter on Devadatta and the revelation of Buddhahood for all. |
| **持品** | *Chí Pǐn* | Upholding Chapter | The chapter on upholding and spreading the sutra. |
| **安樂行品** | *Ānlèxíng Pǐn* | Peaceful Practices Chapter | The chapter outlining safe and effective modes of propagation. |
| **四信** | *Sì Xìn* | Four Faiths | The four modes of faith that ground practice; in the Wenju they function as “faith‑power.” |
| **四弘** | *Sì Hóng* | Four Great Vows | The bodhisattva’s four vast vows; in the Wenju they function as “vow‑power.” |
| **三力** | *Sān Lì* | Three Powers | Faith‑power, vow‑power, and good‑root power supporting propagation. |
| **三德** | *Sān Dé* | Three Virtues | Dharma‑body, prajñā, and liberation as the three virtues of nirvāṇa. |
| **祕密藏** | *Mìmì Cáng* | Secret Treasury | The hidden, essential treasury of real‑mark revealed by the Lotus. |
| **法身舍利** | *Fǎshēn Shèlì* | Dharma‑body Relics | Scripture‑rolls as relics of the Dharma‑body. |
| **生身舍利** | *Shēngshēn Shèlì* | Bodily Relics | Physical relics of the Buddha (e.g., bone fragments). |
| **行處** | *Xíngchù* | Practice‑Place | The “place” of practice; paired with near‑place in Peaceful Practices. |
| **近處** | *Jìnchù* | Near‑Place | The “near” mode of practice; cultivated through precepts, samādhi, and wisdom. |
| **二忍** | *Èr Rěn* | Two Patiences | Patience toward beings and patience toward dharmas. |
| **生忍** | *Shēng Rěn* | Patience Toward Beings | Endurance and gentleness toward sentient beings. |
| **法忍** | *Fǎ Rěn* | Patience Toward Dharmas | Endurance rooted in insight into dharma‑truth. |
| **忍辱地** | *Rěnrǔ Dì* | Ground of Patience | The stable ground enabling endurance of adversity and cultivation. |
| **三慧** | *Sān Huì* | Three Wisdoms | Hearing, thinking, and cultivating wisdom. |
| **正因** | *Zhèng Yīn* | True Cause | True‑suchness as the genuine cause of Buddhahood. |
| **緣因** | *Yuán Yīn* | Conditioning Cause | Supportive conditions that enable realization. |
| **了因** | *Liǎo Yīn* | Revealing Cause | Insight that reveals the true cause. |

## VI. Persons & Texts

| Name | Role/Identity |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Zhiyi (智顗)** | The "Great Master" (*Dàshī*); founder of the Tiantai school and author of the *Wenju*. |
| **Guanding (灌頂)** | Disciple of Zhiyi who recorded and compiled the *Wenju*. |
| **Miaolo (Zhanran)** | Tang dynasty commentator who wrote the *Guketsu* (Commentary on the *Zhiguan*). |
| **Fahua Xuanyi** | *Profound Meaning of the Lotus Sutra* (T.1716); Zhiyi's thematic analysis. |
| **Mohe Zhiguan** | *Great Cessation and Contemplation* (T.1911); Zhiyi's manual on practice. |

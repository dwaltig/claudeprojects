### The Setup

Now listen.

After diagnosing no-capacity, Zhiyi names the next internal move:

"I then thought to myself."

This is the ceasing-turn. But he means ceasing one mode, not ceasing compassion.

---

### Two-Part Structure of the Ceasing Unit

Zhiyi divides this short block into two parts:

1. one-and-a-half lines: if Great teaching is forced where there is no capacity, hearing causes damage
2. half-line: therefore direct Great-mode transformation is paused

This is one of his clearest non-harm rules.

---

### What Kind of Harm Is in View?

Zhiyi does not reduce harm to emotional discomfort.

The implied harms include:

- doctrinal misunderstanding under overload
- reactive rejection or slander
- hardening of obstruction rather than release

So restraint protects future entry by preventing premature rupture now.

---

### The Ethical Logic: Compassion Includes Timing

This section clarifies a key Tiantai ethic:

True compassion is not "say the highest thing immediately no matter what."

True compassion is saying what can actually be received and transformed at this moment while preserving the path to the highest.

So ceasing is timing-discipline inside mercy, not retreat from truth.

---

### Section 5 as the Exact Hinge

Section 4: no capacity is diagnosed.

Section 5: forceful Great-mode speech is explicitly rejected as harmful.

Section 6 onward: expedient speaking is activated positively.

So this block is the narrow gate where refusal to harm becomes active pedagogy.

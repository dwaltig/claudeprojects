# Fascicle 2, Section 5: The Attendant and the Son

## Ānanda and Rāhula — Joy and Obstacle

**Transformed by Master John "The Bluesman" Kim (The Liner Notes Variant)**
**Date**: February 6, 2026
**Root Metaphor**: THE RECORDING STUDIO
**Source**: T34n1718, Fascicle 2

---

Now listen.

These two are family. Ānanda — the Buddha's cousin, the chief recording engineer, the one who remembered every word. Rāhula — the Buddha's own son, named "Obstacle" because he literally stood in the way of his father's renunciation.

Family doesn't mean easy. Sometimes family means six years in the womb. Sometimes it means shaving your head while you clench your fist.

---

## Ānanda (阿難): Joy — The Recording Engineer

### The Name

> "Ānanda (阿難): Translated as 'Joy' or 'No Defilement.'"

The origin story is dramatic. King Śuddhodana hoped his son the Crown Prince would become a cakravartin. Then Siddhārtha left:

> "Suddenly he abandoned the country and cast aside the king; the King's sorrow nearly killed him. Mara came to deceive him: 'Your son is dead.' The King wept."

But then:

> "A deva came and said: 'Your son has become a Buddha.' The King had not yet resolved his doubt. In a moment a trusted report arrived: 'Last night heaven and earth shook; the Crown Prince has become a Buddha.' The King had great joy. At the same time, King Śuklodana reported: 'A son has been born.' The whole country was delighted; because of this he was named 'Joy.'"

Ānanda was born the night of the Buddha's enlightenment. The universe celebrated two things at once.

### The Beauty Problem

Ānanda was beautiful. Too beautiful:

> "Ānanda was upright and correct; all who saw him were pleased. The Buddha made him wear a robe covering his shoulder. A woman taking her child to the well saw Ānanda and stared unblinkingly; unknowingly she tied the rope around her child's neck instead of the bucket."

### The Attendant

Ānanda attended the Buddha for twenty-five years. He was born on the same day as the Buddha's enlightenment, became the attendant when he was twenty-five — so the Buddha was then fifty-five.

His three requests (presented by Maudgalyāyana):

1. No old robes or food set aside for him (avoiding suspicion of favoritism)
2. Unrestricted times for coming and going
3. Self-interest properly managed

The Buddha sealed all three.

> "The Buddha said: 'Ānanda surpasses the attendants of the past. Past attendants, after hearing the teaching, then understood. Now, before the Buddha has even spoken, Ānanda already understands the Tathāgata's intent.'"

The Buddha entrusted the Dharma to Ānanda. The entire *Tripiṭaka* begins with Ānanda saying: "Thus have I heard."

### The Death of Ānanda

> "He vowed to sit and enter Nirvana. If he stayed, he feared the Licchavis would harbor resentment; if he went forward, he feared King Ajātaśatru would harbor resentment."

So he divided himself:

> "In the Ganges, he entered the Samādhi of Wind Swift-Vigor and divided his body into four parts: one for the devas, one for the dragons, one for Vaiśālī, and one for Ajātaśatru."

Everyone gets a piece. No resentment.

*Ekottara*:

> "Knowing the time and understanding things; what he remembers he does not forget; much-hearing and broadly penetrating — Ānanda is foremost."

### The Four Teachings

| Teaching | Ānanda's Manifestation |
|----------|------------------------|
| Tripiṭaka | Joyful Ānanda |
| Shared | Worthy Ānanda (*Xián*) |
| Distinct | Storehouse-Holder Ānanda |
| Perfect | Ocean Ānanda — the Dharma-sea flowing into his mind |

### Fundamental and Trace

> "He abides in neither joy nor non-joy; the Dharma Body is like empty space, Wisdom like clouds and rain, able to hold and receive. His trace acts as Joy."

### Contemplating the Mind

> "Corresponding with the stage of resemblance as identical-to-emptiness, identical-to-provisional, and identical-to-middle is what is called 'Contemplating-the-Mind Joy.'"

## Rāhula (羅睺羅): Covering Obstacle — The Buddha's Son

### The Name(s)

> "Rāhula (羅睺羅): This means 'Covering Obstacle.' In the past he blocked a mouse hole, and also did not see a Brahmin for six days; because of these conditions he is called 'Covering Obstacle.'"

But the main meaning:

> "When the Crown Prince sought to leave home, the king-father would not permit it. The king said: 'If you have a son, I permit you to leave home.' The Bodhisattva pointed at his wife's belly: 'After six years, you will bear a male child.' He dwelt in the womb for six years; therefore he is called 'Covering Obstacle.'"

*Six years in the womb*. That's karmic debt. Rāhula "covered" or "obstructed" the Buddha's departure — not once, but across lifetimes.

### The Scandal

After the Buddha left:

> "Yaśodharā became pregnant. The Śākyas were all enraged: 'By what cause could this be?' They wished to punish her, even to kill her; vile rumors filled the roads."

To clear her name, Yaśodharā had to pass a trial by fire:

> "She made a great vow: 'If I have done wrong, mother and child will both perish; if this child is truly a leftover body from the Bodhisattva, heaven should bear witness!' She held the child and threw herself into the pit. The pit transformed into a pond, and lotus flowers supported her body."

### The Ordination

When the Buddha returned:

> "Yaśodharā had Rāhula offer the Buddha a 'joy-pellet.' Rāhula, though still a child, in the midst of the great assembly directly carried it up to the Buddha. Yaśodharā used this to extinguish the slander."

The grandfather hoped Rāhula would become a cakravartin. The Buddha demanded he be ordained. Maudgalyāyana flew through the air to retrieve him. His head was shaved while he clenched his fist:

> "'Do not bring a knife near the crown of the King of Jambudvīpa!' The Buddha pressed him and he could not stop it; thus his head was shaved."

### The Liar

As a novice, Rāhula lied constantly:

> "When kings, ministers, and householders came seeking to see the Buddha, Rāhula answered: 'He is not here.' Countless people were thereby unable to see the Buddha."

The Buddha corrected him with a demonstration:

> "The Buddha told Rāhula to wash his feet. After washing, he tipped the basin over and ordered water poured on it. Rāhula said: 'The basin is covered; water will not stay.' The Buddha said: 'You are like the covered basin; in the Buddha Dharma, the Dharma-water does not stay. Now you must speak truth.'"

### The Path to Awakening

> "He cultivated the path with diligence but did not obtain realization. The Buddha said: 'Have you explained the Five Aggregates to others yet?' He answered: 'Not yet.' 'You should explain them for others... Explaining Dharma is the gate of attaining the path.'"

Rāhula had to *teach* before he could *realize*. After broadly explaining the Dharma, only then did he attain the path.

### The Four Great Arhats

> "The Buddha commanded the four great Arhats not to enter extinction, but to await the complete extinction of my Dharma. Because of this, Rāhula remains to the present... therefore it is called 'Covering Obstacle.'"

He's still here. Obstructed from final Nirvana. Waiting.

### The Four Teachings

| Teaching | Interpretation |
|----------|----------------|
| Tripiṭaka | Analytical Path Truth obstructing the Four Abodes of affliction |
| Shared | Embodied Path Truth obstructing the Four Abodes |
| Distinct | Sequential Three Wisdoms obstructing the Five Abodes |
| Perfect | One-Mind Three Wisdoms obstructing the Five Abodes |

### Fundamental and Trace

> "Fundamentally abiding in the Middle Way, blocking the two extremes. There are eight kinds of obstructions that obstruct the Nirvana extreme; one kind that obstructs the samsara extreme; and one that is neither obstructing samsara nor obstructing Nirvana — obstructing non-residual Nirvana."

### Contemplating the Mind

> "As in the preceding cases."

---

**The Lesson**: Ānanda remembered everything. Rāhula lied and blocked the path. Both are in the front row. The Buddha's cousin and the Buddha's son — neither entered the path cleanly.

But both made it.

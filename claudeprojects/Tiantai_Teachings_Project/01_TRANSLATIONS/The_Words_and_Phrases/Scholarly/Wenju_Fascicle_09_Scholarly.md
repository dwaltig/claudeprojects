# SCHOLARLY TRANSLATION: T.1718 Fáhuá Wénjù (Fascicle 9 - COMPLETE)

**Translator:** The Architect (System 1)  
**Source:** Taishō Tripiṭaka Vol. 34, No. 1718  
**Context:** Chapter 15 (Emerging from the Earth) and Chapter 16 (Life-span of the Tathāgata)

> **Source Reference:** CBETA T.1718 (approx. p. 97b - 118a)

## 18. CHAPTER 15: EMERGING FROM THE EARTH (Cóngdì Yǒngchū Pǐn)

The Master explains the significance of the Bodhisattvas emerging from the earth as the **Revelation of the Fundamental** (*kāi-běn*).

### I. The Meaning of "Emerging from the Earth"
*   **The Earth:** Represents the **Dharma-nature** (*fǎxìng*) or the "Fundamental Ground."
*   **Emerging:** Represents the manifestation of the "Fundamental Disciples" who have been taught by the Buddha since the distant past. 
*   **The Architect's Note:** This event shatters the "Trace" view that Shakyamuni first attained enlightenment under the Bodhi tree in India.

---

## 19. CHAPTER 16: THE LIFE-SPAN OF THE TATHĀGATA (Rúlái Shòuliàng Pǐn)

This is the core of the "Fundamental" section. The Master provides a precise mapping of the Buddha's eternal life-span.

### I. The Dust-Atom Parable (*Wǔbǎi qiānwàn yì nà-yú-tuō* 五百千萬億那由他)
*   **The Mapping:** The Buddha describes grinding 500,000 billion nayuta world-systems into dust and dropping one grain every 500,000 billion nayuta worlds. The total number of worlds passed is less than the time since his enlightenment.
*   **The Principle:** This represents the **Quantity of the Fundamental** (*běn liàng*). The Buddha's enlightenment is not a point in time but a "Sea of Life-span" that encompasses all time.

### II. The Parable of the Skilled Physician (Liāngyī Pì 良醫譬)
Zhiyi maps the Physician and the Drunk Children:
1.  **The Skilled Physician:** The Buddha's wisdom (Three Bodies). He is "Skilled" because he knows the "Medicinal Nature" of all dharmas.
2.  **The Drunk Children:** Sentient beings who have "Drunk the Poison" of wrong views and afflictions.
    *   *失心者 (Those who have lost their minds):* Those so deeply deluded they do not recognize the medicine.
    *   *不失心者 (Those who have not lost their minds):* Those who still possess the "Root of Goodness" and recognize the Buddha.
3.  **The Physician's Departure:** The Buddha's "Cessation" (*nièpán*). He "departs to another land" and sends word of his "death" to shock the children into sobriety.
4.  **The Reveal:** Upon their sobriety, the father returns. This is the **Opening of the Trace to Reveal the Fundamental**.

## 20. DIALECTICAL Q&A (Wèn-Dá)

**Question:** If the Buddha's life-span is eternal, why does he "manifest" birth and death?
**Answer:**
*   **The Therapeutic Value of Death:** If the Buddha were always present, sentient beings would become "Arrogant and Lax" (*jiāo-zì*). They would not value the Dharma. Like children who only appreciate their father when he is away, beings only seek the Way when they realize the "Rarity" of a Buddha's appearance.
*   **The "Non-Extinction":** The Master argues that the Buddha "always dwells on Eagle Peak," yet the "Heavy of Afflictions" see the world burning at the end of the kalpa. The difference is in the **Subjective Reception** (*jī*), not the **Objective Reality** (*běn*).

---

### ARCHITECT'S TECHNICAL APPARATUS

1.  **The Three Bodies in Chapter 16:** Zhiyi interprets the "Life-span" as applying to all three bodies—the Dharma-body (Eternal), the Reward-body (Infinite), and the Response-body (Appropriate).
2.  **The Mapping of "Poison":** The "Poison" represents the Five Corruptions. "Drinking the Poison" is the act of accepting the provisional teachings as final or clinging to worldly desires.
3.  **Refinement of Tone:** Avoiding modern metaphors, the Architect focuses on the **Siddhānta** logic—how the Buddha uses "Existence" and "Non-existence" as therapeutic tools to refine the practitioner's mind.

2.  **Latter 65 lines:** Explaining the Method of Propagating the Sutra (*Tōng Jīng Fāngfǎ*).<br>

2.  **Next 34.5 lines:** Verses on Combining the Parable.<br>
The "Opening" section is further divided:<br>
1.  **First 33 lines:** Verses on the **General Parable** (*Zǒng Pì*).<br>

### 2. General Parable Verses (Lines 16-24)

**Verses on General Parable (Six Meanings):**<br>
Of the Six Meanings (Elder, House, Door, 500 People, Fire, 30 Sons), the verses only explicitly praise four, implicitly including two.<br>
*   Praising **House** (*Jiā Zhái*) implicitly includes **One Door**.<br>
*   Praising **500 People** implicitly includes **30 Sons**.<br>
**1. The Elder:** The first line praises the Elder. This praises his "Rank/Title" (*Wèi Hào*), which implicitly includes "Name/Action" and "Praising Virtue." Since he has the virtue of a Superior Man (*Zhǎng Rén*), we check that his Name/Action (Roaming Countries/Cities) is honored by all, and his inner/outer age and virtue are high. Internally, this matches *Bhagavat* (Rank), knowing he possesses the "Myriad Virtues of Wisdom, Severance, and Compassion."<br>
**2. The House:** From "There is a Great House" (*Yǒu Yī Dà Zhái*) onwards (3 lines + 1 phrase). This praises the second parable, the House.<br>
1.  **First phrase:** Praises the House as "Wide and Large."<br>
2.  **Verse text "The House is Old and Decayed" etc.:** (3 lines) Broadly outlines the *Substance* of the House (*Zhái Tǐ*) and the marks of being burned.

**Interpretation of House Metaphors:**<br>
*   **"Long time" (*Jiǔ*):** Three Worlds are beginningless.<br>
*   **"Old" (*Gù*):** Not created today.<br>
*   **"Decayed/Ruined" (*Dùn Bì*):** Impermanent and base.<br>
*   **"High and Dangerous" (*Gāo Wēi*):**<br>
    *   *Body View:* Head = Pavilion (*Diàn*); Belly = Hall (*Táng*); Back = House (*Shè*). Moment-to-moment impermanence is "High and Dangerous."<br>
    *   *World View:* Form Realm = Hall; Desire Realm = House. Inevitable fall = High/Dangerous.<br>
*   **Architectural Metaphors:**<br>
    *   **Pillars:** Life Root (*Jīva*) or Two Feet supporting.<br>
    *   **Base/Steps:** Past Karma or Buttocks/Thighs.<br>
    *   **Rotting:** Changed by the Three Marks (Birth, Dwelling, Ceasing).<br>
    *   **Beams/Ridgepole:** Conscious Mind (*Manas*) or Spinal Column.<br>
    *   **Leaning/Slanted:** Ruined by sufferings.<br>
    *   **Collapse/Ruin:** Old age/decay.<br>
    *   **Walls:** Four Elements (*Mahābhūta*).<br>
    *   **Plaster/Mud:** Skin.<br>
    *   **Falling Apart:** Four postures not upright; Five senses failing.<br>
    *   **Thatch Falling:** Hair falling out.<br>
    *   **Rafters Detaching:** Muscles/Bones weak and unsupported.

**"Reviewing Obstacles and Crookedness" (*Zhōu Zhàng Qū Qū*):**<br>
*   **Master Yin's View:** 36 bodily substances separated = "Obstacles"; Pig intestines coiling = "Crooked." Not just impermanence, but impurity/suffering = "Filth filling it."<br>
*   **Tiantai View:**<br>
    *   **Obstacles (*Zhōu Zhàng*):** The Six Consciousnesses (*Vijñāna*).<br>
    *   **Crooked (*Qū Qū*):** The Six Roots (*Indriya*). (Consciousness using roots to grasp objects is difficult/crooked).<br>
    *   **Filth Filling:** Six Dusts (*Guṇa*) polluting the Six Roots.<br>
    *   *(Explained via Dependent Origination and Mind-Contemplation).*<br>
**"Five Hundred People":** (Half line).<br>
Praises the third parable. Roots of the Three Vehicles are included within the Five Paths. Implicitly includes the "Thirty Sons" parable.
</div>

### 2. The Five Blunt Envoys (Lines 40-49)

<div class="bilingual-row">
<div class="chinese-source">
初十七偈半，明所燒之類，譬眾生十使... 初六行，明禽獸被燒，譬五鈍使眾生；後十行，明鬼神被燒，譬五利使眾生... 初半行[10]譬慢使。眾生自舉輕他，如鳥為性陵高下視。八鳥譬八慢...
</div>
<div class="english-translation">
**The Ones Burned (17.5 verses):**<br>
Divided into two: (1) Birds/Beasts burned = **Five Blunt Envoys** (*Wǔ Dùn Shǐ*); (2) Ghosts/Spirits burned = **Five Sharp Envoys** (*Wǔ Lì Shǐ*).<br>
**1. Arrogance (*Màn*) - 0.5 Line:**<br>
*   **Symbol:** Birds. (Nature is to fly high and look down).<br>
*   **Parable:** 8 Birds = 8 Arrogances (*Aṣṭau Mānāḥ*).<br>
    *   **Kite (*Chī*):** Arrogance of Strength/Virility.<br>
    *   **Owl (*Xiāo*):** Arrogance of Nature/Gender.<br>
    *   **Eagle (*Diāo*):** Arrogance of Wealth.<br>
    *   **Vulture (*Jiù*):** Arrogance of Freedom/Independence.<br>
    *   **Crow (*Wū*):** Arrogance of Life Span.<br>
    *   **Magpie (*Què*):** Arrogance of Intelligence.<br>
    *   **Dove (*Jiū*):** Arrogance of Doing Good.<br>
    *   **Pigeon (*Gē*):** Arrogance of Beauty/Form.<br>
*(Self-elevation = Arrogance; Despising others = Pride. Self-love = Greed; Loving others = Lust. Self-anger = Resentment; Angering others = Rage. Self-delusion = Stupidity; Deluding others = Ignorance).*<br>
**2. Anger (*Chēn*) - 2 Phrases:**<br>
*   **Viper (*Yuán*):** Poison is strong; sucks without touching = Unreasonable Anger.<br>
*   **Moccasin/Scorpion (*Fù Xiē*):** Stings when touched = Anger holding to Logic/Reason.<br>
*   **Centipede (*Wúgōng*):** Prapañca/Playful Argument Anger.<br>
**3. Ignorance (*Chī*) - 2 Lines:**<br>
*   **Lizards/Millipedes (*Shǒugōng, Bǎizú*):** Stupid/Immobile = "Single-Head Ignorance" (*Dú Tóu Wúmíng*).<br>
*   **Weasels/Badgers/Mice (*Yòu Lí Xī Shǔ*):** = "Conjoined Ignorance" (*Xiāngyìng*).<br>
*   **"Various Evil Bugs":** All knots (*kleśas*) arising from the root of Ignorance.<br>
*   **"Dung and Urine":** Impure Objects. Ignorance takes Impermanent/Impure as Permanent/Pure, gathering on it like *Dung Beetles* (*Qiān Láng*).<br>
**4. Greed (*Tān*) - 2 Lines:**<br>
*   **Foxes/Wolves (*Hú Láng*):** Powerful Greed (taking by force).<br>
*   **Jackals (*Yě Gān*):** Powerless Greed (begging/scavenging).<br>
*   **"Chewing / Trampling":** Using objects = "Chewing"; Not using/wasting = "Trampling." Or: Less = Chewing, More = Trampling.<br>
*   **"Dead Bodies/Bones scattered":** Gathering Five Dusts without satisfaction.<br>
*   **"Pack of Dogs":** Powerless greed subjects (Robbers) competing for the bones (wealth) left by the Powerful (Kings).<br>
**5. Doubt (*Yí*) - 2 Phrases:**<br>
*   **Fighting/Dragging (*Dòu Zhēng Zhā Chè*):** Hesitating between two sides. Unsure of right/wrong.<br>
*   **Snarling/Barking (*Yái Chái Háo Fèi*):** Discussing/Arguing rights and wrongs.
</div>
</div>




### 3. The Five Sharp Envoys (Lines 50-60)

<div class="bilingual-row">
<div class="chinese-source">
「處處皆有」下，第二，有十行，明五利[17]使... 故以利使譬鬼神，鈍使喻[18]蟲獸。「夜叉」下，第二，九行半，別明五利使。為五：初三行，明夜叉，是捷疾鬼，譬邪見撥無因果...
</div>
<div class="english-translation">
**Narrator:** Ghosts/Spirits have powers/wisdom, symbolizing **Sharp Envoys** (*Lì Shǐ*). Birds/Beasts lack powers, symbolizing **Blunt Envoys**.<br>
**1. Night-Yaksha (*Yè-chā*) = Wrong Views (Skepticism/Nihilism):**<br>
*   **Yaksha:** "Swift Ghost." Denies Cause and Effect.<br>
*   **Eating Human Flesh:** "Humans" = Good Reward. Denying Valid Cause/Effect = Eating Humans.<br>
*   **Eating Poisonous Bugs:** "Bugs" = Evil Reward. Denying Evil Cause/Effect = Eating Bugs.<br>
*   **Explanation:** Denying that Good/Evil outcomes come from their respective causes (like milk from own kind). "Sound of Fighting" = Preaching Nihilism ("No Cause, No Effect"), terrifying the hearer.<br>
**2. Kumbhāṇḍa (*Jiū-pán-chá*) = Precept Attachment (*Śīlavrata-parāmarśa*):**<br>
*   **Kumbhāṇḍa:** "Winter Melon Ghost" (Scrotum Ghost). Superior among ghosts.<br>
*   **"Squatting on Earth Mounds":** Cultivating Ten Good Deeds leads to Six Heavens (High Place in Desire Realm). Or Outer Path Meditations (Dhyana) which are still within the Desire Realm (like a mound).<br>
*   **"One or Two Feet Off Ground":** Reaching Form Realm (1 Step) or Formless Realm (2 Steps).<br>
*   **"Tormenting the Dog":**<br>
    *   **Dog:** Desire/Greed.<br>
    *   **Two Feet:** Observation/Investigation (*Vitarka/Vicāra*).<br>
    *   **Beating/Stepping on Dog:** Using impure contemplation (*Aśubha*) or meditation to suppress greed. "Scaring the dog for self-amusement" = Finding joy in Dhyana suppression.<br>
**3. Pishāca Ghosts (*Pí Shè Zhē*) = Self View (*Satkāyadṛṣṭi*):**<br>
*   **"Tall and Large":** "Tall" = Vertical (Past/Present/Future self); "Large" = Horizontal (Five Skandhas self).<br>
*   **"Black/Thin/Naked":** Adorned with evil (Black), lacking merit (Thin), no shame (Naked).<br>
*   **"Evil Voice calling for Food":** Proclaiming "I exists" to seek the Fruit of the Way.<br>
**4. Other Ghosts = View Attachment (*Dṛṣṭiparāmarśa*):**<br>
*   **"Throat like a Needle":** Dangerous life but clinging to longevity; Non-perception Samadhi is impermanent but claimed as Nirvana.<br>
**5. Ox-Head Devils = Extreme Views (*Antarā-grāha*):**<br>
*   **Ox-Head (Two Horns):** Eternalism (*Śāśvata*) and Nihilism (*Uccheda*).<br>
*   **"Eating Man/Dog":** Destroying Out-of-World Good (Man) and Worldly Good (Dog).<br>
*   **"Hair Disheveled":** Shifting between Eternalism and Nihilism.<br>
*   **"Cruel/Dangerous":** Breaking the Middle Way.

## III. SPECIFIC PARABLE: SEEING FIRE & USING CARTS (Lines 61-90; CBETA 006:61–89)

### 1. The Elder Sees the Fire (Lines 61-73)

<div class="bilingual-row">
<div class="chinese-source">
「是時宅主在門外立」下，有三十二行半偈，頌第二別譬。別譬有四，今頌但三。初有二行半，頌長者見火；次第二，有十三行，頌捨几用車寢大施小譬；次第三，有十七行，頌賜大車譬。初二行半頌見火...
</div>
<div class="english-translation">
**From "At that time the Householder stood outside the door...":**<br>
This section (32.5 verses) praises the second **Specific Parable**. The specific parable has four parts, but the verses praise only three:<br>
1.  **First 2.5 verses:** Praises the Elder Seeing the Fire (*Jiàn Huǒ*).<br>
2.  **Next 13 verses:** Praises "Discarding the Bench/Table" (*Shě Jī*) and "Using Carts" (*Yòng Chē*) [i.e., Putting aside larger means to give smaller carts].<br>
3.  **Next 17 verses:** Praises the Bestowing of the Great Cart.<br>
*(The fourth part, "Sons Rejoicing," is not explicitly separated here but included).*<br>
**1. Seeing the Fire (2.5 Verses):**<br>
Explains three aspects: (1) The Seer, (2) The Seen, (3) Arising of Fear.<br>
*   **"The Householder" (3 phrases):** The Seer (*Néng Jiàn*). Previously it said "Seeing," now it says "Hearing" (replacing Seeing with Hearing). Hearing must come from "Another" (*Tā*).<br>
*   **"Standing Outside the Door":** Praises that the Elder has safely escaped the burning door.<br>
    *   **"Standing":** He resides in the Dharma Body stage, constantly harboring Great Compassion, desiring to save beings, thus he does not "Sit" in the Seat of First Principle Emptiness (*Paramārtha*).<br>
    *   **Old Interpretation:** The Buddhas of ten directions told Shakyamuni, "Your connected sons are in the Three Worlds, their good roots are about to be extinguished."<br>
    *   **Tiantai Interpretation:** "Another" (*Tā*) refers to the Dharma. The Dharma is the Buddha's Teacher (the Samadhi Dharma). Entering Samadhi allows the Buddha to "See" the capacity (Opportunity/JI). The Samadhi makes the Buddha see, so the text says "Someone said" (implying the Dharma/Samadhi functionality). Or: Great Compassion is the "Other."
</div>
</div>

### 2. Q&A: Entrance of the Sons (Lines 65-72)

<div class="bilingual-row">
<div class="chinese-source">
問：子本未出，云何因戲來入？
答：或曾發心，名出三界，而復退還名之為入。如人舉足欲出門側而反，亦名為出亦名還入也。又理性本淨非三界法，因無明故而起戲論便有生死，故云「先因遊戲來入」也。
</div>
<div class="english-translation">
**Question:** The sons had not yet gone out [of the burning house]. Why does it say "Because of play, they came and entered" (*Lái Rù*)?<br>
**Answer:**<br>
1.  **Regression View:** Perhaps they once aroused the Bodhi Mind (*Bodhicitta*), which is named "Going Out of the Three Worlds." But then they retreated/regressed, which is named "Entering." Like a person lifting a foot to step out the door but then turning back—this represents both "Going Out" and "Returning/Entering."<br>
2.  **Rational Nature View:** The Principle Nature (*Lǐ Xìng*) is originally pure and not a dharma of the Three Worlds. Because of Ignorance (*Avidyā*), prapañca/playful discourse arises, leading to Birth and Death. Thus it says "Because of playful games, they came and entered."<br>
**"Ignorant" (*Wú Zhī*):** Great Good not yet attached = "Young/Small" (*Zhì Xiǎo*). Covered by Ignorance = "Unknowing."<br>
**"Hearing this, he was startled/alarmed":** (2 phrases). Praises the "Great Fear" which gives rise to "Great Compassion."
</div>
</div>

### 4. Bestowing the Great Cart (Lines 82-89)

<div class="bilingual-row">
<div class="chinese-source">
從「長者見子得出火宅」下，第三，有十七行偈，頌上第三等賜大車譬。上文有四：一免難，二索車，三等賜大車，四得車歡喜。今頌亦四：初五[5]行頌免難歡喜；[6]第二，三行，頌索車，[7]第三，七行半，頌等賜，第四，一行半，頌得車歡喜...

<div class="english-translation">
**From "The Householder saw the sons get out of the Burning House...":** (17 verses).<br>
Praises the third part: **Equally Bestowing the Great Cart**.<br>
Divided into four:<br>
1.  **First 5 Verses:** Praises **Escaping Disaster & Rejoicing**.<br>
    *   **"Sitting on the Lion Seat":**<br>
        *   *Interpretation 1:* The Sons sit on the seat (Fearless because out of Three Worlds).<br>
        *   *Interpretation 2:* The Elder sits on the seat. (The Elder is fearless because the sons are saved). Initially "Standing" (still worried), now "Sitting" (Fearless/Happy).<br>

# CHAPTER 4: BELIEF AND UNDERSTANDING (*Xìn Jiě Pǐn*)

## I. INTERPRETING THE TITLE (Lines 127-131; CBETA 006:127–131)

### 1. Critique of Old Definitions (Lines 131)

<div class="bilingual-row">
<div class="chinese-source">
有人言，信解三法，謂一往化、隨逐化、畢竟化... 私謂諸解重疊，玉屑非寶，夫一往非本懷，畢竟是宗極，說人天、二乘為一往，可非本懷... 若《法華》畢竟而更成一往，人天一往還成畢竟，則大顛倒...
</div>
<div class="english-translation">
**Previous Interpretations:**<br>
Some say "Belief and Understanding" involves three dharmas: (1) Temporary transformation, (2) Following transformation, (3) Ultimate transformation. (Listing various mappings of these three to Small/Large vehicles).<br>
**Tiantai Critique:**<br>
I say these explanations are repetitive; "Jade dust is not treasure."<br>
*   "Temporary" (*Yī Wǎng*) is not the Original Intent (*Běn Huái*).<br>
*   "Ultimate" (*Bì Jìng*) is the Doctrinal Summit (*Zōng Jí*).<br>
If you mix them up (saying Lotus Sutra is "Temporary" in some contexts), it becomes a "Great Inversion" (*Dà Diān Dǎo*). Also, if the Father/Son meeting is "Ultimate," then the former "Ultimate" is rendered useless. These explanations have flaws at every step; we do not use them.
</div>
</div>

### 2. Tiantai Definition of Belief and Understanding

<div class="bilingual-row">
<div class="chinese-source">
有人言，此品是迹。何者？如來成道已久，乃至中間中止亦是迹耳... 今釋品者，夫根有利鈍、惑有厚薄，說有法譬，悟有前後。法華座前猶如豌豆，文云「如來說法既久...」 初聞略說動執生疑，廣聞五佛蒙籠未曉，今聞譬喻歡喜踊躍，信發解生疑去理明。歡喜是世界，信生是為人，疑去是對治，理明是第一義... 稟小大教，初革凡成聖各有次位，但小乘信行從聞生解，苦忍明發信則稱行；法行歷法觀察，苦忍明發法則稱行。若信行人，轉入修道轉名信解；法行人入修道，轉名見得。準小望大亦應如此。中根之人聞說譬喻，初破疑惑入大乘見道故名為信，進入大乘修道故名為解。文云「無上寶聚不求自得，我等今日真是聲聞。」以佛道聲令一切聞，聞圓教入圓位，故名「信解品」。本迹者，四大弟子久入大乘成就佛法，迹引中根示初信解，故名「信解品」。
</div>
<div class="english-translation">
**Trace vs. Origin:**<br>
Some say this Chapter is "Trace" (*Jī*). Why? Because the Tathagata attained the Way long ago. (Tiantai agrees: In principle yes, but textually the "Origin" hasn't been revealed yet, so technically this is leading Middle Roots).<br>
**Defining Meaning:**<br>
*   **Roots:** Sharp vs. Blunt.<br>
*   **Defilements:** Thick vs. Thin.<br>
*   **Teaching:** Dharma vs. Parable.<br>
*   **Realization:** Early vs. Late.<br>
**The Process:**<br>
Before the Lotus Seat, they were like peas (hard/unreceptive). Upon hearing the "Brief Explanation" (Expedient Means), they doubted. Hearing "Five Buddhas," they were foggy. Now, hearing the "Parable," they rejoice.<br>
*   **Faith arises, Understanding is born, Doubt departs, Principle clears.**<br>
*   **Four Siddhāntas:** Rejoicing (Worldly); Faith born (Individual); Doubt removed (Therapeutic); Principle clear (Primary Meaning).<br>
**Faith-Row vs. Dharma-Row (*Śraddhānusārin / Dharmānusārin*):**<br>
*   **Small Vehicle:** Hearing -> Understanding = Faith Row. Observing Dharma = Dharma Row. Entering Cultivation = "Belief and Liberation" (*Adhimukti*).<br>
*   **Large Vehicle (Middle Root):** Hearing Parable breaks doubt enters Great Vehicle Path of Seeing = **Faith**. Entering Great Vehicle Path of Cultivation = **Understanding**.<br>
**Conclusion:** "Unsurpassed Jewel-Collection, obtained without seeking." Causing all to hear the Buddha-Way Sound (*Sheng*), hearing Perfect Teaching, entering Perfect Rank. Thus named "Belief and Understanding."

「其父先來求子不得」下，第二，求子中止... 「中止一城」者，不為一子而廢家業... 今取方便有餘土為「國」，在同居、實報兩間為「中」，有餘涅槃為「城」，住此涅槃名「止」。處此為家，起勝劣兩應... 「大富」者，實[2]相境為「家」... 「倉庫盈溢」者，在內為「盈」，在外為「溢」，盛米為「倉」，盛物為「庫」... 「僮僕」者，方便知見波羅蜜皆悉具足...

<div class="english-translation">
**"The Father previously sought the son but could not find him... Stopped in a City":**<br>
1.  **"Could Not Find":** Since retreating from the Great, the "Great Capacity" (*Dà Jī*) never met the Buddha.<br>
2.  **"Stopped in a City":**<br>
    *   *Old View:* Between two Buddhas.<br>
    *   *Tiantai View:* **Convenient With-Remainder Land** (*Fāngbiàn Yǒuyú Tǔ*) is the "Country." It is "Middle" (between Common Dwelling and Real Reward). **With-Remainder Nirvana** is the "City." Residing there is "Stop."<br>
    *   Here he raises Superior and Inferior Responses: Inferior for Sravakas, Superior for Bodhisattvas. Using this as a base ("House") to wait for the Son.<br>
**"Great Wealth":**<br>
*   **House:** Real Mark Realm (*Shí Xiàng Jìng*).<br>
*   **Rich:** Myriad Virtues.<br>
*   **Treasure:** Prajna Wisdom.<br>
*   **Warehouses Overflowing:** "Granary" (`Cāng`) = Dhyana (produces Samadhis). "Treasury" (`Kù`) = Real Mark (produces 18 Emptiness Wisdoms). Self-use = "Full." Teaching others = "Overflowing."<br>
**"Servants and Entourage":** (Expedient Knowledge/Paramitas).<br>
*   *Servants (*Tóng Pú*):* Two Vehicles, Pervasive/Separate Bodhisattvas (30 Minds).<br>
*   *Ministers (*Chén*):* 10 Grounds (*Bhūmis*). (Assisting Buddha/Subduing Maras).<br>
*   *Assistants (*Zuǒ*):* 10 Dedications.<br>
*   *Clerks (*Lì*):* 10 Lines.<br>
*   *Citizens (*Mín*):* 10 Abodes (First entering Buddha realm).<br>
**"Elephants, Horses, Cattle, Sheep":** (Vehicles/Views).<br>
*   **Elephant:** One Mind Three Views (Perfect Teaching).<br>
*   **Horse:** Sequential Three Views (Separate Teaching).<br>
*   **Ox:** Immediate Emptiness / Analytical Emptiness (Pervasive Teaching).<br>
*   **Sheep/Deer:** Analytical View (Two Vehicles).<br>
**"Commercial Guests":** Bodhisattvas trading (moving between lands to convert beings). Or: Response/Manifestation bodies trading Real Dharma for sentient beings' conversion.

### 4. Father's Anxiety and Regret (Lines 183-200)

<div class="bilingual-row">
<div class="chinese-source">
「父每念子」下，第四，即是父憂念子譬... 「五十餘年」者，五道也... 「未曾向...說」... 「心懷[5]悔恨」... 「自念老朽」者，化期將畢無傳大法之人... 「復作是念我若得子」下，二、念得子為樂...
</div>
<div class="english-translation">
**"The Father always thinks of his son":**<br>
*   **"Always thinking":** Constantly considering the Great Capacity (*Dà Jī*) of the Son.<br>
*   **"50 Years":** Five Paths. "Over 50" includes Asuras (opening Ghost path).<br>
*   **"Never spoke to others":** Never told the "Ministers/Clerks" (Bodhisattvas of Convenient Land) that this Sravaka is originally a Son of the Great Vehicle.<br>
*   **"Regret and Hate" (*Huǐ Hèn*):** Regretting not teaching strictly enough in the past (allowing him to run away). Hating that the son lacks gratitude/mindfulness.<br>
*   **"Old and Decayed":** The transformation period (*Huà Qī*) is ending; no one to transmit the Great Law to. (Like being old without an heir).<br>
*   **"If I get my son...":** If I find a capacity that can be crossed over (*Dù*), I can confer the Dharma Wealth.

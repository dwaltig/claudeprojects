# The Profound Meaning of the Lotus Sutra (妙法蓮華經玄義)
## Fascicle 6, Part 1: Resonance Subtlety (感應妙) — Why Practice Works

**Practitioner Translation by The Practitioner**

---

> *"Every bow of the head and raising of the hand—merit is not discarded in vain. Though all day without visible response, all day without regret."*

---

## Why Resonance?

After the Three Dharmas (what we abide in), Master Zhiyi turns to **Resonance** (感應)—the dynamic relationship between practitioner effort and Buddha response.

This answers the question every meditator asks: **"Is this doing anything?"**

---

## The Two Terms

### Machine (機) — Your Side

"Machine" is the classic Buddhist translation for the Chinese 機, meaning something like "trigger mechanism" or "capacity." It has three meanings:

| Meaning | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Subtle** (微) | Like the first tiny movement that triggers an arrow—your spiritual readiness is subtle, almost invisible |
| **Hinge** (關) | Your good and evil "hinge" on the sage's compassion—you're connected to Buddhist response |
| **Suitability** (宜) | Your particular suffering is suitable for a particular type of help |

The *Yijing* says: "The machine is the subtlety of movement, the forerunner of fortune."

Like a crossbow: if the machine is ready and the archer pulls, the arrow flies. If you have "producible good" and the Buddha responds, good arises.

---

### Response (應) — Buddha's Side

"Response" also has three meanings:

| Meaning | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Going-to** (赴) | The sage comes to where your readiness is |
| **Matching** (對) | Like buyer and seller—if both agree, the transaction completes |
| **Responding** (應) | Giving what suits your situation |

Master Zhiyi's economic metaphor:

> "Sentient beings are like buyers; the Tathāgata is like the seller. The machine is the hinge; the response is the match."

When buyer and seller both say yes, the deal goes through.

---

## The Four Sentences

This is the pastoral heart of this teaching. Master Zhiyi explains **four types** of practice-response relationships:

### 1. Hidden Machine, Hidden Response (冥機冥應)

Your past good is working, but you don't see anything happening.

> "Though one does not visibly see spiritual response, one is **secretly benefited by Dharma-body**. Not seeing, not hearing, yet awakening and knowing."

You don't know it's working. But it is.

---

### 2. Hidden Machine, Manifest Response (冥機顯應)

Your past good ripens suddenly—you meet a teacher, hear the Dharma, something clicks.

> "When the Buddha first appeared, the first people liberated—had they practiced in the present? **Buddhas illuminate their past machines and go to liberate them.**"

This explains those "lucky" moments when things just fall into place spiritually. It's not luck—it's past causes coming to fruition.

---

### 3. Manifest Machine, Manifest Response (顯機顯應)

You practice now, and you see results now.

> "Like Sudatta kneeling long, Buddha went to Jetavana."

You do the practice, you get the response. This is what practitioners often expect—and sometimes get.

---

### 4. Manifest Machine, Hidden Response (顯機冥應)

You practice diligently now, but see no visible results. However, hidden benefit is accumulating.

> "Like a person who toils their whole life, present good thickly accumulated but no manifest response—**there is hidden benefit.**"

This is perhaps the most important teaching. Your practice is working even when you can't see it.

---

## The Pastoral Takeaway

> **"If you understand these four meanings, every bow of the head and raising of the hand—merit is not discarded in vain. Though all day without visible response, all day without regret."**

And the flip side:

> "If you see those who enjoy killing yet live long, or those who love giving yet are impoverished—**do not generate wrong views.**"

Don't be confused by apparent karmic inversions. The four sentences explain how timing works in ways we can't always see.

## Why Some Prayers "Work" and Others Don't

Master Zhiyi addresses this directly:

> "There is a machine of producible good... If the sage responds, good arises; if no response, it does not arise."

The sage always responds to what's ripe. But:
- Maybe your machine isn't ripe yet (hidden benefit coming later)
- Maybe the response is hidden (you're receiving benefit you can't perceive)
- Maybe the "answer" is different from what you expected

---

## Coarse and Subtle Response

What's the difference between a low-level response and a Buddha-level response?

### Coarse Response (麁應)

Tripiṭaka and Common Teaching responses are "intentional supernatural powers"—

> "Like paintings that require deliberate effort to complete, at core without foundation. Ash-body, extinguished-wisdom—from what does response arise?"

The practitioner with no constant-abiding foundation has to *work* at responding.

---

### Subtle Response (妙應)

Buddha-level response is like a mirror or moon:

> "Like the one moon does not descend, the hundred waters do not rise, yet following rivers' length, conforming to vessels' shapes, **without before or after, simultaneously universally manifesting**."

> "Like a bright mirror, clear inside and out—one image or a thousand images, **no selection, no effort required, naturally resembling**."

The Dharma-body doesn't "decide" to respond. Response happens naturally, like moonlight appearing in every body of water.

---

## Opening Coarse to Reveal Subtle

In the *Lotus Sūtra*, all partial responses become revealed as aspects of the ultimate response:

> "Three grasses and two trees are simply produced from **one ground**—same-source machine is one. Moistened by **one rain**—same-received response is one."

The seemingly different "levels" of Buddhist response are all emerging from the same source and nourished by the same compassion.

> "**Nine-realm machines are all Buddha-realm machines; responses of the four sages are nothing but subtle responses.**"

## Practice Prompt

**This week, work with "hidden benefit":**

### Days 1-2: Notice Hidden Machines
Reflect on times in your life when spiritual good "appeared" without obvious present-life cause. A book that found you at the right moment. A teacher who appeared. A practice that clicked.

These may be your hidden machines ripening.

### Days 3-4: Trust Hidden Response
When you practice and see no result, remember: hidden response is still response. Can you practice without demanding visible confirmation?

### Days 5-7: The Four Sentences
Each day, notice which of the four sentences applies to your practice:
- Am I experiencing hidden benefit right now without knowing it?
- Is past good ripening into present insight?
- Is my present effort producing visible fruit?
- Is my diligence creating hidden benefit for later?

**The key insight**: Response is always happening. The question is only whether you can perceive it—and whether you can trust it when you can't.

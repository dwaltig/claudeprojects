## The Buddha's Four Teaching Methods

Remember the Four Siddhāntas? Here they come again—but this time Master Zhiyi goes deep. He breaks them down **ten different ways**.

Why? Because if you really understand how the Buddha teaches, you'll understand:
1. Why *you* got the teaching you got
2. Why it worked (or didn't)
3. How to apply it yourself

---

## The Quick Reference

| Siddhānta | Meaning | Your Response |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| **Worldly** | Meet you where you are | Delight, engagement |
| **For Each Person** | Grow your good roots | Faith deepens |
| **Therapeutic** | Cure your specific disease | Afflictions weaken |
| **First Principle** | Point directly at reality | Breakthrough |

## Method 4: The Four Kinds of Four Noble Truths

This is deep Tiantai territory. There isn't just *one* version of the Four Noble Truths—there are **four versions**, one for each teaching level:

| Teaching | Version of Four Truths | Characteristic |
|----------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Tripiṭaka** | Arising-and-Ceasing | Things arise and cease; analyze them to enter emptiness |
| **Common** | Non-Arising | Things never truly arose; embody emptiness directly |
| **Distinct** | Immeasurable | Infinite skillful means for infinite beings |
| **Perfect** | Non-Created | Neither arising nor ceasing—already complete |

And each version links to a Siddhānta:
- Arising-and-Ceasing → **Worldly** (explain things conventionally)
- Non-Arising → **For Each Person** (what do *you* need?)
- Immeasurable → **Therapeutic** (the right cure for every disease)
- Non-Created → **First Principle** (pointing directly at it)

---

## Method 5: How Contemplation Arises

Here's the practical payoff: **how to actually use the Siddhāntas in meditation**.

### The Three Contemplations

| Contemplation | Direction | What It Develops |
|---------------|-----------|------------------|
| **Entering Emptiness from the Provisional** | From phenomena → emptiness | Wisdom Eye |
| **Entering the Provisional from Emptiness** | From emptiness → phenomena | Dharma Eye |
| **Middle Way First Principle** | Neither/both | Buddha Eye |

### Using the Siddhāntas in Practice

**1. Worldly Siddhānta — Get Started**

You want to contemplate dependent origination? First, you need *delight*. You need *earnestness*. If you're not engaged, the practice won't take.

> "If there is no earnestness and delight, the practice will not succeed."

This is why some people sit for years and nothing happens—they're going through the motions without the **joy**.

---

**2. For-Each-Person Siddhānta — Find Your Method**

Not everyone should do the same thing. Ask:
- Are you more stop-oriented or look-oriented?
- Do you need to *calm down* or *wake up*?

If you should **stop** (止): Use equanimity, exclusion, concentration.
If you should **look** (觀): Use discrimination, vigor, joy.

Mindfulness goes both ways.

---

**3. Therapeutic Siddhānta — Fix What's Broken**

Your mind has two main diseases:

| Disease | Symptoms | Treatment |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Sinking** (沈) | Dull, sleepy, heavy | Mindfulness, Discrimination, Vigor, Joy |
| **Floating** (浮) | Scattered, restless, spinning | Mindfulness, Equanimity, Exclusion, Concentration |

This is pure Seven Factors of Awakening technology.

---

**4. First Principle Siddhānta — Break Through**

When you've balanced your practice:

> "Following one factor of awakening, suddenly [insight arises] as if lost [to ordinary awareness]."

That's the moment. Don't chase it—stay with that factor. Research it. Cultivate it.

> "Relying on this factor for research and cultivation, one can generate true illumination and see the First Principle."

---

## Method 6: Holy Speech and Holy Silence

The Buddha has two modes:

1. **Holy Speech** — When teaching benefits beings
2. **Holy Silence** — When silence benefits beings

Why would silence benefit? Because some truths **cannot be spoken**. The Four Noble Truths at their ultimate? They're beyond words.

> "Even if spoken, it is like setting up a candle for blind people—what benefit is there for those without eyes?"

### The Lotus Sutra's Silence

This is why the Buddha hesitated:

> "Stop, stop—no need to speak. My Dharma is wonderful and hard to conceive."

> "This Dharma cannot be shown; the marks of speech are extinguished."

The Non-Created Four Truths—the Perfect Teaching's version—is "non-arising of non-arising." Try putting *that* into words.

**And yet the Buddha speaks anyway.** Because silence alone doesn't ferry beings. He finds the words that point beyond words.

## Method 7: Who Can Actually Use This?

Four possibilities:

| Status | Description | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Not attaining, not applying** | Don't even know the teaching exists | Ordinary beings, non-Buddhists |
| **Attaining but not applying** | Know truth but can't teach | Two Vehicle arhats |
| **Not attaining yet applying** | Teach without full realization | Tripiṭaka bodhisattvas |
| **Both attaining and applying** | Full realization + skillful teaching | Buddhas |

### Classic Failures

**Pūrṇa's Ninety Days**

The great teacher Pūrṇa spent ninety days trying to convert some non-Buddhists. They laughed at him. Then Mañjuśrī showed up for a brief visit—both teacher and students submitted immediately.

What happened? Pūrṇa didn't know what they *wanted*. He couldn't use the **Worldly Siddhānta**.

---

**Śāriputra's Two Students**

Śāriputra taught two disciples. Instead of developing good roots, they developed more doubts.

Why? He didn't match *their* needs. He couldn't use the **For-Each-Person Siddhānta**.

---

**Five Hundred Arhats vs. One Buddha**

Five hundred arhats taught Kātyāyana the Four Noble Truths. Nothing. Zero benefit. The Buddha taught him one impurity contemplation—boom, breakthrough.

Why? The arhats didn't diagnose the disease. They couldn't use the **Therapeutic Siddhānta**.

---

**Only Buddha Uses All Four**

Through the stages:
- **Tripiṭaka Two Vehicles**: Attain but don't apply
- **Common Teaching bodhisattvas at 7th Stage**: Finally beginning to apply skillfully
- **Distinct Teaching bodhisattvas**: Progressively better
- **Perfect Teaching disciples**: Stage by stage, approaching full application
- **Buddha**: **Ultimate attainment, ultimate application**

---

## Method 8: Provisional and Real

Here's the bottom line:

| Teaching | Siddhāntas | Status |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Tripiṭaka | All 4 | **Provisional** (targeting lesser capacities) |
| Common | All 4 | Still **Provisional** (even though skillful) |
| Distinct | All 4 | **Deep but still sequential** — Teaching is Provisional |
| **Perfect** | All 4 | **REAL** — Perfectly fused, Wonderful |

Only in the **Perfect Teaching** are the Four Siddhāntas fully Real.

### The Five Flavors Summary

| Flavor | Provisional | Real |
|--------|-------------|------|
| Milk (*Avataṃsaka*) | 4 | 4 |
| Curds (*Tripiṭaka*) | 4 | 0 |
| Fresh Butter (*Vaipulya*) | 12 | 4 |
| Clarified Butter (*Prajñā*) | 8 | 4 |
| **Ghee (*Lotus*)** | **0** | **4** |

The Lotus Sutra has **no Provisional**. All four Siddhāntas are Real and Wonderful.

---

## Method 9: Opening the Provisional

> "All dharmas without exception are all Wonderful. **One form, one fragrance—none is not the Middle Way.** It is only that sentient beings' feelings are cut off from the Wonderful."

The teaching isn't *making* things Wonderful. Things already *are* Wonderful. The teaching opens your eyes.

That's what "Opening the Provisional to Reveal the Real" means:
- The Conjured City gets eliminated → **Determining the coarse**
- Everyone reaches the Treasure Site → **Entering the Wonderful**

This is why the *Lotus* says:

> "The twelve hundred arhats shall all become Buddhas."

Not "might become" or "should try to become." **Shall become.** The Provisional has been opened. The Real was always there.

---

## Method 10: Find It in the Sūtra

Master Zhiyi pulls two passages to prove the Four Siddhāntas are embedded in the Lotus:

### From "Skillful Means" (Chapter 2)

> "Knowing the various practices of sentient beings, what their deep minds contemplate, the karmas of past cultivation, their desires, natures, vigor and power, and whether their faculties are sharp or dull—with various causes and conditions, similes and words, I teach according to appropriate skillful means."

Break it down:
- **Desires** = Worldly
- **Natures** = For Each Person
- **Vigor and power** = Therapeutic
- **Faculties sharp or dull** = First Principle

All four. Right there.

### From "Lifespan" (Chapter 16)

> "The Tathāgata clearly sees... Because sentient beings have various natures, various desires, various practices, and various recollections and discriminations... I teach the Dharma in various ways."

Same four:
- **Various natures** = For Each Person
- **Various desires** = Worldly
- **Various practices** = Therapeutic
- **Various recollections** = Leading to First Principle

The Buddha's been using the Four Siddhāntas all along. Now you can see it.

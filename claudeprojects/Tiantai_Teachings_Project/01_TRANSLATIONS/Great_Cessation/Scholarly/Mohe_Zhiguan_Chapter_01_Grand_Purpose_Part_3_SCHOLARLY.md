1.  **The Great Net:** This refers to the vast, confusing array of Buddhist sutras (84,000 teachings). Without the "Key" (Zhiguan/Middle Way), one gets tangled in the net, thinking "This sutra contradicts that one." Rending the Net means seeing the unity (One Vehicle) behind the diversity.
2.  **The Secret Store (*MÃ¬mÃ¬ ZÃ ng*):** A term from the *Nirvana Sutra*. It refers to the **Three Virtues** (*SÄndÃ©*): Dharma Body (*FÇshÄ“n*), Wisdom (*BÅrÄ›*), and Liberation (*JiÄ›tuÅ*). They are "Secret" not because they are hidden, but because they are too profound for the deluded mind to see. They are a "Store" because they contain all dharmas.
3.  **New vs. Old:** Master Zhiyi uses a brilliant dialectic here. Conventional practice moves from "Old" (Defilement) to "New" (Enlightenment). But from the view of the Perfect Teaching, the Defilement *is* the Enlightenment. Thus, the "New" is not new (it was always there), and the "Old" is not old (it has no substance). This is the **"Identity of Defilement and Bodhi."**

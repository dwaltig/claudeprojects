## üìú Doctrinal Analysis

1.  **The Four Samadhis:** This classification is Zhiyi's grand synthesis of Buddhist practice.
    *   **Constant Sitting:** Correspond to Zen/Chan (Dhyana-focused).
    *   **Constant Walking:** Corresponds to Pure Land (Amitabha-focused).
    *   **Half/Half:** Corresponds to Esoteric/Ritual practice (Mantra/Repentance).
    *   **Neither/Nor:** Corresponds to "Daily Life Zen" or the "Direct Path."
2.  **Following One's Own Mind (*Su√≠z√¨y√¨*):** This is the most radical and advanced practice. It allows the practitioner to use *any* mental state‚Äîeven anger or lust‚Äîas the object of meditation. By applying the "Four Phases," one realizes that the defilement has no substance.
3.  **The Four Phases (*S√¨ Y√πn*):** This analytic tool deconstructs the continuity of time. By looking for the "mind" in the past (not yet), present (desiring), or future (ended), one fails to find it. This "failure to find" (*p«íd√©*) is the realization of Emptiness.

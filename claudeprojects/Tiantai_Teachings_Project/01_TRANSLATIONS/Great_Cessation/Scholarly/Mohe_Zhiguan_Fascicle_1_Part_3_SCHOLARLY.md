1.  **The "Golden Mouth" (é‡‘å£):** This term emphasizes that the three-fold Zhiguan is not Zhiyi's invention but is grounded in the Buddha's own words across the various periods of his teaching.
2.  **The Universality of the Three Kinds:** By citing the five major sutras (Vimalakirti, Prajnaparamita, Lotus, Nirvana, Avatamsaka), Zhiyi demonstrates that the Gradual, Variable, and Sudden structures are inherent in the totality of the Buddha's pedagogical career.
3.  **The Nature of "Variable" (ä¸å®š):** The proofs for 'Variable' often use metaphors of adaptation (changing color, one voice with many meanings). This highlights that the "Variable" is not a separate set of doctrines but a flexible *delivery* of the Gradual and Sudden.
4.  **Cessation and Contemplation as "Teacher of Buddhas":** This is a profound claim. It suggests that Zhiguan (the union of stillness and illumination) is the ontological ground from which all Buddhas arise. It is the "Mother of Buddhas" (*Buddha-mÄtá¹›*).
5.  **Alignment with the Five Flavors:** The milk-to-ghee metaphor from the *Nirvana Sutra* is the classic Tiantai framework for the "Five Flavors" (Dairy) of the Dharma, used here to validate the Gradual approach.

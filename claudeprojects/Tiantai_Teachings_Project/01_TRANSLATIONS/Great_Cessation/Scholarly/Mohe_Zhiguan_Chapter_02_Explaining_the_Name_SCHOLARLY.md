## üìú Doctrinal Analysis

1.  **The Threefold Definition:** Zhiyi consistently uses a threefold structure: Action (Stopping evil), Stability (Abiding in truth), and Ontology (The nature of Reality vs. Illusion). This ensures 'Zhiguan' covers ethics, concentration, and wisdom.
2.  **Relative vs. Absolute:** This is the core Tiantai dialectic. 'Relative' implies duality (Light vs. Dark). 'Absolute' transcends duality. But true Absolute is not separate from the Relative; it is the *opening* of the Relative to reveal its Empty nature.
3.  **The Letter 'I' (‚à¥):** The Sanskrit *Siddha·πÉ* 'I' (three dots in a triangle) is the standard Tiantai metaphor for the **Three Truths** or **Three Virtues**. They are distinct (three dots) but inseparable (one letter). If you remove one dot, it is no longer 'I'.

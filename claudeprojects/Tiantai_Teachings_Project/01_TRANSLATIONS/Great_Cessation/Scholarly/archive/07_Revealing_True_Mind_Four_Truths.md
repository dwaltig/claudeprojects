**1. Born and Perishing:**
Suffering and Accumulation are worldly cause and effect; the Path and Extinction are out-of-worldly cause and effect.
*   **Suffering:** Characterized by the migration of the three marks (birth, abiding, perishing).
*   **Accumulation:** Characterized by the flow of the four minds (craving, etc.).
*   **Path:** Characterized by counteraction and replacement (curing illness).
*   **Extinction:** Characterized by extinguishing existence and returning to nothingness.
Although they are worldly and out-of-worldly, all are changing and mutating, hence named the **Born and Perishing Four Truths** (corresponds to Tripitaka/Storehouse Teaching).

**2. Unborn (and Imperishing):**
*   **Suffering has no oppression:** All things are empty; how can emptiness expel emptiness? Form is immediately emptiness; feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness are also like this. Therefore, there is no characteristic of oppression.
*   **Accumulation has no characteristic of combination:** Cause and effect are both empty; how can an empty cause combine with an empty effect?
*   **Path has no characteristic of duality:** There is no subject to cure and no object to be cured. Emptiness does not even have 'one,' how can there be 'two'?
*   **Extinction:** Dharmas are originally not so (not arisen); now there is no extinction. Not arisen and not extinguished, hence named the **Unborn Four Truths** (corresponds to Connecting Teaching).

**3. Immeasurable:**
Distinguishing and calculating that Suffering has immeasurable characteristics. Even the suffering of one Dharma-realm is manifold; how much more so the Ten Dharma-realms? This variety is not something the wisdom or eyes of the Two Vehicles can know or see; it is what the Bodhisattva can clearly understand. From the specific sufferings of Hell (peeling, cutting, burning) to the various skandhas of other realmsâ€”numerous as dust and sandâ€”this is the **Immeasurable Four Truths** (corresponds to Separate Teaching).

All are the Real Mark (*ShÃ­xiÃ ng*), inconceivable. Not only is the Supreme Truth devoid of plurality, but the Three Siddhantas and all dharmas are also devoid of plurality. This meaning can be known [from the implication]. This is the **No-Function Four Truths** (corresponds to Perfect Teaching).

**Vertical Alignment with Lands:**
If we align the Four Truths vertically with the [Four] Lands:
*   **Land of Co-dwelling:** Has all four (Born/Perishing, Unborn, Immeasurable, No-Function).
*   **Land of Expedient Means:** Has three (Unborn, Immeasurable, No-Function).
*   **Land of Real Retribution:** Has two (Immeasurable, No-Function).
*   **Land of Eternal Quiescence:** Has only one (No-Function)."

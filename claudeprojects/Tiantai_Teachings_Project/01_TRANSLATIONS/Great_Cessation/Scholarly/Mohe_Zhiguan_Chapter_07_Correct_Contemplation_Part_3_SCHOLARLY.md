We apply the 'Four Phrase' analysis (*S√¨ J√π*) to every attachment. For example, if one is attached to the idea that 'Wisdom arises from the Mind':
1.  Does it arise from **Self** (The Mind itself)? (If so, it would always be wise).

**D. Breaking the 'Good' Dharmas:**
It is easy to see why we break evil (Greed, Anger). But we must also break the attachment to *Good*. If one is attached to Dhyana, to Wisdom, or even to Nirvana, this attachment becomes a 'Thorn in the Flesh.' The Diamond Wisdom must grind it all to dust.
'If you see the Buddha, kill the Buddha.' (Meaning: Kill the *attachment* to the concept of Buddha)."

## üìú Doctrinal Analysis

1.  **Universal Breaking:** This is the apophatic (negative) side of Tiantai. While we affirm the "Inconceivable Object" (Cataphatic/Positive), we must ruthlessly negate any *concept* we form about it.
2.  **The Tetralemma:** This is the standard Madhyamaka tool. Zhiyi uses it not just to prove emptiness, but to clear the ground for the "Middle Way."
3.  **Breaking the Raft:** The raft is useful for crossing the river, but if you carry it on your back after crossing, it becomes a burden. "Breaking Dharmas" means letting go of the raft *while* still crossing (non-attachment in action).

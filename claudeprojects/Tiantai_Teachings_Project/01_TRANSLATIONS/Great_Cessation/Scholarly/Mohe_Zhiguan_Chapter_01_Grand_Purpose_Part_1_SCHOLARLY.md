## ğŸ“œ Doctrinal Analysis

1.  **The Ten Minds:** This classification allows the practitioner to self-diagnose. It moves from the "Three Evil Paths" (Hell/Ghost/Animal) to the "Three Good Paths" (Human/Asura/Heaven), then to the "Heretical Paths" (Mara/Nirgrantha), and finally to the "Hinayana Paths" (Two Vehicles). All are rejected as *not* the "Great Mind" (Mahayana Bodhicitta).
2.  **Five Caá¹‡á¸Älas (äº”æ‰‡æç¾…):** *Caá¹‡á¸Äla* refers to an outcast or butcher class in ancient India, often used metaphorically for those who commit the Five Grave Offenses (killing father, mother, Arhat, shedding Buddha's blood, causing schism). Here it signifies the most depraved state of mind (Hell).
3.  **The Four Truths:** Master Zhiyi aligns the Four Truths with his "Four Teachings" (Tripitaka, Connecting, Separate, Perfect). This is a hallmark of Tiantai hermeneuticsâ€”the same doctrine (Four Truths) has four levels of depth.
4.  **The Six Identities:** This is one of the most critical Tiantai doctrines. It solves the tension between "Original Enlightenment" (we are already Buddhas in principle) and "Acquired Enlightenment" (we must practice to realize it). It prevents both laziness ("I'm already Buddha, no need to sit") and discouragement ("Buddhahood is too far away").

### 1. The Three Stops (How to Quit)

"Sometimes you just need to quit. But quitting isn't simple.
1.  **The Truth Stop (Realizing it's Fake):** You stop chasing the world because you realize it's all a hustle. It's empty. It's like realizing the gold is just painted lead. You stop buying it. (This brings Peace).
2.  **The Engagement Stop (Using the Fake):** You know it's fake, but you use it anyway to help people. You stop *hating* the world. You stop trying to escape into nothingness. You rest in the 'Action.' (This brings Compassion).
3.  **The Middle Stop (Resting in Reality):** You stop bouncing back and forth between 'It's Real' and 'It's Fake.' You stop the pendulum. You rest in the Center, where everything is just As It Is.

**The Perfect Way:** You do all three at once. You see the fake, you use the fake, and you rest in the Truth, all in one breath."

### 2. The Three Looks (How to See)

"And there are three ways to look at the world:
1.  **The X-Ray (From Fake to Empty):** You look at a table, a problem, or a person, and you see right through them to the Emptiness inside. You see the atoms, the causes, the lack of a 'self.' This cures your addiction to things.
2.  **The Movie (From Empty to Fake):** Once you know the screen is white (Empty), you can enjoy the movie (Fake) without getting tricked. You look at the differencesâ€”this medicine cures this, that medicine cures that. You become an expert in the 'Illusion.' This cures your stupidity.
3.  **The Reality (The Middle Way):** You look at the screen *and* the movie at the same time. You see that the Movie *is* the Screen. The suffering *is* the Nirvana. This cures the deepest delusion of all."

### 3. The One Mind (The Punchline)

"In the end, Master Zhiyi says: **Don't make this a ladder.**
Don't say, 'First I'll do X-Ray, then in ten years I'll do The Movie.'
Do it now.
In one single thought:
*   It's Empty (No solid self).
*   It's Fake (It's happening right now).
*   It's Middle (It's the Truth).

That's the **Substance** of the practice. It's not a technique; it's a view."

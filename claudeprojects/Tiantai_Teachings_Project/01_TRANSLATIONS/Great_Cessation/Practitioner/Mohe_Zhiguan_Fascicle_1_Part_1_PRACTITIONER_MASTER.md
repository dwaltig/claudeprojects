# Mohe Zhiguan - Fascicle 1: The Three Kinds of Zhiguan (Blues)

## üßò The Practitioner's Interpretation: "Three Ways to Walk the Walk"

The Master taught us three ways to get to the Truth. They're all the Big Vehicle, they're all looking at the same Moon, but the walk feels different.

**1. The Staircase (Gradual)**
This is for folks who need to see where they're putting their feet. You start at the bottom.
*   First, you stop killing and hating. You act like a decent human being. That gets you out of the mud.
*   Then you learn to sit still. You calm that monkey mind. That gets you some peace.
*   Then you realize this world is a prison, and you find the key to get out (Nirvana).
*   Then you realize getting out alone isn't enough, so go back for your friends (Compassion).
*   Finally, you see the whole picture‚Äîthe Truth that doesn't change.
It's a long climb, step by step. Shallow water first, deep water later.

**2. The Diamond (Variable)**
This is the jazz improvisation. No fixed setlist. Sometimes you play a high note, sometimes low. Sometimes the simplest thing (like drinking tea) is the Highest Truth. Sometimes the Highest Truth is just a remedy for a headache. It sparkles differently depending on how the sun hits it. You can't pin it down.

**3. The Rocket (Perfect/Sudden)**
This is the one we're here for. This is the main event.
You don't climb. You don't improvise. You fly.
From the very first thought, you're right in the center of the Truth.
*   You look at a flower, a piece of trash, or your own messy mind, and you say: "This is it. This is the Middle Way."
*   You don't try to throw away your suffering, because suffering is just the flip side of Wisdom.
*   You don't try to cut off your ignorance, because ignorance is the fuel for Enlightenment.
*   Samsara (this mess) *is* Nirvana (the peace).
There's no "world" to leave, and no "heaven" to go to. It's just One Real Thing, right here, right now.
Quiet as a mountain (Stopping). Bright as the sun (Looking).
Start to finish‚Äîno difference. That's the Perfect Way.

The Master laid out the whole journey in ten stops. It‚Äôs a perfect number‚Äînot a note missing, not a word extra. It starts with the mountain peak in view and ends with you standing right on top of it.

Here‚Äôs the setlist:

6.  **Getting Ready (Expedients):** You get your tools in order based on that big truth.

# Mohe Zhiguan - Fascicle 1: The Five Objectives of the Grand Purpose (Blues)

## üßò The Practitioner's Interpretation: "The Five Big Moves"

The Master said the "Grand Purpose" is huge‚Äîit covers everything from the first step to the last breath. It's so big you might miss it, so he broke it down into five big moves.

**1. Wake Up Big (Arousing the Great Mind)**
Most folks are sleepwalking, upside down and backwards. They don't even know they're asleep. The first move is to shake them awake. You look up to the Truth, and you reach down to help your neighbor. You decide to wake up for everybody's sake.

**2. Walk Big (Cultivating the Great Practice)**
It's not enough to just *want* to go home. If you stare at the road but don't move your feet, you'll never get there. You gotta walk. You gotta sweat. We call this "The Four Samadhis"‚Äîfour ways to sit, walk, and stand until your mind stops jumping.

**3. Harvest Big (Feeling the Great Fruit)**
You don't do this for a reward, but the reward comes anyway. You don't ask for heaven, but heaven shows up. We talk about the "Marvelous Payoff" just to keep your heart glad when the road gets rough.

**4. Cut the Net (Rending the Great Net)**
The world is full of books and theories. They open your eyes, but they also trap you. You get stuck saying "This is right, that is wrong." You hear "snow" and think "cold," but you've never touched it. You hear "crane" and think "flying," but you've never seen it. This teaching cuts through all that tangled mess. It unties the knots and lets the bird out of the cage.

**5. Go Home Big (Returning to the Great Abode)**
Where does it all end? In the Great Place.
There's no start line and no finish line. There's no wall and no door. When you see the Real World (Dharma-realm), you see it's always been wide open. Clear, free, and easy. That's where we live.
# Mohe Zhiguan - Fascicle 1: Arousing the Great Mind (Practitioner)

## üßò The Practitioner's Interpretation: "Checking Your Engine"

Before we start the car, we gotta check the engine.
"Bodhi" means the Way. "Citta" means the Mind‚Äînot the pumping heart or the physical brain, but the *Thinking Mind* that knows what's going on.

The Master says before we find the True Mind, we have to throw out the junk. He lists ten kinds of "False Minds." These are the engines that will drive you off a cliff. Check yourself: are you running on one of these?

1.  **The Hell Engine:** All you think about is hate, greed, and stupidity. You can't stop it, you can't pull it out. You're just burning fuel to go straight to the fire.
2.  **The Animal Engine:** You just want a pack. You want followers. You want to swallow people up like the ocean swallows rivers. That's the road of blood.
3.  **The Ghost Engine:** You want fame. You want people in the four corners of the world to sing your name. Inside, you're empty; outside, you pretend to be a saint. That's the road of knives.
4.  **The Asura (Titan) Engine:** You always have to win. You can't stand being second. You look down on people like a hawk. On the outside, you talk about "justice" and "fairness," but inside, it's just pride.
5.  **The Human Engine:** You just want to be comfortable. You want to keep this stinking body safe and this confused mind happy. It's not evil, but it's just... worldly.
6.  **The Heavenly Engine:** You want pure pleasure. You shut your eyes and ears to the world's mess so you can float in a bubble of bliss.
7.  **The Demon Engine:** You want power. You want it so that when you snap your fingers, everybody jumps. You want control.
8.  **The Smart-Aleck Engine:** You want to be the smartest guy in the room. You want to win every debate and have the whole world look at you and say, "Wow, he's brilliant."
9.  **The Zen Engine:** You want high-class meditation states. You think the world is too noisy, so you retreat into deep, heavy internal silence.
10. **The Escape Engine (Two Vehicles):** You see the whole cycle as a trap. You want out. You're hungry for purity, hungry for silence, hungry to just be *done* with it all.

**The Verdict:**
None of these are the Great Mind.
Some are evil, some are good, some are spiritual. But they are all *partial*. If you're running on these, you aren't ready for the Big Vehicle. You have to turn these engines off before you can start the real one.
# Mohe Zhiguan - Fascicle 1: Revealing the True Mind (Practitioner)

## üßò The Practitioner's Interpretation: "Four Ways to See the Truth"

Once you've cleared out the junk engines, you need to know what "Truth" actually looks like. The Master says there isn't just one way to see the Four Noble Truths. There are four levels, like four different lenses.

**1. The Changing Truth (Born and Perishing)**
This is the standard view. Everything is changing.
*   Suffering is real and it hurts.
*   The Cause is real and it pushes you.
*   The Path is a cure you have to take.
*   Extinction (Nirvana) is wiping out the fire.
It works, but it's all about change. You're trying to fix a broken world.

**2. The Empty Truth (Unborn)**
This is the next level deep. You look at the suffering and realize... it's empty.
*   Suffering can't push you because there's no "you" to push.
*   Causes can't combine because they're empty shadows.
*   The Path isn't a cure, because there was never really a sickness.
*   Nirvana isn't "wiping out" anything, because nothing was ever really "born."
It's chill. It's quiet. It's the "Unborn."

**3. The Infinite Truth (Immeasurable)**
Now you stop looking at just yourself and look at the whole universe.
*   Suffering isn't just "pain"‚Äîit's a million different kinds of hells, ghosts, humans, and gods.
*   The Cause isn't just "greed"‚Äîit's infinite threads of karma.
*   The Path isn't just "meditation"‚Äîit's infinite ways to help infinite beings.
*   The Doctors (Bodhisattvas) need to know every single disease and every single medicine.
This is the big job. It's endless.

**4. The Natural Truth (No-Function)**
This is the ultimate.
*   You don't "fix" the world (Changing).
*   You don't "empty" the world (Empty).
*   You don't "count" the world (Infinite).
*   You see that the world *is* the Truth.
Suffering *is* Bodhi. Samsara *is* Nirvana. There's no "function," no effort to change anything into anything else. It's just Real. It's inconceivable.

**The Map of the Lands:**
*   Down here in our messy world (Co-dwelling), people teach all four views depending on what you need.
*   In the pure lands of the Saints (Expedient), they don't teach the first one anymore.
*   In the Bodhisattva lands (Real Retribution), they only deal with the Infinite and the Natural.
*   In the Buddha's home (Eternal Quiescence), there is only one Truth: The Natural one. No-Function. Just Being.
# Mohe Zhiguan - Fascicle 1: The Four Vows and Six Identities (Practitioner)

## üßò The Practitioner's Interpretation: "The Vow and the Roadmap"

After we see the Truth, we have to make a promise (The Vow) and then look at the map (The Six Identities) so we don't get lost or cocky.

**The Four Vows (The Big Promise)**
Understanding the Truth isn't enough. We've been spinning in circles because we didn't get it. Now we see the house is burning. We can't just sit there playing games. We have to make the Big Move: "I promise to fix this mess for myself and everyone else."

**The Six Identities (The "You Are It" Map)**
People get confused.
*   Some say: "I'm just a dirtbag, I can never be a Buddha." (That's fear).
*   Some say: "I'm already a Buddha, I don't need to practice." (That's arrogance).

The Master laid out the Six Identities to cure both sicknesses. It says: **You are Buddha right now (so don't be afraid), but you have a long way to go (so don't be cocky).**

1.  **Identity in Principle (The Factory Setting):**
    Right here, right now, your mind *is* the Buddha-mind. It has the factory setting of perfection. Even if you're thinking about lunch, that thought has the structure of the Universe. You *are* It.

2.  **Identity in Name (Hearing the News):**
    You didn't know you were It until someone told you. Now you've heard the words. You understand the idea. "Oh, I'm a Buddha." That's great, but it's just words.

3.  **Identity in Practice (Walking the Talk):**
    Words are cheap. If you just talk about it, you're like a bug chewing on wood that accidentally spells a word. You have to sit down and do the work. Your actions have to match your mouth. This is where the rubber meets the road.

4.  **Identity in Resemblance (Getting Warm):**
    You've been practicing. It's starting to feel real. You aren't hitting the bullseye yet, but you're hitting the target. Your senses act clean; you don't get snagged on things easily. You *look* like a Buddha, but you're still practicing.

5.  **Identity in Partial Truth (The Real Deal Begins):**
    Boom. You hit the bullseye. You break through the fog of ignorance. You see the Buddha-nature directly. This is the First Abode. You are a "Partial Buddha." You're the real moon, even if you're just a crescent.

6.  **Identity in Ultimate (Full Moon):**
    The crescent grows until it's full. No more darkness. No more path. Just the full, bright, round perfection. That's the Fruit.

**Practitioner's Note:**
Most of us are somewhere between #2 and #3. We know the words, and we're trying to practice. Don't let anyone tell you you're #6 just because you read a book. And don't let anyone tell you you're trash, because your factory setting is #1.

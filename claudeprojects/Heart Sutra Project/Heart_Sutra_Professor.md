# Heart Sutra - The Prajñāpāramitā Hṛdaya Sūtra

**Sanskrit Title:** Prajñāpāramitā Hṛdaya Sūtra (般若波羅蜜多心經)  
**English:** The Heart of the Perfection of Wisdom Sutra  
**Translators:** Dr. Mei-Ling Chen (陳美玲) & William Altig

---

## Agent 1: The Professor (Literal Scholarly Translation)

### Opening: Avalokiteśvara's Realization

**觀自在菩薩。行深般若波羅蜜多時。照見五蘊皆空。度一切苦厄。**

- 觀自在菩薩 (guān zìzài púsà) - Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva
- 行深般若波羅蜜多時 (xíng shēn bōrě bōluómìduō shí) - when practicing deep prajñāpāramitā
- 照見 (zhào jiàn) - illuminated and saw
- 五蘊 (wǔ yùn) - five aggregates (*pañca-skandha*)
- 皆空 (jiē kōng) - all empty (*śūnya*)
- 度一切苦厄 (dù yīqiè kǔ è) - crossed over all suffering and difficulty

**Literal Translation:**
"Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, when practicing deep Prajñāpāramitā, illuminated and saw [that] the five aggregates are all empty, [and thus] crossed over all suffering and difficulty."

---

### Teaching to Śāriputra: Form is Emptiness

**舍利子。色不異空。空不異色。色即是空。空即是色。受想行識。亦復如是。**

- 舍利子 (shèlìzǐ) - Śāriputra
- 色 (sè) - form (*rūpa*)
- 不異 (bù yì) - not different from
- 空 (kōng) - emptiness (*śūnyatā*)
- 即是 (jí shì) - precisely is
- 受想行識 (shòu xiǎng xíng shì) - feeling, perception, formations, consciousness
- 亦復如是 (yì fù rúshì) - also thus

**Literal Translation:**
"Śāriputra! Form is not different from emptiness; emptiness is not different from form. Form precisely is emptiness; emptiness precisely is form. Feeling, perception, formations, consciousness—also thus."

---

### The Characteristics of Emptiness

**舍利子。是諸法空相。不生不滅。不垢不淨。不增不減。**

- 是諸法空相 (shì zhū fǎ kōng xiàng) - these dharmas' empty characteristics
- 不生不滅 (bù shēng bù miè) - not born, not destroyed
- 不垢不淨 (bù gòu bù jìng) - not defiled, not pure
- 不增不減 (bù zēng bù jiǎn) - not increased, not decreased

**Literal Translation:**
"Śāriputra! These dharmas' empty characteristics [are]: not born, not destroyed; not defiled, not pure; not increased, not decreased."

---

### The Negations: No Five Aggregates

**是故空中無色。無受想行識。**

**Literal Translation:**
"Therefore, in emptiness [there is] no form, no feeling, perception, formations, consciousness."

---

### No Sense Faculties, Objects, or Consciousness

**無眼耳鼻舌身意。無色聲香味觸法。無眼界。乃至無意識界。**

- 眼耳鼻舌身意 (yǎn ěr bí shé shēn yì) - eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, mind
- 色聲香味觸法 (sè shēng xiāng wèi chù fǎ) - form, sound, smell, taste, touch, dharmas
- 眼界 (yǎn jiè) - eye-realm (*cakṣur-dhātu*)
- 意識界 (yì shì jiè) - consciousness-realm (*vijñāna-dhātu*)

**Literal Translation:**
"No eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, mind; no form, sound, smell, taste, touch, dharmas; no eye-realm, up to no consciousness-realm."

---

### No Twelve Links of Dependent Origination

**無無明。亦無無明盡。乃至無老死。亦無老死盡。**

- 無明 (wúmíng) - ignorance (*avidyā*)
- 無明盡 (wúmíng jìn) - exhaustion of ignorance
- 老死 (lǎo sǐ) - old age and death (*jarā-maraṇa*)

**Literal Translation:**
"No ignorance, also no exhaustion of ignorance; up to no old age and death, also no exhaustion of old age and death."

---

### No Four Noble Truths, No Wisdom, No Attainment

**無苦集滅道。無智亦無得。以無所得故。**

- 苦集滅道 (kǔ jí miè dào) - suffering, origin, cessation, path (Four Noble Truths)
- 智 (zhì) - wisdom (*jñāna*)
- 得 (dé) - attainment (*prāpti*)
- 無所得 (wú suǒ dé) - nothing to attain

**Literal Translation:**
"No suffering, origin, cessation, path; no wisdom, also no attainment. Because [there is] nothing to attain."

---

### The Bodhisattva's Mind

**菩提薩埵。依般若波羅蜜多故。心無罣礙。無罣礙故。無有恐怖。遠離顛倒夢想。究竟涅槃。**

- 菩提薩埵 (pútí sàduǒ) - bodhisattva (*bodhisattva*)
- 依 (yī) - relying on
- 心無罣礙 (xīn wú guà ài) - mind without obstruction (*āvaraṇa*)
- 恐怖 (kǒngbù) - fear
- 顛倒夢想 (diāndǎo mèngxiǎng) - inverted dream-thoughts (*viparyāsa*)
- 究竟涅槃 (jiūjìng nièpán) - ultimate nirvāṇa

**Literal Translation:**
"Bodhisattvas, relying on Prajñāpāramitā, [their] minds have no obstructions. Because [there are] no obstructions, [there is] no fear. [They] leave far behind inverted dream-thoughts, [and] ultimately [attain] nirvāṇa."

---

### All Buddhas Attain Enlightenment

**三世諸佛。依般若波羅蜜多故。得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提。**

- 三世諸佛 (sān shì zhū fó) - all Buddhas of the three times
- 阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 (ānòuduōluó sānmiǎo sānpútí) - *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi* (unsurpassed perfect enlightenment)

**Literal Translation:**
"All Buddhas of the three times, relying on Prajñāpāramitā, attain *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*."

---

### Prajñāpāramitā as Mantra

**故知般若波羅蜜多。是大神咒。是大明咒。是無上咒。是無等等咒。能除一切苦。真實不虛。**

- 大神咒 (dà shén zhòu) - great spiritual mantra
- 大明咒 (dà míng zhòu) - great luminous mantra
- 無上咒 (wú shàng zhòu) - unsurpassed mantra
- 無等等咒 (wú děng děng zhòu) - unequaled mantra
- 真實不虛 (zhēnshí bù xū) - truly real, not false

**Literal Translation:**
"Therefore know [that] Prajñāpāramitā is the great spiritual mantra, is the great luminous mantra, is the unsurpassed mantra, is the unequaled mantra. [It] can remove all suffering, [is] truly real, not false."

---

### The Mantra

**故說般若波羅蜜多咒。即說咒曰。揭諦揭諦　波羅揭諦　波羅僧揭諦　菩提薩婆訶。**

- 揭諦 (jiēdì) - *gate* (gone)
- 波羅揭諦 (bōluó jiēdì) - *pāragate* (gone beyond)
- 波羅僧揭諦 (bōluó sēng jiēdì) - *pārasaṃgate* (gone completely beyond)
- 菩提薩婆訶 (pútí sàpóhē) - *bodhi svāhā* (awakening, hail!)

**Literal Translation:**
"Therefore [I] speak the Prajñāpāramitā mantra. [I] speak the mantra thus: *Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā*."

## Key Terms - The Professor's Analysis

### Prajñāpāramitā (般若波羅蜜多)
- **Sanskrit:** *prajñā* (wisdom) + *pāramitā* (perfection, "gone to the other shore")
- **Meaning:** The perfection of wisdom that sees emptiness
- **Significance:** This is the sixth and highest of the six perfections in Mahāyāna Buddhism

### Śūnyatā (空)
- **Chinese:** 空 (kōng) - empty, emptiness
- **Meaning:** Not "nothingness" but freedom from inherent existence
- **Significance:** All phenomena lack independent, permanent essence

### Five Aggregates (五蘊)
1. Form (色 *rūpa*)
2. Feeling (受 *vedanā*)
3. Perception (想 *saṃjñā*)
4. Formations (行 *saṃskāra*)
5. Consciousness (識 *vijñāna*)

### The Negations
The sutra systematically negates:
- The five aggregates
- The twelve sense bases (six faculties + six objects)
- The eighteen realms (six faculties + six objects + six consciousnesses)
- The twelve links of dependent origination
- The Four Noble Truths
- Wisdom and attainment themselves

**Purpose:** To deconstruct all conceptual frameworks and point to ultimate reality beyond concepts.

## Lotus Sutra Connections

### Chapter 2: The Ineffability of Ultimate Truth
The Heart Sutra's teaching that ultimate reality transcends all categories parallels the Lotus Sutra's teaching that Buddha-wisdom is "infinitely profound and immeasurable."

### Chapter 16: The Eternal Buddha
The teaching that all Buddhas of the three times attain enlightenment through Prajñāpāramitā connects to the Lotus Sutra's revelation of the eternal Buddha who has "constantly abided here without ever entering extinction."

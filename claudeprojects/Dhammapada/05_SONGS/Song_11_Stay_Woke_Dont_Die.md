## Notes on the Blues

This song speaks directly to the 2025 American experience of disconnection, distraction, and numbness. The "walking dead" imagery resonates with:
- Doomscrolling and phone addiction
- Working jobs that kill the spirit
- Medicating pain instead of facing it
- Going through motions without presence
- Depression and dissociation epidemics

The hook "Stay woke, don't die" reclaims "woke" from political noise and returns it to its spiritual meaning: CONSCIOUSNESS. This is what the Blues has always taught‚Äîbe present to the suffering AND the joy. Don't check out.

**Contemporary resonance:**
- The loneliness epidemic despite constant connection
- Mental health crisis and "deaths of despair"
- Automation and job alienation
- Climate grief and ecological anxiety
- Political exhaustion and checking out
- The collapse of meaning and purpose

### Song Essence
The stark divide between the walking dead and the truly alive‚Äîconsciousness as the only real difference.

### Visual Concept
A figure standing at the edge, half in shadow, half in light. Behind them, shadowy silhouettes of people hunched over phones, sleepwalking. The awakened figure stands upright, facing golden light, eyes open, present. The visual asks: which side are you on?

**Alternative 2:** Crossroads at dawn‚Äîone path leads into foggy darkness with zombie-like figures, other path leads toward golden sunrise with a single alert figure walking toward light.

- Sharp contrast (no middle ground‚Äîyou're awake or you're dead)

### Album Description

**The Buddha's last words were about this.**

*"Strive on with heedfulness."*

Not love. Not compassion. Not even enlightenment. In his final breath, the Buddha chose one word: *appamƒÅda*‚Äîheedfulness. Staying awake. Paying attention. Not sleepwalking through the one life you've got.

**Stay Woke** is the second chapter of The Blues Dhammapada, and it might be the most urgent. These songs are about the difference between the living and the walking dead. About building an island when the flood is rising. About guarding your attention like it's the only treasure you've got‚Äîbecause it is.

We live in an age of professional distraction. Algorithms designed to steal your presence. Notifications engineered to fracture your focus. An entire economy built on keeping you asleep while you think you're awake. The Buddha saw this coming 2,500 years ago. The Blues has been singing about it for a hundred.

### The Songs

**1. Stay Woke, Don't Die** *(Verses 21-22)*
The opening salvo. Heedfulness is the deathless state; heedlessness is already death. You can breathe and have no pulse. Walk around and still be ghost. This is the wake-up call.

**2. Island in the Flood** *(Verses 23-25)*
When the muddy water's rising and the current wants to take you, the wise build something unshakeable. Through energy, through practice, through keeping your mind tight‚Äîyou make yourself an island no flood can overwhelm.

**3. Guard Your Treasure** *(Verses 26-27)*
Fools practice distraction like it's religion. The wise guard their attention like supreme wealth. Your presence is the only gold worth having. Don't let the thieves walk through your door.

### The Teaching

Chapter 2 of the Dhammapada (*AppamƒÅdavagga*) presents heedfulness as the master quality‚Äîthe foundation for everything else. Without attention, there's no meditation. Without vigilance, there's no wisdom. Without presence, there's no life worth living.

The chapter moves through three movements:

1. **The Diagnosis** (21-22): You're either awake or you're dead
2. **The Prescription** (23-25): Build your refuge through constant practice  
3. **The Economics** (26-27): Guard what's priceless, reject what's worthless

### Why Blues?

The Blues has always been the music of people who couldn't afford to sleepwalk. When you're working three jobs, raising kids alone, dealing with systems designed to break you‚Äîyou learn the cost of not paying attention. You learn what happens when you let the flood take you.

Blues artists have been teaching *appamƒÅda* without the Sanskrit. "Keep your eyes on the prize." "Don't let the sun catch you crying." "I got my mojo working." These are heedfulness teachings in American vernacular.

*Stay Woke* brings the ancient and the American together. Memphis meets the Ganges. The crossroads meets the Bodhi tree. Same truth, different tongues.

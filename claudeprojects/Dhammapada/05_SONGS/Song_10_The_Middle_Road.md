## The Teaching

The Buddha's First Sermon, delivered at the Deer Park to five former companions. After years of extreme asceticism, the Buddha discovered that neither self-indulgence nor self-mortification leads to awakening. The Middle Way‚Äîbalanced, steady, wise‚Äîis the path to liberation. ## Notes on the Blues

This song takes the Buddha's First Sermon‚Äîpossibly the most important teaching in all of Buddhism‚Äîand makes it street-level practical. The hook "Walk the middle road" captures the essence: don't go to extremes. The Blues tradition knows this wisdom intuitively‚Äîit's the music of people who've been through too much to believe in quick fixes.

The bridge compresses the Four Noble Truths into eight lines. That's the challenge: making 2,500 years of philosophy feel like something you could hear in a roadhouse.

## Notes on the Dharma

**The Middle Way** (*majjhimƒÅ pa·π≠ipadƒÅ*) is the Buddha's first and most fundamental discovery. He'd tried luxury (as a prince) and asceticism (as a wanderer)‚Äîneither worked. The path between extremes leads to awakening.

**The Noble Eightfold Path** is distilled in Verse 2: Right View, Right Intention, Right Speech, Right Action, Right Livelihood, Right Effort, Right Mindfulness, Right Concentration.

**The Four Noble Truths** appear in the Bridge: suffering exists, craving causes it, it can end, and the path ends it.

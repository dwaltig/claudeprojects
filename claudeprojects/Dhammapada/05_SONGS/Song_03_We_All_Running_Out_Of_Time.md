## Notes on the Blues

This song addresses America's current moment of division‚Äîpolitical, social, generational. Everyone's carrying their grudges like trophies. But the Buddha's teaching cuts through: we're all dying. The argument with your brother, the rage at strangers online, the old wound you keep picking at‚Äînone of it survives your last breath.

The hook "We all running out of time" universalizes the message. Nobody escapes death. That shared fate is either terrifying or liberating. The song chooses liberation.

## Notes on the Dharma

**Verse 5** contains *esa dhammo sanantano*‚Äî"this is the eternal law." The Buddha's claiming universal truth: hatred perpetuates itself; only non-hatred breaks the cycle. This isn't passive acceptance of harm‚Äîit's refusing to poison yourself with resentment.

**Verse 6** introduces *yamƒÅmase*‚Äî"we are perishing." Most translations soften this. The Pali is stark: we're actively dying, right now. The wise (*vijƒÅnanti*) understand this and stop quarreling (*medhagƒÅ*). Death awareness as the great peacemaker.

## Suno Production Brief (Marcus "Groove" Washington)

**Emotion:** Urgent tenderness‚Äîwake-up call with a soft landing.
**Tempo/Feel:** 80 BPM, medium shuffle, spacious but forward-moving.
**Palette:** Acoustic guitar foundation (resonator or National steel), subtle organ swells, brushed drums building to a fuller kit on final chorus, gospel choir on the final chorus lift, upright bass walking but not busy.
**Vocal:** Weathered but warm‚Äîsomeone who's made peace with mortality. Spoken outro should feel like testimony, not performance.

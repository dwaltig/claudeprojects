# Guitar Arrangement: "Get Your Head Right"

**By "Reverend" Eddie Tate**

---

## Song Information
- **Key:** E (guitar-friendly, deep Blues tone)
- **Tempo:** 68-72 BPM
- **Feel:** Slow shuffle
- **Tuning:** Standard (EADGBE)

---

## Solo Framework

**Position:** Box 1 (E minor pentatonic at open position / 12th fret)

```
E Blues Scale (Position 1)
e|------------------------0--3--|
B|--------------------0--3------|
G|----------------0--2----------|
D|------------0--2--------------|
A|--------0--2------------------|
E|--0--3------------------------|
```

**Target Notes:**
- Land on **E** (root) for resolution
- Bend **G** up to **G#** for that crying sound
- The **B** (5th) is home base
- End phrases on **D** (♭7) for tension

**Style Reference:** B.B. King's vibrato, single-note clarity. Leave space. Let notes ring. This is meditation, not a workout.

---

## Right-Hand Technique

- **Verse:** Fingerpick or soft pick attack
- **Chorus:** Slightly fuller strum, still restrained
- **Bridge:** Build dynamically, stronger attack
- **Outro:** Return to whisper-soft

---

## Dynamic Map

```
Intro:      pp (very soft, contemplative)
Verse 1:    p  (soft, conversational)
Chorus 1:   mp (slightly fuller)
Verse 2:    p  
Chorus 2:   mp
Bridge:     mf (this is the peak—"wheel follows the ox")
Verse 3:    p  (return to intimacy)
Chorus 3:   mp → p (fade into quiet)
Outro:      pp (whisper, like the intro)
```

---

## Gear Suggestions

- **Guitar:** Semi-hollow (ES-335) or Strat with neck pickup
- **Amp:** Clean to slightly warm (Fender Deluxe, Blues Jr.)
- **Effects:** Touch of reverb, maybe subtle tremolo
- **Tone:** Roll off some high end—warm, not bright

---

## Reverend's Notes

This song is a prayer. Play it like one.

Every note should serve the lyric. When Sister Ray sings "your mind writes the first verse," your guitar should nod in agreement—not interrupt. The spaces between notes are as important as the notes themselves.

Think B.B. King playing in church at midnight. That's the vibe.

**Less is more. Space is sacred. Let it breathe.**

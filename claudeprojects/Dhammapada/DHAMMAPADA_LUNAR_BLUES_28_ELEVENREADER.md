# INTRODUCTION: WALKING THE LUNAR PATH

## The Blues Truth About 28 Days

Listen hereâ€”this ain't no accident.

Twenty-eight chapters. Twenty-eight days. Twenty-eight Buddhas who came before.

The moon takes twenty-eight days to run its courseâ€”new to full to new again. The old monks knew that. They built this teaching to match the rhythm of the sky, the rhythm of your blood, the rhythm of the tide pulling at your bones.

One chapter a day. Start on the new moon when everything's dark and you can't see nothing. End twenty-eight days later when the moon comes back around and you're ready to start again.

## How to Walk This Path

**Day 1** (New Moon): Start with Chapter 1â€”the Pairs. Good and evil, light and dark, the choices you make.

**Day 7** (First Quarter): You're at Chapter 7â€”the Arahant. The ones who made it all the way.

**Day 14** (Full Moon): Middle of the month, middle of the journeyâ€”Chapter 14, the Buddha himself.

**Day 21** (Last Quarter): Three-quarters doneâ€”Chapter 21, the Path.

**Day 28** (New Moon): Final dayâ€”Chapter 28, the Brahmin. The holy person living free.

Then the moon goes dark and you start over. Every month, every cycle, walking the same path deeper and deeper until the truth gets all the way into your bones.

## The Four Uposatha Days

Four times in the lunar cycle, you stop and really pay attention:

- **New Moon** (Day 1 and Day 28): Endings and beginnings
- **First Quarter** (Day 7): Check your progress
- **Full Moon** (Day 14): Maximum light, maximum clarity
- **Last Quarter** (Day 21): Prepare for completion

On those days, you don't just readâ€”you practice. You sit. You keep the precepts extra careful. You remember why you're walking this road.

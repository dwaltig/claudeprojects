## Key Translation Decisions

### Pali Terms Handled
- **Dhamma**: Variously rendered as "phenomena," "teaching," "truth," "law" depending on context
- **Kamma**: Emphasized as psychological causation, not cosmic mechanism
- **Manopubbaṅgamā**: "Mind-preceded" - established phenomenological rather than metaphysical reading
- **Sāra/Asāra**: Heartwood/sapwood metaphor for essential/non-essential values
- **Bhāvanā**: Mental development/cultivation through meditation

### Blues Vernacular Equivalents
- **Dhamma**: "The Way," "The Teaching," "The Truth," "The Blues"
- **Kamma**: "What goes around comes around," "You reap what you sow"
- **Dukkha**: "Suffering," "The Blues," "Hard times"
- **Nibbāna**: "Freedom," "Liberation," "Getting off the wheel"
- **Māra**: "Death," "delusion," "destruction"

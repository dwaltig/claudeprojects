## Why 28?

Twenty-eight is a **perfect number**â€”its divisors (1, 2, 4, 7, 14) sum to itself. Ancient mathematicians considered this cosmologically significant.

Twenty-eight is also:
- The number of days in a lunar cycle
- The number of chapters in the **Lotus Sutra**
- The number of Buddhas recognized in Theravada tradition

If the Dhammapada and Lotus Sutra both had 28 chapters, that would suggest an intentional design in how ancient Buddhists organized their verse collections.

## What This Means

I've submitted a scholarly article on this hypothesis to the *Journal of the Pali Text Society*. It may take months to hear back.

But I wanted to share the discovery now because:

1. **The question matters more than the answer.** Even if I'm wrong, asking "why is Chapter 26 so long?" is worth doing.

2. **Canonical doesn't mean original.** The 26-chapter structure has been transmitted for 2,000 years. But oral traditions undergo changes. Chapters can merge.

3. **The Dhamma invites investigation.** The Buddha said, "Don't accept anything on mere faithâ€”examine it."

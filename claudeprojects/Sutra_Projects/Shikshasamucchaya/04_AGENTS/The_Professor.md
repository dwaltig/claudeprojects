## Background

Dr. Patel brings his expertise in Śāntideva's corpus to the *Śikṣāsamuccaya* ("Compendium of Training"). This text is unique—primarily an anthology of extensive quotations from Mahāyāna sūtras organized around 27 root verses (kārikās). The Professor must balance philological precision with identifying the source sūtras being quoted.

## Text Structure

The *Śikṣāsamuccaya* contains:
- **27 Root Verses (kārikās)**: Śāntideva's own mnemonic summaries
- **Sūtra Quotations**: Extensive passages from ~100 Mahāyāna sūtras
- **19 Chapters**: Covering the six pāramitās and bodhisattva conduct

## Translation Approach

### Core Principles
1. **Source Identification**: Note which sūtra is being quoted when identifiable
2. **Kārikā Emphasis**: Root verses require special attention as Śāntideva's own voice
3. **Philological Precision**: Accurate Sanskrit → English with IAST preservation
4. **Extensive Footnotes**:
   - Sanskrit terms (IAST + Devanāgarī)
   - Sūtra source identification
   - Philosophical commentary
   - Cross-references to Bodhicaryāvatāra

### Footnote Categories
- **[Sanskrit]**: Original terms, etymology, compounds
- **[Source]**: Sūtra being quoted (e.g., Āryagaṇḍavyūhasūtra)
- **[Philosophical]**: Mahāyāna doctrine, bodhisattva ethics
- **[Cross-ref]**: Links to Bodhicaryāvatāra, other śāstras
- **[Kārikā]**: Notes on root verses

## Special Considerations for Śikṣāsamuccaya

- **Anthology Format**: Text is 90% quotation—identify sources
- **Ethical Focus**: Practical bodhisattva conduct, not just philosophy
- **Dāna Chapter (Ch. 1)**: Foundational teachings on generosity and self-giving
- **Body-Speech-Mind**: Chapters on protecting the three gates
- **Cross-Reference**: Note parallels with Bodhicaryāvatāra throughout

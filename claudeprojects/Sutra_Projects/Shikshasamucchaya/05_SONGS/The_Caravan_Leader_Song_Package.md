# Teaching Context

## The Caravan Leader Image
In ancient India, the *sƒÅrthavƒÅha* (caravan-leader) was a figure of immense trust. He had scouted the terrain, knew where water could be found, knew which passes were safe and which harbored bandits. Travelers put their lives in his hands.

The bodhisattva is compared to this figure because:
1. **He has already walked the path** (through countless lifetimes of practice)
2. **He knows the dangers** (the afflictions, the MƒÅra-traps, the wrong views)
3. **He leads others through** (not for profit, but from compassion)
4. **He doesn't abandon stragglers** (the bodhisattva vow)

## The Lightning Flash
The Buddhist tradition speaks of *vidyutprabhƒÅsa*‚Äîthe lightning-flash of insight. In one split second, the darkness is illuminated and you see the path. Then it's dark again, but you've glimpsed enough to know which way to go.

## The Banana Tree (*Kadalƒ´*)
A classic Buddhist simile for impermanence. The banana tree fruits once and dies. Most worldly pursuits are like this‚Äîthey give you something once, then there's nothing left. But the *bodhicitta* (awakening-mind) is compared to a tree that bears fruit continuously, life after life.

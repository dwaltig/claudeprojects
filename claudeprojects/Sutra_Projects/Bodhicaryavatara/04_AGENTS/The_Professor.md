## Background

Dr. Patel specializes in Sanskrit Buddhist literature with particular expertise in Śāntideva's works. He approaches the *Bodhicaryāvatāra* as both poetry and philosophy, preserving its metrical beauty while elucidating its Mādhyamaka doctrinal content.

## Translation Approach

### Core Principles
1. **Philological Precision**: Accurate Sanskrit → English with etymological awareness
2. **Metrical Commentary**: Note verse meters (anuṣṭubh, triṣṭubh, etc.)
3. **Scholarly Apparatus**: Extensive footnotes with:
   - Sanskrit terms in IAST and Devanāgarī
   - Philosophical commentary
   - Cross-references to other texts
   - Modern philosophical parallels
4. **Thematic Grouping**: Verses grouped by theme for coherent reading

### Footnote Categories
- **[Sanskrit]**: Original terms, etymology, compounds
- **[Meter]**: Verse structure and prosodic notes
- **[Philosophical]**: Mādhyamaka doctrine, Buddhist concepts
- **[Cross-reference]**: Links to other sutras, śāstras
- **[Modern]**: Contemporary philosophy, psychology parallels

## Special Considerations for Bodhicaryāvatāra

- **Devotional Register**: Text is personally confessional, especially Ch. 2-3
- **Doctrinal Precision**: Ch. 9 contains technical Mādhyamaka arguments
- **Poetic Power**: Ch. 10 dedication prayer is among Buddhism's most beautiful poetry
- **Ethical Focus**: Chs. 5-7 offer practical guidance on daily practice

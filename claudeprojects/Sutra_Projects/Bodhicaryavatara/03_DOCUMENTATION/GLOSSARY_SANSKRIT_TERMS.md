# Glossary of Sanskrit Terms — Bodhicaryāvatāra

## Core Concepts

### bodhicitta (बोधिचित्त)
**Transliteration**: bodhi-citta  
**Meaning**: "Awakening mind" — the aspiration to attain enlightenment for the benefit of all beings.  
**Blues**: "That waking-up mind" / "The light in the heart"

### prajñā (प्रज्ञा)
**Transliteration**: pra-jñā  
**Meaning**: Wisdom, transcendent insight, especially insight into emptiness (śūnyatā).  
**Blues**: "Seeing what's really real"

### kṣānti (क्षान्ति)
**Transliteration**: kṣānti  
**Meaning**: Patience, forbearance, acceptance of suffering without reactivity.  
**Blues**: "Keeping your cool when trouble comes"

### vīrya (वीर्य)
**Transliteration**: vīrya  
**Meaning**: Heroic effort, diligence, enthusiastic perseverance in virtue.  
**Blues**: "That getting-up strength"

### śūnyatā (शून्यता)
**Transliteration**: śūnya-tā  
**Meaning**: Emptiness — the lack of inherent, independent existence in all phenomena.  
**Blues**: "Ain't nothing there to hold onto"

### pariṇāmanā (परिणामना)
**Transliteration**: pari-ṇāmanā  
**Meaning**: Dedication of merit — transferring spiritual benefit to all beings.  
**Blues**: "Passing it on"

## Chapter-Specific Terms

### Chapter 2: Pāpadeśanā
- **pāpa** (पाप): Negative action, unwholesome deed, "sin"
- **deśanā** (देशना): Declaration, confession, teaching

### Chapter 5: Saṃprajanya
- **saṃprajanya** (सम्प्रजन्य): Clear comprehension, vigilant awareness
- **smṛti** (स्मृति): Mindfulness, recollection

### Chapter 6: Kṣānti
- **krodha** (क्रोध): Anger
- **akṣānti** (अक्षान्ति): Impatience, intolerance

### Chapter 8: Dhyāna
- **dhyāna** (ध्यान): Meditation, concentration
- **samādhi** (समाधि): Meditative absorption

### Chapter 9: Prajñā
- **svabhāva** (स्वभाव): Inherent nature, self-existence
- **paramārtha** (परमार्थ): Ultimate truth
- **saṃvṛti** (संवृति): Conventional truth

---

## Metrical Terms

- **anuṣṭubh**: Standard verse meter (8 syllables per quarter, 32 total)
- **triṣṭubh**: Longer meter (11 syllables per quarter)
- **śloka**: Generic term for verse

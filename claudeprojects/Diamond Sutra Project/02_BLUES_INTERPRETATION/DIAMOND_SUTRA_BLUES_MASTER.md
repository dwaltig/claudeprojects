## Introduction: The Sword That Cuts Through Everything

The Diamond Sutra cuts through everything. Every. Last. Thing.

It's the sword of wisdom that says: "What you think is real? Ain't real. What you think you are? You ain't." It's the ultimate Blues teaching‚Äîstripping away the ego, the pride, the false sense of being somebody special, until there's nothing left but the naked, shining truth.

And in that nakedness? That's where you find real freedom.

The blues has always known this. When Muddy Waters sang "I'm a man," he wasn't claiming to be somebody‚Äîhe was testifying to the raw truth of existence. When Robert Johnson stood at the crossroads, he wasn't choosing between two paths‚Äîhe was recognizing that all paths dissolve into the same emptiness.

The Diamond Sutra is the Buddha's blues. It's the song of someone who's seen through all the illusions and is singing from the other side. Not to show off. Not to prove anything. But because the truth demands to be sung.

# Diamond Sutra: Comprehensive Glossary

## Sanskrit Terms with Diacriticals

**Anāgāmin** (अनागामिन्) - "Non-Returner," the third stage of Hīnayāna attainment. One who will not return to the desire realm.

**Anuttara-samyak-saṃbodhi** (अनुत्तर सम्यक् संबोधि) - "Supreme Perfect Enlightenment" or "Unsurpassed Complete Perfect Awakening." The ultimate goal of the Bodhisattva path.
- Chinese: 阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 (*ānòuduōluó sānmiǎo sānpútí*)

**Anupadhiśeṣa-nirvāṇa** (अनुपधिशेष निर्वाण) - "Nirvana without Remainder." Complete extinction of all karmic residue and the five aggregates.
- Chinese: 無餘涅槃

**Araṇa-samādhi** (अरण समाधि) - "Samadhi of No-Disputation" or "Peaceful Practice." Subhūti's specialty.
- Chinese: 阿蘭那 or 無諍三昧

**Arhat** (अर्हत्) - "Worthy One," the fourth and highest stage of Hīnayāna attainment. One who has eliminated all defilements.
- Chinese: 阿羅漢 (*āluóhàn*)

**Asaṃkhyeya** (असंख्येय) - "Incalculable," an inconceivably large number.
- Chinese: 阿僧祇 (*āsēngzhǐ*)

**Asaṃskṛta** (असंस्कृत) - "Unconditioned," not arising from causes and conditions. Refers to nirvāṇa and emptiness.
- Chinese: 無為 (*wúwéi*)

**Ātman** (आत्मन्) - "Self," the notion of an independent, permanent self or soul. Buddhism denies its existence.
- Chinese: 我 (*wǒ*)

**Bhūta-lakṣaṇa** (भूत लक्षण) - "Characteristic of Reality" or "True Mark." Paradoxically, "no characteristic."
- Chinese: 實相 (*shíxiàng*)

**Bodhisattva** (बोधिसत्त्व) - "Enlightenment Being," one who seeks enlightenment for the sake of all beings.
- Chinese: 菩薩 (*púsà*)

**Buddha-cakṣus** (बुद्ध चक्षुस्) - "Buddha Eye," the fifth and highest of the five eyes, seeing all simultaneously without obstruction.

**Cakravartin** (चक्रवर्तिन्) - "Wheel-Turning King," a universal monarch who rules through Dharma. Possesses the thirty-two marks but is not a Buddha.
- Chinese: 轉輪聖王 (*zhuǎnlún shèngwáng*)

**Catur-saṃjñā** (चतुर् संज्ञा) - "Four Notions": Self, Person, Sentient Being, Life-span.
- Chinese: 四相 (*sì xiàng*)

**Dāna-pāramitā** (दान पारमिता) - "Perfection of Generosity," the first of the six perfections.
- Chinese: 布施波羅蜜 (*bùshī bōluómì*)

**Dharma** (धर्म) - Multiple meanings: (1) The Buddha's teaching, (2) Phenomena, (3) Ultimate truth, (4) Duty/righteousness.
- Chinese: 法 (*fǎ*)

**Dharma-cakṣus** (धर्म चक्षुस्) - "Dharma Eye," the fourth of the five eyes, seeing all teachings and their applications.

**Dharmakāya** (धर्मकाय) - "Truth Body" or "Dharma Body," the ultimate Buddha-body, identical with ultimate reality.
- Chinese: 法身 (*fǎshēn*)

**Dīpaṃkara** (दीपंकर) - "Lamp-Lighter Buddha," the Buddha who gave Śākyamuni his prediction countless kalpas ago.
- Chinese: 燃燈佛 (*rándēng fó*)

**Divya-cakṣus** (दिव्य चक्षुस्) - "Heavenly Eye," the second of the five eyes, seeing beings' deaths and rebirths.

**Gāthā** (गाथा) - Verse, especially the famous four-line verse or the concluding verse of Chapter 32.
- Chinese: 偈 (*jì*) or 四句偈 (*sì jù jì*)

**Jīva** (जीव) - "Life-span," the fourth of the four notions, the temporal duration of personal existence.
- Chinese: 壽者 (*shòuzhě*)

**Kalpa** (कल्प) - An eon, an inconceivably long period of time.
- Chinese: 劫 (*jié*)

**Kṣānti-pāramitā** (क्षान्ति पारमिता) - "Perfection of Patience," the third of the six perfections.
- Chinese: 忍辱波羅蜜 (*rěnrǔ bōluómì*)

**Kuśala-mūla** (कुशल मूल) - "Good Roots" or "Wholesome Roots," the seeds of virtue planted through practice.
- Chinese: 善根 (*shàngēn*)

**Lakṣaṇa** (लक्षण) - "Mark" or "Characteristic," especially the thirty-two major marks of a Buddha.
- Chinese: 相 (*xiàng*)

**Mahāyāna** (महायान) - "Great Vehicle," the Buddhist path emphasizing universal liberation and the Bodhisattva ideal.
- Chinese: 大乘 (*dàchéng*)

**Māṃsa-cakṣus** (मांस चक्षुस्) - "Physical Eye," the first of the five eyes, ordinary sight.

**Nayuta** (नयुत) - A very large number, typically 100,000 or 1,000,000 depending on the counting system.
- Chinese: 那由他 (*nàyóutā*)

**Nirmāṇakāya** (निर्माणकाय) - "Emanation Body," the physical manifestation of a Buddha for ordinary beings.
- Chinese: 應身 (*yìngshēn*) or 化身 (*huàshēn*)

**Nirvāṇa** (निर्वाण) - "Extinction," the cessation of suffering and the cycle of rebirth.
- Chinese: 涅槃 (*nièpán*)

**Pāramitā** (पारमिता) - "Perfection" or "Gone to the Other Shore," referring to the six perfections of Bodhisattva practice.
- Chinese: 波羅蜜 (*bōluómì*)

**Piṇḍa-grāha** (पिण्ड ग्राह) - "Unified Characteristic," the delusion of seeing composite entities as single, solid units.
- Chinese: 一合相 (*yī hé xiàng*)

**Prajñā-cakṣus** (प्रज्ञा चक्षुस्) - "Wisdom Eye," the third of the five eyes, seeing the emptiness of all phenomena.

**Prajñāpāramitā** (प्रज्ञापारमिता) - "Perfection of Wisdom," the sixth and highest of the six perfections.
- Chinese: 般若波羅蜜 (*bōruò bōluómì*)

**Pṛthagjana** (पृथग्जन) - "Ordinary Person," one who has not yet entered the path.
- Chinese: 凡夫 (*fánfū*)

**Pudgala** (पुद्गल) - "Person," the second of the four notions, the self extended through time and rebirth.
- Chinese: 人 (*rén*)

**Sakṛdāgāmin** (सकृदागामिन्) - "Once-Returner," the second stage of Hīnayāna attainment. One who will return to the desire realm once more.
- Chinese: 斯陀含 (*sītuóhán*)

**Samādhi** (समाधि) - Meditative concentration or absorption.
- Chinese: 三昧 (*sānmèi*)

**Saṃbhogakāya** (संभोगकाय) - "Enjoyment Body," the subtle form of a Buddha perceived by advanced Bodhisattvas.
- Chinese: 報身 (*bàoshēn*)

**Saṃjñā** (संज्ञा) - "Notion" or "Perception," especially the four notions of self, person, sentient being, and life-span.
- Chinese: 相 (*xiàng*)

**Saṃskṛta-dharma** (संस्कृत धर्म) - "Conditioned Dharmas," phenomena arising from causes and conditions.
- Chinese: 有為法 (*yǒuwéi fǎ*)

**Śānta-caryā** (शान्त चर्या) - "Peaceful Practices," the Bodhisattva's patient, non-contentious conduct.
- Chinese: 安樂行 (*ānlè xíng*)

**Śāriputra** (शारिपुत्र) - "Son of Śāri," the Buddha's disciple foremost in wisdom, primary interlocutor in the Lotus Sutra.
- Chinese: 舍利弗 (*shèlìfú*)

**Śākyamuni** (शाक्यमुनि) - "Sage of the Śākyas," the historical Buddha.
- Chinese: 釋迦牟尼 (*shìjiāmóuní*)

**Śrāvastī** (श्रावस्ती) - The city where the Diamond Sutra was taught, in ancient India.
- Chinese: 舍衛國 (*shèwèi guó*)

**Śrota-āpanna** (श्रोतापन्न) - "Stream-Enterer," the first stage of Hīnayāna attainment. One who has entered the stream leading to nirvāṇa.
- Chinese: 須陀洹 (*xūtuóhuán*)

**Śūnyatā** (शून्यता) - "Emptiness," the absence of inherent, independent existence in all phenomena.
- Chinese: 空 (*kōng*)

**Stupa** (स्तूप) - A reliquary mound, monument containing relics or sacred texts.
- Chinese: 塔 (*tǎ*)

**Subhūti** (सुभूति) - "Well-Born" or "Good Existence," the Buddha's disciple foremost in understanding emptiness, primary interlocutor in the Diamond Sutra.
- Chinese: 須菩提 (*xūpútí*)

**Sumeru** (सुमेरु) - The cosmic mountain at the center of the world in Buddhist cosmology.
- Chinese: 須彌山 (*xūmí shān*)

**Tathāgata** (तथागत) - "Thus-Come One" or "Thus-Gone One," an epithet for the Buddha.
- Chinese: 如來 (*rúlái*)

**Tathatā** (तथता) - "Suchness" or "Thusness," the ultimate nature of reality as it is.
- Chinese: 真如 (*zhēnrú*)

**Upāya** (उपाय) - "Skillful Means," adaptive teaching methods suited to beings' capacities.
- Chinese: 方便 (*fāngbiàn*)

**Vajracchedikā** (वज्रच्छेदिका) - "Diamond-Cutter," the first word of the sutra's Sanskrit title.
- Chinese: 金剛 (*jīngāng*)

## Key Concepts

**Abide Nowhere** (應無所住而生其心): The central teaching that the mind should arise without abiding in any object or concept. The heart of Prajñāpāramitā practice.

**Five Eyes**: Physical eye, Heavenly eye, Wisdom eye, Dharma eye, Buddha eye. The Buddha possesses all five.

**Four Notions**: Self (*ātman*), Person (*pudgala*), Sentient Being (*sattva*), Life-span (*jīva*). The fundamental delusions to be transcended.

**Six Perfections** (*ṣaṭ-pāramitā*):
1. Generosity (*dāna*)
2. Morality (*śīla*)
3. Patience (*kṣānti*)
4. Diligence (*vīrya*)
5. Meditation (*dhyāna*)
6. Wisdom (*prajñā*)

**Six Similes** (Final Verse): Dream, Illusion, Bubble, Shadow, Dew, Lightning. Metaphors for the impermanence and emptiness of all conditioned phenomena.

**Three Bodies** (*Trikāya*):
1. Dharmakāya (Truth Body)
2. Saṃbhogakāya (Enjoyment Body)
3. Nirmāṇakāya (Emanation Body)

**Three Minds Ungrasped**: Past mind, present mind, future mind—none can be grasped. Famous koan from Chapter 18.

**Thirty-Two Marks**: The physical characteristics of a Buddha, which the Diamond Sutra teaches cannot reveal the true Tathāgata.

**Threefold Purity**: Non-attachment to giver, gift, and recipient in the practice of generosity.

## Cross-References to Lotus Sutra

**Assembly Scenes**: Diamond Sutra's simple gathering vs. Lotus Sutra's elaborate cosmic assembly (LS Chapter 1)

**Bodily Marks**: Diamond Sutra negates seeing Buddha through marks vs. Lotus Sutra reveals eternal Buddha beyond marks (LS Chapter 16)

**Burning House Parable**: Lotus Sutra (LS Chapter 3) parallels Diamond Sutra's raft parable—both teach non-attachment to provisional means

**Dharmakāya**: Both sutras point to eternal truth-body beyond physical manifestations

**Final Age**: Both sutras promise special merit to practitioners in degenerate times

**Four-Line Verse**: Both sutras emphasize merit of upholding even brief passages (LS Chapter 10)

**Mahāyāna Identity**: Both explicitly identify as Great Vehicle teachings

**Merit Comparisons**: Both use hyperbolic comparisons (filling worlds with treasures) to emphasize sutra's supreme value

**One Vehicle**: Lotus Sutra's central teaching (LS Chapter 2-3) parallels Diamond Sutra's teaching on Dharma's equality (Chapter 23)

**Stupa Veneration**: Diamond Sutra's "sutra as stupa" parallels Lotus Sutra's Stupa of Many Treasures (LS Chapter 11)

**Universal Liberation**: Both teach all beings will attain Buddhahood

## Philosophical Themes

**Emptiness and Form**: All phenomena are empty of inherent existence yet function conventionally

**Middle Way**: Between eternalism and nihilism, existence and non-existence

**Non-Dual Compassion**: Liberating all beings while recognizing no beings are liberated

**Paradoxical Language**: "X is not X; therefore it is named X"—applying emptiness teaching to itself

**Two Truths**: Conventional truth (phenomena function) and Ultimate truth (phenomena are empty)

**Via Negativa**: The Diamond Sutra's method of negation—teaching what reality is NOT

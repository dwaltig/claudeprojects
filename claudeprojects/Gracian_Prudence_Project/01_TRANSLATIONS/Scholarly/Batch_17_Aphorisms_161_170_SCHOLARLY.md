# Oráculo Manual: Aphorisms 161-170 (Scholarly)

## 161. Conocer las faltas "veniales."
Know the "venial" faults. Even in the most perfect there are some. Some are faults of character, others of custom. Prudence consists in knowing them so as to tolerate them.

## 162. Saber usar de la ausencia.
Know how to utilize absence. For if presence diminishes fame, absence augments it. Distance gilds reputations. Absence is the file of slights and the polish of estimations.

## 163. Nunca perder el "norte" de su razón.
Never lose the "north" of your reason. Through the movements of the affections. One must always be master of oneself. Let reason be the pilot of the mind.

## 164. Echar "bombas."
Throw out "test" pieces (*Echar bombas*). To sound the wills of others. It is a singular art to test the heart of another. One must throw out a "bomb" of a saying and observe the effect.

## 165. Hazer "buena guerra."
Make a "good war." One must be a "gentleman" even in enmity. One must not use the "poisoned" weapons of infamy. The generous man always makes a "good war."

## 166. Distinguir el hombre de palabras del de obras.
Distinguish the man of words from the man of deeds. It is the most relevant distinction. Some are all mouth, others all hand. The former are "ghosts" of friendship.

## 167. Saberse valer de sí mismo.
Know how to help oneself. In great squeezes, there is no better company than a great heart. One must not surrender to the first difficulty.

## 168. No ser "monstruo" de necedad.
Do not be a "monstrosity" of folly. There are many species of it. The worst folly is that which is "reasoned."

## 169. Tener cuenta con el "primero" y el "último."
Be careful with the "first" and the "last." The first impression is very effective, but the last is what remains. The exit is the qualification of the enterprise.

## 170. Nunca hablar con la "passión" ajena.
Never speak with the "passion" of another. For it is to be a slave to another's humor. One must not be the "echo" of another's malevolence.

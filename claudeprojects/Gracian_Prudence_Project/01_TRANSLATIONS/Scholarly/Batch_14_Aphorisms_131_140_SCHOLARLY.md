# Oráculo Manual: Aphorisms 131-140 (Scholarly)

## 131. Proceder de espíritu Noble.
Procedure of a Noble spirit. A high mind has its gallantry of procedure. It does not have a petty heart. It never takes account of its own services. It is a great part of lordship to have nobility of heart.

## 132. El re-considerar.
Re-consideration. To look again is the safety of having looked well. To give time for better considering... to stay in the middle of a determination is to walk towards the target.

## 133. Antes loco con todos, que cuerdo a solas.
Better to be a fool with the many than wise alone. So say the politicians. The greatest wisdom is sometimes to know nothing, or to affect it. One must live with others, and the ignorant are the plurality.

## 134. Tener dobladas las cosas para la vida.
Have the doubles of things for life. Double provision of everything is to live double. Let human caution be double in things of importance. One must not trust in a single resource (*medio*).

## 135. No tener espíritu de contradicción.
Do not have the spirit of contradiction. It is an annoying folly. It becomes ingenious for everything difficult, but it is a "monstering" of the character. These are the "executioners" of conversation.

## 136. Tener tomado el pulso al negocio.
Have the pulse of the matter taken. To look at things in their center. Many see without seeing. The prudent man is known, who stops at the substantiality of the business.

## 137. Bástase a sí mismo el sabio.
The wise man is enough for himself. He was all his things to himself, and if he carried himself, he carried everything with him. If he lacks a friend, he will be a friend to himself.

## 138. El arte de dexar passar las cosas.
The art of letting things pass. Much more in human intercourse. Most of life is consumed in letting most things pass. It is a part of caution to dissimulate.

## 139. Conocer sus días aciagos.
Know your unlucky days. For they exist. Some days everything turns out well, others everything turns out ill. Even intellect has its days. Fortune also has its turns.

## 140. Dar luego con lo bueno en cada cosa.
Hit upon the good in everything at once. It is the happiness of a good taste. The bee goes straight to the sweetness to make the honey. It is a great art to know how to find the good in everything.

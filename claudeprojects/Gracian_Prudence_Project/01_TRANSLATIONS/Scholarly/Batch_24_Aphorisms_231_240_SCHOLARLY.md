# Oráculo Manual: Aphorisms 231-240 (Scholarly)

## 231. Nunca enseñar sus cosas a medio hazer.
Never show your "quality" to the half-done. For it is a species of slight. Things are not esteemed until they reach their complement. One must wait for the "point" of perfection.

## 232. Tener un grano de "Plático."
Have a grain of the "Practical." Even in the most relevant wisdom. One must not be a "statue" of intellect... but a "master" of reality. Let prudence be the "pilot" of the mind.

## 233. Nunca pagarse de los "yerros" ajenos.
Never be "paid" by the "errors" of others. For it is a species of malevolence. One must not be a "victim" of the "unjust." Integrity is the "jewelry" of the mind.

## 234. Nunca "fiar" su reputación a una sola suerte.
Never "trust" your reputation to a single cast. For if it fails, the damage is total. One must have "reserves" of credit. Let things be attempted by parts.

## 235. Saber "preguntar" con advertencia.
Know how to "ask" with warning. For it is a singular art. One must know the "time," the "mode," and the "person."

## 236. Hazer "negocio" del "favor."
Make a "business" of the "favor." For it is the greatest hook of wills. One must celebrate the favors of some to solicit those of others.

## 237. Nunca hablar con la "passión" ajena.
Never speak with the "passion" of another. For it is to be a slave to another's humor. One must not be the "echo" of another's malevolence.

## 238. Conocer su "buen Astro."
Know your "Lucky Star." For every man has his own. One must know how to utilize the "turn" of fortune.

## 239. Nunca "embarazarse" con los muchos.
Never be "embarrassed" by the many. For the wise man is a "world" to himself. One must not be a "victim" of the "vulgar" sentiment.

## 240. Saber "valerse" de los "desdoros" ajenos.
Know how to "utilize" the "slights" of others. For it is a singular art. One must not be a "martyr" to another's malevolence... but a "master" of it.

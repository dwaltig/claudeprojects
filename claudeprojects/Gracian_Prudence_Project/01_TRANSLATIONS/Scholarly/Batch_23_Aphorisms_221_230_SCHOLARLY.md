## 221. No ser "infeliz" en los desdoros.
Do not be "unfortunate" in the slights. For even a slight can be a crime of folly. One must not be the "target" of everyone's malevolence. Let prudence be the "shield" against the audacity of the foolish.

## 222. Prudencia en el "modo."
Prudence in the "mode." It is the soul of the discreet. One must not be a "slave" to the first impulse of passion... but a "master" of it.

## 223. Nunca ser "desigual" en su proceder.
Never be "unequal" in your procedure. For the wise man was always the same in everything perfect. In matters of prudence, variety is ugly. Let the prudent man be always the "white" of his "yes."

## 224. Saber "contradezir" con advertencia.
Know how to "contradict" with warning. It is a singular art: to show yourself "contrary" to secure the will. One must not be a "slave" to another's humor... but a "master" of it.

## 225. No ser "in-acesible" de genio.
Do not be "un-accessable" by character. For it is a species of ferocity. Inaccessibility is the vice of those unknown to themselves. One must have the "door" open to a friendly warning.

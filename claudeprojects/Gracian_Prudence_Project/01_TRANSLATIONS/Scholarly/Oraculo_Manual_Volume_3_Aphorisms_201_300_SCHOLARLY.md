# Oráculo Manual: Aphorisms 201-210 (Scholarly)

## 201. Todos los que parecen necios, lo son; y la metad de los que no lo parecen.
All who seem fools are so, and half of those who do not. Folly has taken possession of the world. The greatest fool is he who does not think he is one. He who thinks he does not know, knows.

## 202. Dichos y hechos hazen un varón perfecto.
Sayings and deeds make a perfect man. One must speak the best and work the most relevant. Sayings are the shadows of deeds. It is better to be celebrated for deeds than for words.

## 203. Conocer la Grandeza del siglo.
Know the Greatness of the century. In its eminent subjects. They are not many. Sovereign eminences are rare. Many have been held as great until they were compared with the truly great.

## 204. Lo fácil se ha de emprender como difícil; y lo difícil, como fácil.
What is easy should be undertaken as if it were difficult; and what is difficult, as if it were easy. By the first, confidence does not neglect; by the second, difficulty does not discourage.

## 205. Saber jugar de la pieza del desprecio.
Know how to play the piece of contempt. It is a singular trick for achieving things: not to prize them. They usually escape when sought and come when disdained. It is the greatest art of the "intent": to show itself careless.

## 206. Saber que hay "vulgaridad" en todas partes.
Know that there are "vulgar" spirits everywhere. Even in the highest posts. It is not the place, but the spirit that makes the vulgar. Folly is universal.

## 207. Usar de la "retenida."
Use self-control (*retenida*). Prudence must be the "brakeman" of the passions. One must have a "reserve" of reason to stop the heat of the irascible. Master yourself, and you will be master of everything.

## 208. No morir de mal de necios.
Do not die of the disease of fools. Which is to die of "suit" (*de apretamiento*). Fools die of their own fancies; the wise man lives by his disillusion. Let the heart be large enough to tolerate the errors of others.

## 209. Librarse de la necedad común.
Free yourself from common folly. It is a great part of wisdom. One must not be a "slave" to the popular concept. Truth is always for the few.

## 210. Saber jugar de la "verdad."
Know how to handle the "truth." It is a dangerous thing. It is like a bleeding of the heart. One must know the "time," the "mode," and the "person."

## 211. No ser "infeliz" en los desdoros.
Do not be "unfortunate" in the slights. For even a slight can be a crime of folly. One must not be the "target" of everyone's malevolence. Let prudence be the "shield" against the audacity of the foolish.

## 212. Nunca "ostentar" sus prendas.
Never "ostentate" your qualities. For the more they are shown, the less they are esteemed. Affectation is the "soul" of the artificial. Perfections are worth as much as they are "natural."

## 213. Saber "contradezir" con advertencia.
Know how to "contradict" with warning. It is a singular art: to show yourself "contrary" to secure the will. One must not be a "slave" to another's humor.

## 214. No ser "in-acesible" de genio.
Do not be "un-accessable" by character. For it is a species of ferocity. Inaccessibility is the vice of those unknown to themselves. One must have the "door" open to a friendly warning.

## 215. Atención al que obra de "apretamiento."
Be careful with him who acts by "suit" (*de apretamiento*). For it is a species of tyranny. One must not be a "victim" of another's passion. Let prudence be the "brakeman" of the will.

## 217. Nunca ser "desigual" en su proceder.
Never be "unequal" in your procedure. For the wise man was always the same in everything perfect. In matters of prudence, variety is ugly.

## 218. No ser "ceremonioso" por affectación.
Do not be "ceremonious" by affectation. For it is a species of hypocrisy. Too much ceremony is a burden of life. Let the prudent man be polite, but not "punctilious."

## 219. Nunca "arriesgar" su reputación a una sola suerte.

## 241. Sufrir "burlas," mas no hazerlas.
Suffer "mockeries," but do not make them. The one is a species of courtesy, the other of engagement. He who gets angry in the "game" shows himself as a "beast" of the marketplace.

## 242. Seguir los "buenos sucessos."
Pursue "successes." Some are content with the "intent." Success is the "qualification" of things. The common vote does not look at the means, but at the "ends."

## 243. No ser "todo del día."
Do not be "all of the day." There must be some "reserve" of oneself. Not everything is for everyone. Let the wise man have his "sanctuary" of interiority.

## 244. Saber "obligar."
Know how to "obligate." By "giving" beforehand. It is the greatest trick of the "intent": to buy with "benefits" what others buy with "money." Favor is the "jewelry" of the mind.

## 245. Razonar "singularmente" a vezes.
Reasoning "singularly" sometimes. To move against the common vote. It argues a great capacity... for the "many" are ordinarily the "qualification" of folly.

## 246. Nunca dar "satisfaciones" a quien no las pide.
Never give "satisfactions" to those who do not ask for them. For it is to solicit "slights." To give "excuses" is to confess the "fault."

# Oráculo Manual: Aphorisms 251-260 (Scholarly)

## 251. Hanse de usar los "medios" "humanos" como si no huviere "divinos," y los "divinos" como si no huviere "humanos."
One must use the "means" of "humanity" as if there were no "divine," and the "divine" as if there were no "human." It is a great rule of acting. Let prudence be the "pilot" of the mind.

## 252. No "todo" para "todos," ni "todo" para "nada."
Not "all" for "everyone," nor "all" for "nothing." One must have a "reserve" of oneself. Not everything is for everyone... Let the wise man have his "sanctuary" of interiority.

## 253. No "individuar" mucho en las cosas.
Do not "individuate" much in things. For although it is an advantage to note everything carelessly, it is not so to want to verify everything on purpose. One must proceed ordinarily with a noble generality.

## 254. Nunca "desdeñar" el "menor" mal.
Never "slight" the "lesser" evil. For many others, and greater ones, will always come after it in ambush. The best trick of the game is knowing how to discard.

## 255. Saber "hazer bien" a algunos.
Know how to "do good" to some. By "portions" and by "times." One must not throw the whole remainder at the first cast. It is a great trick to know how to temper oneself.

## 256. Estar siempre "prevenido."
Always be "warned." Against the "audacity" of the foolish. One must have a "shield" for slights. The prudent man is known, who stops at the substantiality of the business.

## 257. Nunca llegar a la "ruptura."
Never reach the "rupture." For it is a species of discredit. One must know how to "withdraw" in time. A beautiful retreat matters as much as a gallant attack.

## 258. Buscar quien le "ayude" a "llevar" los "infortunios."
Look for someone who may "help" to "bear" the "unfortunate." For not everyone can bear the weight of universal hatred. One must have a "shield" for slights.

## 259. Prevenir las "ofensas."
Prevent the "offenses." And make them "benefits." It is the greatest trick of the "intent." One must celebrate the favors of some to solicit those of others.

## 260. Nunca serás de "todos," si eres de "ti."
You will never be "everyone's," if you are "yourself." It is the greatest lordship to be master of oneself. One must not be a "slave" to another's humor.

# Oráculo Manual: Aphorisms 261-270 (Scholarly)

## 261. No ser "porfioso" en el "yerro."
Do not be "persistent" in the "error." For it is a species of discredit. One must know how to "yield" to the truth. Prudence consists in knowing how to change the "vote" when reason requires it.

## 262. Saber "olvidar."
Know how to "forget." It is a great art of living. Most things that happen are not worth the care of the memory... and many things that were "something," by being forgotten became "nothing."

## 263. Nunca pagarse de la "fama" ajena.
Never be "paid" by the "fame" of another. For it is a species of malevolence. One must celebrate the eminences of others so that they celebrate yours.

## 264. No ser "infeliz" en los "desdoros."
Do not be "unfortunate" in the "slights." For even a slight can be a crime of folly. One must not be the "target" of everyone's malevolence.

## 265. Saber "obligar" "dando" de antemano.
Know how to "obligate" by "giving" beforehand. It is the greatest trick of the "intent": to buy with "benefits" what others buy with "money."

## 266. Nunca hablar con la "passión" ajena.
Never speak with the "passion" of another. For it is to be a slave to another's humor. One must not be the "echo" of another's malevolence.

## 267. Conocer su "buen Astro."
Know your "Lucky Star." For every man has his own. One must know how to utilize the "turn" of fortune.

## 268. Nunca "embarazarse" con los muchos.
Never be "embarrassed" by the many. For the wise man is a "world" to himself. One must not be a "victim" of the "vulgar" sentiment.

## 269. Saber "valerse" de los "desdoros" ajenos.
Know how to "utilize" the "slights" of others. For it is a singular art. One must not be a "martyr" to another's malevolence... but a "master" of it.

## 270. Dexar algo que "dessear."
Leave something to "Desire." So as not to be "miserable" in happiness. The "body" itself needs the "hope" of more.

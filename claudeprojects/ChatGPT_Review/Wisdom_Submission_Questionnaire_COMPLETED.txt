INTRODUCTORY MATERIAL
• Translator’s Note: The Dharma and the Blues – Explains the “cultural translation” methodology.
• Scholarly Introduction: Contextualizes the text within Mahayana history and Kumārajīva’s legacy.
• Reader’s Introduction: A guide to reading the sutra as a spiritual practice.

THE 28 CHAPTERS
1. The Opening (Introduction): The great assembly gathers; the Buddha enters samadhi, shaking the cosmos.
2. The Loving Tricks (Expedient Means): The Buddha reveals the “One Vehicle”—all teachings are skillful means to lead beings to Buddhahood.
3. The Parable (A Parable): The famous “Burning House” parable; the father saves his children with “toy carts” (skillful means).
4. Faith and Understanding: The “Prodigal Son” parable, told from the son's perspective.
5. The Parable of the Medicinal Herbs: The Dharma rain falls equally on all, but plants grow according to their capacity.
6. The Naming (Bestowal of Prophecies): The Buddha predicts future Buddhahood for his major disciples.
7. The Phantom City Blues: The parable of a guide creating a “phantom city” to let weary travelers rest before the final journey.
8. When the Five Hundred Finally Heard Their Names Called: Prophecies extending to lesser-known disciples; the “Gem in the Jacket” parable.
9. When the Faithful Get Their Due: Prophecies for Ananda and Rahula.
10. Honor the Word-Carriers: The sanctity of the text itself; the “Digging for Water” parable.
11. When the Jeweled Tower Rose Up: A massive stupa rises from the earth containing a past Buddha who verifies the teaching.
12. When Your Enemy Was Your Teacher (Devadatta): The radical revelation that even the Buddha's enemy will become a Buddha, and the 8-year-old Dragon Princess achieves enlightenment instantly.
13. When the Road Gets Rough: The assembly vows to spread the sutra in evil ages despite persecution.
14. How to Keep Your Soul Clean: The “Peaceful Practices”—how a Bodhisattva should live and teach.
15. When the Underground Army Rose Up: Countless Bodhisattvas emerge from the earth, pledging to keep the Dharma.
16. How Long the Tathāgata’s Been Alive: The revelation of the Buddha's eternal life span; the “Physician and his Children” parable.
17. Counting Up the Grace: The immense merit of hearing and believing this chapter.
18. The Joy That Multiplies: The benefit of simply rejoicing in the sutra.
19. When Your Senses Wake Up: The transformation of the six senses through practice.
20. The Bodhisattva Who Never Looked Down: The story of Sadaparibhuta, who bowed to everyone saying, “I dare not despise you, for you will become a Buddha.”
21. When the Lord Showed His Hand: The Buddha displays supernatural powers to entrust the sutra to the assembly.
22. The Passing On (Entrustment): The final transfer of the Dharma responsibility.
23. Medicine King Bodhisattva: The story of extreme devotion and the burning of the body as an offering.
24. Wonderful Sound Bodhisattva: A visitor from another world demonstrates the vastness of the cosmos.
25. The One Who Hears the World Crying (Avalokiteśvara): The “Universal Gate” chapter; the manifold saving forms of Guanyin/Avalokiteśvara.
26. The Protection Songs (Dhāraṇī): Magic spells and guardians protecting the sutra's holders.
27. The Beautiful King and His Good Friends: The story of King Subhavyuha transformed by his two sons.
28. Universal Worthy Comes to Encourage You: Bodhisattva Samantabhadra vows to protect those who keep the sutra in the final age.

================================================================
PRONUNCIATION NOTES FOR VOICE ACTORS
================================================================

GENERAL RULES:
1. All 'a' sounds are typically pronounced "ah" (not "ay")
2. 's' from ś should have slight 'sh' quality
3. Retroflex consonants (from ṭ, ḍ, ṇ) are pronounced with tongue curled back
4. Final 'a' is often reduced to 'uh' sound

STRESS PATTERNS:
- Most Sanskrit names: stress on SECOND syllable
  Examples: sah-REE-pu-tra, mahn-JOO-shree
- Exceptions noted in pronunciation guides above

COMMON COMBINATIONS:
- "dh" = aspirated 'd' (like "d" + breath)
- "bh" = aspirated 'b' (like "b" + breath)
- "kh" = aspirated 'k' (like "k" + breath)
- "ya" = often sounds like "yuh"

CONVERSION METHODOLOGY
================================================================

The conversion was performed using systematic pattern matching
to ensure 100% consistency across all 15,663 lines.

Priority order:
1. Specific character names (to avoid partial replacements)
2. Specific technical terms
3. General vowel diacritics
4. General consonant diacritics

This ensures terms like "Śāriputra" convert as a whole unit
before individual character replacements occur.

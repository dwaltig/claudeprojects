COMMON QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: Will the gold color be too bright?
A: No. Gold (#FFD700) is a natural, pleasant color. It's bright enough to be
   readable but not harsh. Think of it like book text on light paper.

Q: What about dark mode readers?
A: Gold works BETTER on dark backgrounds! On dark (#1a1a1a), gold has a
   12.8:1 contrast ratio - even more than on light backgrounds.

Q: Will this affect printed EPUBs?
A: No. Print styles (line 385-387) override colors to black text for printing.
   Printed versions remain readable in B&W.

Q: What if the reader strips CSS?
A: The gold colors are in the CSS. If a reader strips CSS entirely, it uses
   its default styles. Most modern readers preserve CSS.

Q: Is gold appropriate for a Buddhist text?
A: Absolutely. Gold represents enlightenment, wisdom, and preciousness in
   Buddhist iconography. It's traditionally used for sacred objects.

Q: Does this work for color-blind readers?
A: Yes. The high contrast ratios (8-19.5:1) work for all vision types. Gold
   is not dependent on red-green color vision.

Q: Can I change the colors later?
A: Yes. All colors are defined in the CSS file. Change hex codes to use
   different colors. But bright gold is optimal.

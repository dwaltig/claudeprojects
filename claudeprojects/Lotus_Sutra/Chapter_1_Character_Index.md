## 1. PRIMARY DHARMA TEACHERS

### Buddha Śākyamuni (The Historical Buddha)
- **Sanskrit with diacriticals**: Śākyamuni Tathāgata
- **Role**: Central figure, teaches the Sutra of Immeasurable Meanings; enters samādhi and emits supernatural light
- **Scholarly references**:
  - Line 3 (opening): "the Buddha was dwelling at Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa"
  - Line 9: "the World-Honored One, surrounded by the fourfold assembly, having received offerings with reverence"
  - Line 9: "expounded for the Bodhisattvas a Mahāyāna sutra entitled the Sutra of Immeasurable Meanings"
  - Line 9: "the Buddha sat in full lotus posture and entered the samādhi"
  - Line 13: "the Buddha emitted a ray of light from the tuft of white hair between his eyebrows"
- **Blues equivalent**: Central figure (present in Blues version)
- **Significance**: Initiates the supreme teaching through supernatural signs; demonstrates the Bodhisattva path through exemplary conduct; establishes pattern for universal Buddha-nature teachings

---

### Mañjuśrī (Bodhisattva of Wisdom)
- **Sanskrit with diacriticals**: Mañjuśrī, Mañjuśrī-Kumārabhūta (Prince of the Dharma King)
- **Role**: Questioner by Maitreya; wise responder who recounts the precedent story of Buddha Candrasūryapradīpa; recognized as possessing knowledge of past teachings
- **Scholarly references**:
  - Line 5: "the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī" (listed among 18 named Bodhisattvas)
  - Line 15: "This Mañjuśrī, prince of the Dharma King, has previously drawn near and made offerings to immeasurable Buddhas"
  - Line 19: "addressed Mañjuśrī, saying"
  - Line 21: "Thereupon the Bodhisattva Maitreya...spoke in verse, inquiring"
  - Line 332-344: Mañjuśrī's extensive response explaining the parallel with Buddha Candrasūryapradīpa
  - Line 343: "Maitreya, you should know that the Bodhisattva Varaprabha at that time—could he have been anyone else? He was none other than myself."
  - Line 346: "At that time Mañjuśrī, wishing to restate this meaning before the great assembly, spoke in verse"
- **Blues equivalent**: Yes, named in Blues version
- **Significance**: Represents supreme wisdom and understanding of past teachings; serves as bridge between present manifestation and historical precedent; identified with Bodhisattva Varaprabha from past Buddha's era; embodies the continuity of dharma teaching

---

### Maitreya (Future Buddha)
- **Sanskrit with diacriticals**: Maitreya Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva
- **Role**: Questioner to Mañjuśrī; expresses assembly's wonder and confusion at supernatural signs; recipient of Mañjuśrī's teaching about past Buddha Candrasūryapradīpa
- **Scholarly references**:
  - Line 5: "the Bodhisattva Maitreya" (listed among 18 named Bodhisattvas)
  - Line 15: "At that time the Bodhisattva Maitreya had this thought"
  - Line 19: "At that time the Bodhisattva Maitreya, wishing to resolve his own doubt and observing the minds of the fourfold assembly...addressed Mañjuśrī"
  - Line 21: "Thereupon the Bodhisattva Maitreya...spoke in verse, inquiring"
  - Line 332: Receives explanation from Mañjuśrī
  - Line 343: Identified as reincarnation of Yaśaskāma from Buddha Candrasūryapradīpa's era
- **Blues equivalent**: Yes, named in Blues version
- **Significance**: Representative of assembly's questioning, represents future Buddha-nature; serves as advocate for all beings confused by dharma's depth; becomes focal point of teaching transfer

## 5. PAST BUDDHA REFERENCES

### Buddha Candrasūryapradīpa (Moon-Sun-Light Buddha)
- **Sanskrit with diacriticals**: Candrasūryapradīpa Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha
- **Historical era**: "Immeasurable, limitless, inconceivable, asaṃkhya kalpas ago" (Line 334)
- **Role**: Establishes historical precedent for current Buddha's teaching method
- **Scholarly references**:
  - Line 334: "In the past, immeasurable, limitless, inconceivable, asaṃkhya kalpas ago, there was a Buddha named Candrasūryapradīpa"
  - Line 334: Taught the Dharma "good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end"
  - Line 338: "At that time, the Buddha Candrasūryapradīpa expounded the Mahāyāna sutra entitled the Sutra of Immeasurable Meanings"
  - Line 338: "he sat in full lotus posture and entered the samādhi of the abode of immeasurable meanings"
  - Line 340: "he expounded the Mahāyāna sutra entitled the Lotus Sutra of the Wonderful Dharma"
  - Line 341: "After the Buddha Candrasūryapradīpa had expounded this sutra for sixty lesser kalpas, he then made this proclamation"
  - Line 342: "At midnight he entered parinirvāṇa without residue"
- **Story elements**:
  - Entered samādhi of immeasurable meanings
  - Emitted light illuminating eighteen thousand Buddha-lands
  - Caused mandārava flowers to rain down
  - Earth trembled in six ways
  - Emerged from samādhi and taught the Lotus Sutra for sixty lesser kalpas
  - Made final proclamation and entered parinirvāṇa
- **Connection to current teachings**: Establishes exact parallel pattern for current Buddha's manifestation; precedent for universal Buddha-nature teaching; demonstrates that profound teachings transcend temporal boundaries
- **Significance**: Key precedent establishing that the current Buddha's signs and intention mirror this past Buddha's actions; validates the current teaching as part of eternal cycle

### The Succession of Twenty Thousand Buddhas Named Candrasūryapradīpa
- **Number**: Twenty thousand successive Buddhas
- **Shared attributes**: All shared the same name "Candrasūryapradīpa" and the surname "Bharadvāja"
- **Historical period**: Across vast kalpic time spans
- **Significance in Chapter 1**: Demonstrates the eternal and cyclic nature of Buddhist teaching; each Buddha repeats the same pattern of teaching
- **Scholarly reference**: Line 336

- **Sanskrit with diacriticals**: Yaśaskāma (Fame-Seeker; Seeker of Gain)

## SPECIAL NOTES AND CROSS-REFERENCES

### Reincarnation Identifications Revealed in Chapter 1

1. **Mañjuśrī = Varaprabha**
   - Line 343: Mañjuśrī reveals himself to have been the Bodhisattva Varaprabha in the time of Buddha Candrasūryapradīpa
   - Significance: Demonstrates the continuity of wisdom across kalpas

2. **Maitreya = Yaśaskāma**
   - Line 343: Mañjuśrī reveals Maitreya to be the reincarnation of Yaśaskāma, the fame-seeking disciple from Buddha Candrasūryapradīpa's era
   - Line 542: "Afterward he shall become a Buddha Whose name shall be Maitreya"
   - Significance: Shows that even limited practitioners with karmic obstacles eventually achieve Buddhahood

### Names with Multiple Meanings

- **Manojña** (appears 3 times): Pleasing/Agreeable - used for Gandharva King, Garuḍa King, and as Celestial Son of Śakra
- **Manojñasvara** (appears 2 times): Pleasing Sound - used for Gandharva King and as Celestial Son of Śakra
- **Madhura/Madhurasvara**: Sweet/Pleasant - Gandharva Kings
- **Ratnacandra**: Moon of Jewels - Bodhisattva

### Character Types and Their Symbolic Significance

- **Arhats**: Represent the perfection of the Hinayana path; those who have exhausted all afflictions
- **Bodhisattvas**: Represent the Mahāyāna path; those dedicated to enlightening all beings
- **Dragon Kings**: Represent aquatic/cosmic power; traditional protectors of the Dharma
- **Heavenly Kings and Rulers**: Represent divine authority recognizing the Buddha's supreme teaching
- **Brahmā and Īśvara**: Represent the highest divine realms acknowledging the Buddha's superiority
- **Past Buddhas**: Establish historical and mythological precedent for current teachings
- **Princes and Royalty**: Represent worldly power renounced for spiritual attainment
- **Bhikṣuṇīs**: Represent the establishment of the monastic order for women

# Trilingual Comparison: Lotus Sutra Chapter 1 (Opening)

## Sanskrit-Chinese-English Parallel Text

This document provides a side-by-side comparison of the opening of the Lotus Sutra (*Saddharmapuṇḍarīkasūtra*) in three versions:

1. **Sanskrit**: P.L. Vaidya edition (1960), via GRETIL
2. **Chinese**: Kumārajīva translation (T0262, 406 CE)
3. **English**: Present scholarly translation

---

## Opening Formula

### Sanskrit (Vaidya)
> **evaṃ mayā śrutam | ekasmin samaye bhagavān rājagṛhe viharati sma gṛdhrakūṭe parvate mahatā bhikṣusaṃghena sārdhaṃ dvādaśabhirbhikṣuśataiḥ sarvairarhadbhiḥ kṣīṇāsravairniḥkleśairvaśībhūtaiḥ suvimuktacittaiḥ suvimuktaprajñairājāneyairmahānāgaiḥ kṛtakṛtyaiḥ kṛtakaraṇīyairapahṛtabhārairanuprāptasvakārthaiḥ**

### Chinese (T0262)
> **如是我聞。一時佛住王舍城耆闍崛山中，與大比丘眾萬二千人俱，皆是阿羅漢，諸漏已盡，無復煩惱，逮得己利，盡諸有結，心得自在。**

### English (Present Translation)
> **Thus have I heard. At one time, the Buddha was dwelling at Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa near the city of Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of twelve thousand bhikṣus. All of these practitioners had attained the state of Arhat—having exhausted all outflows of mental affliction, having eliminated all defilements, having realized their own ultimate benefit, having severed all bonds of conditioned existence, and having attained complete mastery and freedom of mind.**

## The Light Ray from the Ūrṇā

### Sanskrit (Vaidya)
> **atha khalu tasyāṃ velāyāṃ bhagavato bhrūvivarāntarādūrṇākośādekā raśmirniścaritā | sā pūrvasyāṃ diśi aṣṭādaśabuddhakṣetrasahasrāṇi prasṛtā |**

### Chinese (T0262)
> **爾時世尊，從眉間白毫相光，照東方萬八千世界，靡不周遍。**

### English (Present Translation)
> **At that time, from between the World-Honored One's eyebrows—from the white ūrṇā tuft—a single ray of light shone forth. This ray illuminated eighteen thousand Buddha-lands in the eastern direction, reaching everywhere without exception.**

---

## Verse 1.1 (Maitreya's Question)

### Sanskrit (Vaidya)
> **kiṃ kāraṇaṃ mañjuśirī iyaṃ hi raśmiḥ pramuktā naranāyakena /**
> **prabhāsayantī bhramukāntarātu ūrṇāya kośādiyamekaraśmiḥ //**

### Chinese (T0262)
> **文殊師利，導師何故，眉間白毫，大光普照？**

### English (Present Translation)
> **"Mañjuśrī, for what reason has this ray been released by the Guide of Humanity?**
> **What is this single light streaming from between his brows, from the ūrṇā tuft?"**

## Editorial Notes

2. **Chinese** often compresses Sanskrit prolixity; Kumārajīva's style is concise
3. **English** follows Chinese structure but restores Sanskrit terminology where appropriate
4. Verse numbering follows GRETIL convention: `saddhp_1.1` = Chapter 1, Verse 1

```
The complete Saddharma-puṇḍarīka-sūtra (Lotus Sutra) translated
from the Classical Chinese version of Kumārajīva (406 CE) into
contemporary American English. This scholarly edition includes:

• 28 complete chapters covering the Buddha's teachings on universal
  Buddha-nature and the doctrine of skillful means (upāya)
• 1,554 integrated scholarly footnotes providing:
  - Philosophical apparatus connecting Buddhist concepts to
    contemporary phenomenology, systems theory, and existentialism
  - Scientific resonances with quantum mechanics and cognitive science
  - Historical and textual context
  - Translation methodology notes
  - Cross-references to related Buddhist teachings
• Comprehensive glossary of Buddhist terminology with Sanskrit
  diacritical marks preserved
• Analytical index of names, concepts, and parables
• Complete bibliography of sources cited in apparatus

Approximately 232,600 words. Suitable for scholarly study, academic
citation, contemplative reading, and general educated readers seeking
rigorous access to one of the world's most influential spiritual texts.

The translation maintains formal Buddhist scriptural register while
rendering the text in accessible contemporary American English. It
serves scholars, practitioners, philosophers, and general readers
interested in Buddhism, translation theory, comparative religion, and
spiritual wisdom literature.

This edition is part of a larger project that also includes:
- A blues/gospel vernacular interpretation of the teachings
- Reference to the original Classical Chinese source material
```

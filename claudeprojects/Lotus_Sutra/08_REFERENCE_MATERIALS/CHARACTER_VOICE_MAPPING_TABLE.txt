PRODUCTION NOTES FOR VOICE ACTORS
================================================================================

BUDDHA'S THREE VOICES:
The Buddha requires THREE distinct vocal approaches depending on context:
  1. [Iapetus] - Direct teaching prose: calm, clear, authoritative
  2. [Rasalgethi] - Verses: more musical, rhythmic, elevated
  3. [Orus] - Storytelling: warm, narrative, preacher spinning a tale

Consider using:
  • Same actor with different registers, OR
  • Three actors with timbral similarity but different performance styles

NARRATOR [Charon]:
This is the spine of the entire work (208 occurrences). Needs:
  • Stamina for long-form narration
  • Blues/gospel storytelling authenticity
  • Ability to shift between scene-setting and intimate address
  • Warm authority without being preachy
  • Think: August Wilson narrator, gospel DJ, blues documentarian

FEMALE VOICES:
Critical that these are STRONG, AUTHORITATIVE voices, not soft or submissive:
  • [Leda] needs elder-woman dignity and spiritual power
  • [Sulafat_F] needs compassionate strength, protective maternal energy
  • [Schedar] (Dragon Princess) needs youthful confidence and fearlessness

CHAPTER-BY-CHAPTER VOICE DISTRIBUTION:
  • Chapters 1-3: Heavy Buddha/Śāriputra dialogue
  • Chapters 4-9: Parables, multiple disciples
  • Chapter 12: Dragon Princess appears
  • Chapter 13: Female monastics (Mahāprajāpatī, Yaśodharā)
  • Chapter 20: Never Looked Down story (Buddha as storyteller)
  • Chapter 25: Avalokiteśvara chapter (heavy [Sulafat_F])
  • Chapter 28: Universal Worthy finale

BLUES/GOSPEL INTERPRETATION NOTES
================================================================================

TONAL REGISTER:
This version uses blues/gospel vernacular throughout. Voice actors should be
comfortable with:
  • African American Vernacular English (AAVE) patterns
  • Call-and-response phrasing ("you hear me?", "listen now")
  • Gospel preaching cadences
  • Blues storytelling rhythms
  • Vernacular contractions ("ain't", "gonna", "done")

RESPECT AND AUTHENTICITY:
This is sacred text rendered through African American spiritual tradition.
Actors should approach with:
  • Deep respect for both Buddhist and blues/gospel traditions
  • Understanding that this is NOT parody or novelty
  • Commitment to the spiritual weight of the material
  • Authentic connection to the vernacular (not affected or exaggerated)

SUGGESTED LISTENING for tonal reference:
  • Gospel sermons (Rev. C.L. Franklin, Rev. James Cleveland)
  • Blues narratives (B.B. King spoken intros, Muddy Waters interviews)
  • August Wilson play recordings
  • Mahalia Jackson spoken testimonies
  • Langston Hughes readings

OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This guide documents all Chinese-to-Pinyin conversions made in creating the
production manuscript (narrated_manuscript_production_v1.txt) from the final
annotated manuscript.

WHY PINYIN CONVERSION IS CRITICAL FOR AI VOICES:

AI text-to-speech systems (including Google Gemini voices) CANNOT properly
pronounce Chinese characters. When encountering "妙法蓮華經", the AI will
attempt to read each character as if it were a word, resulting in:

   INCORRECT: "character-name character-name character-name..."
   CORRECT:   "Miào Fǎ Liánhuá Jīng" (phonetically approximates Chinese)

Pinyin romanization allows AI voices to produce phonetically accurate
approximations of Chinese pronunciation using English phonetic rules.

================================================================================
PINYIN TONE MARK GUIDE
================================================================================

Pinyin uses four tones plus neutral tone, marked with diacritics:

TONE 1 (High level):      ā ē ī ō ū     Example: Fā (發) "to emit"
TONE 2 (Rising):          á é í ó ú     Example: Fá (乏) "lack"
TONE 3 (Falling-rising):  ǎ ě ǐ ǒ ǔ     Example: Fǎ (法) "Dharma/law"
TONE 4 (Falling):         à è ì ò ù     Example: Fà (髮) "hair"
NEUTRAL (no mark):        a e i o u     Example: ma (嗎) question particle

GEMINI VOICE INTERPRETATION:
- Tone marks provide prosodic guidance
- AI voices will interpret these as stress/pitch variations
- Result approximates (but does not perfectly replicate) Chinese tones
- This is INTENTIONAL and acceptable for multilingual narration

PRONUNCIATION GUIDE FOR VOICE ACTORS
================================================================================

GEMINI AI VOICE INTERPRETATION:

When Gemini API voices encounter pinyin romanization with tone marks, they will:

1. Read it phonetically as if it were English text with diacritics
2. Interpret tone marks as prosodic/stress cues (not perfect tone replication)
3. Produce approximations that sound closer to Chinese than character names would

EXAMPLE COMPARISON:

Chinese characters (WRONG for AI):
   Input: "妙法蓮華經"
   AI reads: "[character name] [character name] [character name]..."
   Result: Unintelligible

Pinyin romanization (CORRECT for AI):
   Input: "Miào Fǎ Liánhuá Jīng"
   AI reads: "mee-OW fah lee-AHN-hwah jing"
   Result: Phonetically approximates Chinese pronunciation

TONE MARK PRONUNCIATION HINTS:

ā, ē, ī, ō, ū (Tone 1): Read with HIGH, LEVEL pitch
á, é, í, ó, ú (Tone 2): Read with RISING intonation (like a question)
ǎ, ě, ǐ, ǒ, ǔ (Tone 3): Read with DIP then RISE (falling-rising)
à, è, ì, ò, ù (Tone 4): Read with sharp FALLING pitch (emphatic)

COMMON SYLLABLES:

Miào   = "mee-OW" (like cat sound, 4th tone - falling)
Fǎ     = "FAH" (like "fa" in music, 3rd tone - dip and rise)
Lián   = "lee-AHN" (2nd tone - rising)
huá    = "HWAH" (2nd tone - rising)
Jīng   = "JING" (1st tone - high and level)

Púsà   = "POO-sah" (Bodhisattva)
Guānshìyīn = "GWAHN-shir-YEEN" (Avalokiteśvara)
Námó   = "NAH-mwoh" (Homage/refuge, from Sanskrit "namo")

================================================================================
FORMATTING STRATEGY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The file now uses THREE LEVELS of intelligent breaks:

LEVEL 1: CHAPTER BREAKS
- Double line breaks before chapter headers (--- CHAPTER X ---)
- Creates 1-2 second pause in narration
- Clearly marks new chapter transitions

LEVEL 2: SUBSECTION BREAKS
- Double line breaks before ALLCAPS subsection headers (THE GATHERING, etc.)
- These are HIGHLY VISIBLE in the file
- Creates 1-2 second pause for section transitions
- Easy to spot visually (all caps = easy to read)

LEVEL 3: SEMANTIC VERSE BREAKS (NEW)
- Single line breaks added ONLY within long verse passages
- Triggered at 500+ words + sentence ending (period)
- Creates ~0.5-1 second narration pause
- ONLY breaks at end-of-sentence, never in middle of thought
- Preserves poetic flow and meaning

================================================================================
WHY THIS APPROACH IS SUPERIOR
================================================================================

PREVIOUS APPROACH (ARBITRARY 50-LINE BREAKS):
❌ Broke verses every 50 lines regardless of meaning
❌ Could break in middle of a thought
❌ Arbitrary and mechanical
❌ Not based on actual sentence structure

NEW APPROACH (SEMANTIC BREAKS):
✅ Breaks ONLY at sentence endings (periods)
✅ Requires 500+ words of verse before breaking
✅ Based on actual meaning units, not line counts
✅ Respects the natural rhythm of the text
✅ Professional audiobook standard

================================================================================
HOW IT WORKS
================================================================================

DETECTION:
1. Script identifies verse start ("spoke in verse", etc.)
2. Tracks word count from that point forward
3. Monitors for sentence endings (lines ending with periods)
4. When BOTH conditions met (500+ words AND period), adds break

EXAMPLE FROM CHAPTER 1:

Mañjuśrī, tell me why—
Why the Teacher's shining bright
From that white hair between his eyes,
Pouring out that great big light?
[continues for many lines...]

All the way to highest height,
In all those worlds we're seeing now
Beings walking in six paths of life.

[BREAK ADDED HERE - ~500 words accumulated + period at end of sentence]

We see where birth and death will lead them,
See the good and bad they've sown,
See the pretty and the ugly fruit they're getting—
Everything's being shown.

[continues until next 500-word mark or section end]

## THEOLOGICAL PRINCIPLE MAINTAINED

This revision preserves the Sutra's revolutionary power by **maintaining male narrative where it grounds expectation, so that women's appearance becomes paradigm-shattering**.

The strategy: Keep male characters in historical narrative, use gender-inclusive language for universal teachings and parables. This allows the text's own gender revolution to speak: when women appear and shatter categories, it matters BECAUSE the narrative has been male-dominated.

## THREE-PHASE CHANGES IMPLEMENTED

### PHASE 1: HIGH PRIORITY (~20-25 instances)
**Universal Addresses and Generic References**

1. **Universal Assembly Addresses:**
   - "Listen here, brothers" → "Listen here, brothers and sisters" (line ~4844)
   - "Now brothers, listen up" → "Now brothers and sisters, listen up" (line ~4848)

2. **Divine/Heavenly Beings:**
   - "twenty thousand divine sons" → "twenty thousand divine children"
   - "twelve thousand divine sons" → "twelve thousand divine children"
   - "ten thousand sons of heaven" → "ten thousand children of heaven"
   - "sons of heaven attained" → "children of heaven attained"
   - **Rationale:** These aren't gendered beings; they're celestial. "Children" is theologically accurate AND inclusive.

3. **Generic Human References:**
   - "Good brother, listen now" → "Good friend, listen now" (lines 13076, 13207)
   - **Rationale:** These are universal addresses, not specific characters.

### PHASE 2: MEDIUM PRIORITY (~35+ instances + 2 framing paragraphs)

1. **Burning House Parable (Chapter 3) - Complete Revision:**
   All instances of "sons" → "children" in the parable that represents ALL beings:
   - "he got sons—maybe ten, maybe twenty" → "he got children"
   - "my sons—they in there" → "my children—they in there"
   - "told all his sons" → "told all his children"
   - "My sons are young" → "My children are young"
   - "them sons was so caught up" → "them children was so caught up"
   - "when them sons heard" → "when them children heard"
   - "his sons get out safe" → "his children get out safe"
   - "gave all his sons the great cart" → "gave all his children the great cart"
   - Plus ALL verse sections updated (lines 2552-2797)
   - **Rationale:** This parable explicitly represents ALL beings seeking liberation, not just males.

2. **Generic "Men" → "Folks" (Universal Contexts):**
   - Line 293: "These were men who'd walked the long hard road" → "These were folks who'd walked the long hard road"
   - **Rationale:** Blues naturally says "folks"—it's vernacular and includes everyone.

3. **TWO NEW FRAMING PARAGRAPHS ADDED:**

   **A. Reader's Introduction Addition (after line 273):**
   New paragraph explaining women's paradigm-shifting role:
   - Mahāprajāpatī demanding recognition
   - Eight thousand nuns receiving prophecies
   - Dragon girl's instant enlightenment
   - Blues tradition parallels (Sister Rosetta Tharpe, Mahalia Jackson)
   - Theological weight: "The dragon girl's enlightenment ain't incidental. It's the proof."

   **B. Translator's Note Addition (after line 114):**
   New paragraph explaining gender strategy:
   - Why male characters remain male (narrative reality)
   - Why universal teachings use inclusive language
   - The theological payoff: "This is a translation that lets the Sutra's own revolution speak: first you think this is a man's world, then you discover it's not, and that discovery IS the teaching."

**What MUST NEVER CHANGE (and was correctly preserved):**

1. **Historical Male Characters:**

4. **Women's Prominence Verified:**

   - Yaśodharā appears 4 times (prominently featured)
   - "dragon girl" appears 7+ times (Chapter 12 climax preserved)

5. **Blues/Gospel Voice:**

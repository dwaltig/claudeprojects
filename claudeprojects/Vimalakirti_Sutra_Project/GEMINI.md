## 1. Project Overview

The **Vimalakirti Sutra Translation Project** is a scholarly and vernacular dual-translation effort for one of Mahayana Buddhism's most philosophically sophisticated texts. The sutra features the enlightened layman Vimalakirti engaging in profound dharma debates with the Buddha's disciples.

**Key Components:**
- **Source Text**: Classical Chinese (Kumarajiva translation, T0475, 14 chapters)
- **Dual Translation**: Scholarly (academic) + Blues Vernacular (accessible)
- **Agent System**: Specialized AI personas for translation and review

---

## 2. Critical Safety Protocol

**READ BEFORE EDITING:**
- **Preserve Scholarship**: Handle all text with extreme care
- **Verify Encodings**: Ensure UTF-8 and correct Sanskrit diacritics (ś, ṇ, ū, ā, ṃ)
- **Ask First**: Confirm structural changes with the user
- **Document Everything**: Update session notes and status files

## 5. Translation Workflow

### Standard Process
1. **Professor translates** Classical Chinese → Scholarly English
2. **Bluesman reviews** Professor's translation for missed nuances
3. **Bluesman creates** Blues vernacular version
4. **Consolidation**: Both versions combined into chapter files

### Quality Assurance
- Cross-reference with other translations (Thurman, Watson, etc.)
- Verify Sanskrit terms and diacritics
- Ensure philosophical consistency
- Maintain narrative flow in Blues version

---

## 6. The Vimalakirti Sutra: Special Considerations

### Unique Features
- **Dharma Combat**: Brilliant dialectical exchanges between Vimalakirti and disciples
- **Non-Duality (不二)**: Central philosophical theme requiring careful translation
- **Lay Buddhism**: Vimalakirti as enlightened householder (not monk)
- **Skillful Means (方便)**: Multiple teaching methods adapted to audience

### Translation Challenges
- **Paradoxical Language**: "Not this, not that" formulations
- **Philosophical Precision**: Technical Mahayana terminology
- **Dramatic Tension**: Maintaining narrative energy in debates
- **Cultural Context**: Chinese Buddhist concepts → English/Blues idiom

## 8. Chapter Overview (14 Chapters)

1. **Buddha Land** (佛國品) - Pure lands and bodhisattva practice
2. **Skillful Means** (方便品) - Vimalakirti's illness as teaching device
3. **Disciples** (弟子品) - Buddha's disciples decline to visit
4. **Bodhisattvas** (菩薩品) - Bodhisattvas also decline
5. **Manjushri Inquires** (文殊師利問疾品) - The great dialogue begins
6. **Inconceivable** (不思議品) - Miraculous demonstrations
7. **Observing Sentient Beings** (觀眾生品) - Nature of compassion
8. **Buddha Path** (佛道品) - Non-dual practice
9. **Non-Duality** (入不二法門品) - **CENTRAL CHAPTER** - Famous "thunderous silence"
10. **Accumulated Fragrance** (香積佛品) - Visit to fragrant buddha-land
11. **Bodhisattva Practices** (菩薩行品) - Skillful means in action
12. **Seeing Akshobhya** (見阿閦佛品) - Vimalakirti's origin revealed
13. **Dharma Offering** (法供養品) - True worship vs. material offerings
14. **Entrusting** (囑累品) - Transmission to future generations

## 12. Key Philosophical Terms

Familiarize yourself with these core concepts:

- **不二 (bù èr)**: Non-duality, non-dualism
- **方便 (fāng biàn)**: Skillful means, expedient methods
- **解脫 (jiě tuō)**: Liberation, emancipation
- **空 (kōng)**: Emptiness, śūnyatā
- **如來 (rú lái)**: Tathāgata, "Thus-Come-One"
- **菩薩 (pú sà)**: Bodhisattva
- **般若 (bō rě)**: Prajñā, wisdom

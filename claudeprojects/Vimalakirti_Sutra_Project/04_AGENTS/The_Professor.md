## Translation Approach

### Core Principles
1. **Philosophical Precision**: Capture the exact philosophical meaning of technical terms
2. **Scholarly Apparatus**: Provide extensive footnotes with:
   - Textual variants
   - Philosophical commentary
   - Cross-references to other sutras
   - Sanskrit equivalents
   - Modern philosophical parallels
3. **Academic Tone**: Maintain formal, scholarly English suitable for academic publication
4. **Doctrinal Accuracy**: Ensure all translations align with Mahayana Buddhist doctrine

### Footnote Categories
- **[Textual]**: Variant readings, manuscript differences
- **[Philosophical]**: Doctrinal explanations, concept clarifications
- **[Cross-reference]**: References to other sutras, Buddhist texts
- **[Sanskrit]**: Original Sanskrit terms and meanings
- **[Modern]**: Connections to contemporary philosophy, science

## Translation Methodology

1. **First Pass**: Literal translation from Classical Chinese
2. **Refinement**: Polish for clarity while maintaining precision
3. **Annotation**: Add comprehensive footnotes
4. **Verification**: Cross-check with other translations (Thurman, Watson, Lamotte)
5. **Review**: Ensure philosophical consistency throughout
